    General journal - Key points

    The General journal is a fundamental component of the accounting system used for recording miscellaneous transactions that do not fit into the other specialized subsidiary journals. It provides flexibility for recording various types of transactions that are not covered by the specific journals, such as the Sales journal, Purchase journal, or Payments and Receipts journals (linked to specific bank accounts).

    Here are some key points about the General journal:

    1. Miscellaneous Transactions: The General Journal is used to record transactions that are unique or do not have a designated journal for their specific type. It includes transactions such as corrections of errors, adjusting entries, accruals, deferrals, depreciation entries, and other non-routine transactions.
    2. Flexibility: The General Journal allows for the recording of diverse transactions, making it a versatile tool in the accounting process. It accommodates transactions that do not fit into predefined categories or require special treatment.
    3. Manual Entries: Unlike other subsidiary journals that may have automated or system-generated entries, the General Journal typically involves manual entries made by accountants or bookkeepers. These entries require careful consideration and proper documentation.
    4. Debits and Credits: The General journal follows the fundamental principles of double-entry bookkeeping, where each transaction is recorded with equal debits and credits to maintain the balance of the accounting equation.
    5. Ledger posting: After entries are recorded in the General Journal, they are posted to the respective accounts in the General ledger. This ensures that the impact of the transactions is reflected accurately in the overall accounting records.
    6. Financial reporting: The transactions recorded in the General Journal are ultimately reflected in the financial statements. They contribute to the preparation of the trial balance, income statement, balance sheet, and other financial reports.
    7. Audit trail: The General Journal provides an audit trail for miscellaneous transactions, allowing for proper documentation and tracking of entries. This helps in ensuring transparency, accountability, and accuracy in financial reporting.
    8. Internal controls: The General Journal, like other accounting journals, plays a role in maintaining internal controls within an organization. It allows for proper authorization, review, and approval processes for miscellaneous transactions, minimizing the risk of errors or fraudulent activities.

    Overall, the General journal serves as a crucial tool in accounting, providing flexibility for recording various types of transactions that do not fit into other specialized journals. It ensures that all transactions are properly documented, recorded, and reported, maintaining the integrity of the financial records and supporting accurate financial analysis and decision-making.

    When should a general journal be used?

    The general journal, on the other hand, is typically used for recording non-routine or unusual transactions that do not fit into other specialized journals. It is a versatile journal that can be used for various purposes, including corrections of errors. If there is no specific journal available for a particular type of transaction, using the general journal is a suitable option.

    However, whenever there is a specialized journal available for a specific type of transaction, it is recommended to use that journal to ensure accuracy and efficiency in record-keeping. This helps maintain consistency and allows for easier analysis and reporting of transactions within their relevant categories.

However, if there is a specific journal available for a particular type of transaction, it is best to use that journal to ensure accuracy and efficiency in record-keeping

    When should a general journal NOT be used?

    A general journal should not be used in certain situations where there are specific journals designed for recording transactions. For example:

    • Sales: For recording sales, specialised journals like the sales journal should be used. To record sales transactions in osFinancials, you can use the sales journal available in the Batch entry section of the Default ribbon. The sales journal allows you to enter and manage sales-related transactions effectively.

    In addition to the sales journal, you can process invoices and credit notes using the Documents section on the Default ribbon. Invoices and credit notes can be linked to the sales journal in Setup → Documents setup (Setup ribbon). When you process invoices and credit notes, the source documents will be automatically generated, allowing you to have physical or digital copies of these documents for record-keeping purposes. These documents can be printed and reprinted as needed, enabling you to send them to your debtors (customers/clients) as required.

    Once the invoices and credit notes are updated and posted to the ledger, the corresponding transactions will be automatically processed in the sales journal. Each item included in the invoice or credit note will be recorded in the sales journal, ensuring accurate tracking of your sales transactions.

    By utilising the sales journal and linking invoices and credit notes, you can efficiently manage and maintain a comprehensive record of your sales activities in osFinancials.

    • Purchases: For recording purchases, specialised journals like the purchase journal should be used. To record purchase transactions in osFinancials, you can use the purchase journal available in the Batch entry section of the Default ribbon. The purchase journal allows you to enter and manage purchase-related transactions effectively.

    In addition to the purchase journal, you can process purchase and supplier return documents using the Documents section on the Default ribbon. Purchases and supplier return documents can be linked to the purchase journal in Setup → Documents setup (Setup ribbon). When you process purchase and supplier return documents, the source documents will be automatically generated, allowing you to have physical or digital copies of these documents for record-keeping purposes. These documents can be printed and reprinted as needed, enabling you to send them to your creditors (suppliers/vendors) as required.

    Once the purchase and supplier return documents are updated and posted to the ledger, the corresponding transactions will be automatically processed in the purchase journal. Each item included in the purchase and supplier return documents will be recorded in the purchase journal, ensuring accurate tracking of your purchase transactions.

    By utilising the purchase journal and linking purchase and supplier return documents, you can efficiently manage and maintain a comprehensive record of your purchase activities in osFinancials.

    • Cash transactions: To record cash transactions in osFinancials, specialised journals such as the cash receipts journal and cash disbursements journal are used. In osFinancials, when setting up bank accounts and petty cash accounts in the Setup → Accounts menu of the Setup ribbon, each account will be associated with its own unique payments journal and receipts journal.

    If the Bank import plugin is not activated in osFinancials, you can manually process cash transactions in the respective payments journal and receipts journal associated with the bank account or petty cash account. To access these journals, you can go to the Batch entry option on the Default ribbon.

    If the Bank import plugin is activated, you have the option to process and import transactions directly into the payments journal and receipts journal associated with the bank account or petty cash account. This can be done through the Cash/Bank entry option on the Default ribbon.

    By using the appropriate journals for cash transactions, you can effectively record and manage your cash receipts and disbursements. The journals allow for accurate tracking of cash flows, providing a clear overview of your financial activities.

    Please note that the availability and functionality of specific features may depend on the configuration and settings of your Set of Books in osFinancials.

    With the plugin, you can download bank statement files from your online banking app or portal provided by your bank or financial institution. These files can be imported into the appropriate bank accounts (cash books) in osFinancials.

    You can download statements for multiple bank accounts and credit cards, as long as you have them linked to your online banking. Each bank account or credit card should have its corresponding Payments and Receipts journals set up in osFinancials.

    Importing the bank statements automatically populates the transactions into the Payments and Receipts columns, saving you time and reducing the risk of errors associated with manual entry.

    The plugin offers a user-friendly interface with context menus that make it easy to locate, select, and search for specific transactions, debtors, creditors, or general ledger accounts. This simplifies the navigation and management of your bank transactions.

    You can link debit transactions to credit transactions and vice versa, allowing for accurate tracking of outstanding balances and efficient management of payments and receipts. You can also link a portion of a payment or receipt to multiple outstanding transactions if needed.

    Additionally, the plugin allows you to link accounts to specific debtors, creditors, or general ledger accounts. This ensures that subsequent bank statement imports automatically allocate transactions correctly based on the predefined account linkages, improving accuracy and saving time.

    The plugin utilizes bank references to automatically allocate or link various transactions to the correct accounts in your Set of Books. It supports automatic receipts for debit orders from your debtors and automatic payments for managing supplier payments.

    In summary, the Bank import plugin in osFinancials simplifies the import of bank statements, automates transaction allocation, and efficiently manages and reconciles your bank accounts. It reduces manual data entry, improves accuracy, and streamlines your financial management processes.
    • Payroll: To record payroll transactions in osFinancials, a specialised journal such as the payroll journal can be used. If you need to add the payroll journal, you can do so in the Setup → Batch types menu on the Setup ribbon.

    By processing payroll transactions in the payroll journal, you can effectively track your salary and wage expenses and payments. This allows you to have a dedicated journal specifically for recording payroll-related transactions, providing specific configuration of the payroll journal and better organisation and analysis of your payroll data.

    In addition to the payroll journal, osFinancials also provides the option to process repeating transactions. Repeating transactions can be accessed in the Edit → Repeating transactions menu on the Default ribbon. This feature allows you to set up and manage transactions that occur on a regular basis, such as recurring payroll entries. You can edit the repeating transactions as needed and import them into the normal payroll journal, ensuring consistency and efficiency in recording your payroll transactions.

    Using a payroll journal and taking advantage of the repeating transactions feature in osFinancials can streamline your payroll processing and help maintain accurate records of your payroll expenses and payments.

    • Depreciation: To record fixed asset transactions in osFinancials, a specialized journal such as the fixed asset journal can be used. If the fixed asset journal is not already added, you can do so in the Setup → Batch types menu on the Setup ribbon.

    By processing transactions for fixed assets in the fixed asset journal, you can effectively track your fixed asset-related transactions. This includes recording acquisitions, disposals, multi-year depreciation, and any transactions related to losses or profits on the disposal of fixed assets. Having a dedicated journal specifically for fixed asset transactions allows for better organization and analysis of your fixed asset data.

    In addition to the fixed asset journal, osFinancials also provides the option to process repeating transactions. The repeating transactions feature can be accessed in the Edit → Repeating transactions menu on the Default ribbon. This feature is useful for setting up and managing transactions that occur on a regular basis, such as recurring depreciation entries for fixed assets. You can edit the repeating transactions as needed and import them into the normal fixed asset journal, ensuring consistency and efficiency in recording your fixed asset transactions.

    Using the fixed asset journal and leveraging the repeating transactions feature in osFinancials can streamline your fixed asset processing and help maintain accurate records of your fixed asset-related transactions. This allows for better tracking and management of your fixed assets throughout their life-cycle.

    The Activa plugin automates the management and processes of fixed assets and the depreciation of fixed assets in your accounts over the lifespan of the fixed assets.  Periodically you will receive a notification screen, which the depreciation shown. From this screen, the depreciation(s) can directly take place.   

    The Activa plugin in osFinancials allows you to register fixed assets that need to be written off. The write-off process occurs over the lifespan of the asset and can be spread across multiple financial years.

    By adding the Activa Fixed asset accounts in the plugin setup, you will receive reminders when new transactions are posted to the ledger or when Activa transactions require processing. This automation streamlines the management of depreciation allowances for fixed assets (tangible assets) in your Set of Books over the asset's lifespan.

    As you record the depreciation expenses for each financial year, they will be reported in the income statement and reduce your net profit. Additionally, the plugin will provide you with the net value of your fixed assets, which is calculated as the fixed assets' cost minus the accumulated depreciation, and this information will be reflected in the balance sheet.

    In summary, the Activa plugin helps automate the depreciation management process, ensures accurate reporting of depreciation expenses, and provides visibility into the net value of fixed assets over their lifespan in both the income statement and the balance sheet.

    When a fixed asset reached the end of life end process that will clear of the total write-off from the balance write-off account and the initial asset account. This will display only the remaining total on the asset account and the contra account the will be cleared of this total.

    By allowing the write-off process to occur over the lifespan of the asset and providing reminders for transaction processing, the plugin further streamlines the management of depreciation allowances for fixed assets.

    The accurate reporting of depreciation expenses in the income statement and the reflection of the net value of fixed assets (cost minus accumulated depreciation) in the balance sheet remain essential features of the plugin.

    Furthermore, the ability to clear the total write-off from the balance write-off account and the initial asset account when a fixed asset reaches the end of its life adds to the efficiency of the process.

    The Activa plugin's powerful grid, custom filtering options, and integrated pivot tables enhance the search and analysis capabilities for active fixed assets, assets requiring processing, and transaction details. The Ledger analyser allows for the printing and exporting of the trial balance, income statement, and balance sheet, while the T-Account viewer facilitates the export of transactions to spreadsheets.

    The Central search feature in osFinancials further aids in searching for transactions related to fixed assets, providing comprehensive functionality for managing and analysing fixed asset data.
