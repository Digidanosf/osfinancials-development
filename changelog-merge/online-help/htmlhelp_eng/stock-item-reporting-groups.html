  1. Stock (Inventory / Products)

    Stock groups

    In osFinancials, you have the option to set up to two (2) reporting groups for your stock items or inventory items, allowing you to categorise and report on your products more effectively. These reporting groups provide a way to organize and analyse your inventory based on specific criteria. 

    These Stock groups can be linked to each stock item (inventory item / product). 
    • You may use the context menu option on the stock grid to set or change the reporting groups for Stock group 1 and/or Stock group 2  for selected stock item(s).
    • You may use the stock grid to filter, search or group stock items.
    • You may print Stock reports and Print barcodes for all or only for stock items linked to a selected Stock group or groups.
    • You may print Sales analysis reports for Stock group 1 or Stock group 2.  

    Examples of reporting groups for stock items / inventory items / products

    Here are some examples of reporting groups that you can use for stock items:

    1. Product category: You can create reporting groups based on the category or type of products you offer. For instance, if you sell electronics, you might have reporting groups like "Computers," "Mobile Devices," "Audio Equipment," etc.
    2. Brand or Manufacturer: Another option is to create reporting groups based on the brand or manufacturer of your products. This can be useful for tracking sales and performance by brand. For example, you could have reporting groups like "Samsung," "Apple," "Sony," etc.
    3. Product line: If you offer multiple product lines, you can create reporting groups to differentiate them. For instance, if you sell clothing, you might have reporting groups like "Men's Apparel," "Women's Apparel," "Children's Apparel," etc.
    4. Location: Reporting groups based on the location of your products can be helpful for inventory management and sales analysis. This is particularly useful if you have multiple warehouses or stores. Examples of reporting groups could be "Warehouse A," "Store Branch 1," "Online Store," etc.
    5. Sales channel: If you sell your products through various channels, you can set up reporting groups to track sales performance across those channels. This could include groups like "Retail Stores," "E-commerce," "Wholesale," etc.

    These are just a few examples, and you can customise the reporting groups in osFinancials to fit your specific business needs. By assigning stock items to the appropriate reporting groups, you can generate reports that provide insights into your inventory, sales, and performance based on the defined categorisation.

    Define groups for stock items / inventory items / products

    To set reporting groups for stock items: 

    1. On the Setup ribbon, select Setup â†’ Groups. The "Groups (Account group 1)" default screen will be displayed. 
    2. Click on the Stock group 1 button.
    3. If you wish to rename the Reporting group name - Stock group 1, over-type the description with your new description. This will change the description of the button for Stock group 1.
    4. To add a new Group to the root directory or list, select the "REPORTING GROUP ROOT" on the list and click on the New button.
    5. Enter the name of your Stock group 1 and click on "REPORTING GROUP ROOT" on the list. 
    6. Repeat the process until you have entered all the Stock groups, etc. 

    Adding sub groups for stock items:

    Select a Stock group, for example; "Power tools" and click on the New button. Enter the name of your sub group.  
    1. If you need to add Reporting group 2 for Stock, click on the Stock group 2 button and repeat the process as for Stock group 1.
    2. Click on the OK button to save your changes and close (exit) this screen.

    Changing the sequence of groups for stock items:

    Select a Stock group, for example; "Power tools" and click on the Move up or Move down button. This will list the stock group according to your preferred sequence.  

    You may click on the Print button to print a list of your stock groups.  

    You may set colours to display on the Stock item list screen. To do this, click on the Back colour button to set the rows background colour and the Font colour button to set the Font colour.  

    To retain and save the selected colors, you also need to set both the "Back color" and the "Font color".
