    VAT/GST/Sales Tax - New Registered Vendor in osFinancials

    If you are already Registered as a VAT/GST/Sales Tax Vendor and you are New to osFinancials  

    If you're new to osFinancials and are a registered VAT/GST/Sales Tax vendor, this option is for you. You'll follow the steps to set up your VAT/GST/Sales Tax accounts, configure tax codes and percentages, and customize document layouts to meet your tax jurisdiction's requirements. This ensures accurate tax calculations and proper tax invoicing in your transactions.

    VAT/GST/Sales tax codes - Template (Set of Books) 

    Most template Set of Books, downloaded from the "Let osFinancials help you to create a Set of Books?" option, already includes basic VAT/GST/Sales tax accounts in the "Current liabilities" section in the Chart of Accounts. 

    Before you start to process any transactions and documents or link your Stock items / Inventory items / Products to the Output and Input tax accounts, you need to check your tax codes and percentages (If you are registered as a VAT/GST/Sales Tax vendor).

    Steps to configure the template Set of Books for VAT/GST/Sales Tax

    As a new user to osFinancials, before you start processing any transactions in journals batches (journals) or to process and transactions in documents it is advisable to review and edit the Chart of Accounts. 

    In cases where you have met the compulsory threshold for VAT/GST/Sales tax registration or have chosen to opt for voluntary registration as a VAT/GST/Sales tax vendor, there are steps to follow when dealing with VAT/GST/Sales tax codes in a template Set of Books:

    1. VAT/GST/Sales Tax accounts: If you are registered as a VAT/GST/Sales Tax vendor, you need to review the basic VAT/GST/Sales Tax accounts included in the "Current liabilities" section in the Chart of Accounts of your selected template Set of Books, downloaded from the "Let osFinancials help you to create a Set of Books?" option. You need to add additional Tax codes and edit the existing tax percentages for your Tax regime and jurisdiction. To do this, navigate to the Setup → Accounts menu on the Setup ribbon.  

    You may print a "Tax code - Tax rates" report on the User reports → Tax menu on the Reports ribbon to print a list of Tax codes and their respective percentages and the status whether the Tax code is enabled or disabled).  
    1. VAT/GST/Sales Tax Registration number: Enter the VAT/GST/Sales Tax registration number in the "Tax reg. no." field on the Setup → Company Info (Address tab).
    2. VAT/GST/Sales Tax - Document Headings: The decision to modify the document name should be guided by the legal requirements and guidelines of your tax jurisdiction to ensure compliance with invoicing regulations. By default, the document type "Invoice" will print on document layout files for the following documents will print on document layout files as follows:
      • Change Document Headings: Change the "Invoice" to "Tax Invoice" for your specific country or jurisdiction and/or industry (Setup → Documents setup menu on the Setup ribbon). Check the headings for the other document types (Credit notes, Quotes, Purchases, Supplier returns and Orders), and change the headings, if necessary.
      • Change the Names of Document Types: You may need to change the appropriate document type names such as "Invoice," "Credit note", "Quote", "Purchase", "Supplier return", or "Order" for your specific country or jurisdiction and/or industry. These document type names will display in the osFinancials interface and will print on "Translatable Document Layout Files with Dynamic Document Types" such as "Layout file", "Document layout" and "Document layout (15 code)" document layout files.
      • POS Tax Invoice: "POS Tax Invoice" will be printed on the "POS-SP100", POS 1 and POS 2 document layout files. To Change the "POS Tax Invoice"  label, you need to go to Tools → Customise language on the Setup ribbon and change the description of "LabelID - 906014".
    1. Select Document Layout Files: By default, "Layout file" is pre-selected in Set of Books templates.  The default "Layout file" supports Tax (VAT/GST/Sales Tax) related expressions and label descriptions. If you are registered as a Tax (VAT/GST/Sales Tax) vendor, you may select other Translatable Document Layout Files with Dynamic Document Types, such as "Document layout" and "Document layout (15 code)" document layout files, which includes tax related expressions and labels.
    2. Stock items/Inventory items Tax classes: When planning, designing and adding your stock/inventory system, you may create Tax classes to link your stock items (goods/services/products) to a Output tax and Input tax codes (including your Sales, Cost of Sales and Stock control accounts). You may need to add tax classes for each of your stock items and link them to the appropriate tax codes (including zero-rated and exempt tax codes).

    You may print a "Tax classes report" report on the Reports → Stock item listing - Tax classes menu (Reports ribbon) to print a list of Tax classes for Output tax, Input tax and their respective sales, cost of sales and stock control accounts.
