    Some features in the basic version of osFinancials5, are: 

    4K monitor support 

    osFinancials supports displays which can handle content in a resolution known as 4K. 4K resolution refers to a display with approximately 4,000 pixels in the horizontal direction, usually specified as 3840 pixels × 2160 lines (8.3 megapixels) or 4096 pixels × 2160 lines (8.8 megapixels). It provides a higher level of detail and clarity compared to lower-resolution displays.

    Themes

    Selecting supported themes 

    osFinancials5 allows you to personalise the interface by selecting different themes from the Theme menu on the Setup ribbon. The default installation includes the "Classic" theme. Both the Classic and "VS2010" themes utilize icons stored in the "Classic folder".

    If you prefer a different look, you can choose the "Blue, Glass, Silver" or "Soft-blue" theme in osFinancials5. These themes feature a navy-blue colour scheme and offer a collection of icons similar to those in the "Material" theme. Selecting a different theme from the Theme menu allows you to customize the interface to your preferences.

    Customising themes

    In osFinancials5, you have the option to edit the "colors.ini" file to customize various aspects of the theme, including theme style, ribbon colours, tablet mode, and more. To make changes, you can open the "colors.ini" file in your preferred system text editor such as Notepad or Notepad++. Within this file, you can modify the values of the settings to suit your specific requirements.

    Custom themes 

    In osFinancials5, you have the option to easily customise the colour of all 227 icons (in the SVG "Scalable Vector Graphics" file format) used in the interface. This customization process can be completed in less than a minute by utilizing a text editor such as Notepad++ and the Search and Replace feature. By replacing the default black colour with a colour of your preference, you can personalize the appearance of the icons in the "Material" theme to suit your taste.

    If you opt for the "Blue, Glass, Silver," or "Soft-blue" theme in osFinancials5, you will encounter a set of icons resembling those found in the "Material" theme, but featuring a navy-blue colour palette. These themes offer an alternative visual presentation with cohesive icons, enabling you to personalize the interface by selecting a different theme from the Theme menu on the Setup ribbon.

    Unlimited Sets of Books

    Unlimited Sets of Books - In osFinancials5, you may create and manage multiple independent Sets of Books (Accounting databases) within the same osFinancials5 installation. Each set of books has its own independent chart of accounts, general ledger, subsidiary ledgers, and transactional data.

    There are 87 Sets of Books (databases) available, which can be downloaded and customized, through the "Let osFinancials help you to create a Set of Books?" option, to meet specific language and tax requirements.     Ledger

    The general ledger is a core component of the accounting system in osFinancials. It serves as a central repository for recording and storing all financial transactions and budgets of a business. The general ledger contains individual accounts that track various types of financial activities, such as revenue, expenses, assets, liabilities, and equity.

    Each account in the general ledger represents a specific category or type of transaction, such as sales, purchases, expenses, etc. Transactions are recorded in the general ledger using a double-entry bookkeeping system, where every transaction has an equal debit and credit entry, ensuring that the accounting equation remains in balance.

    Assets = Liabilities + Equity 

    The general ledger provides a detailed and chronological record of all financial activities, allowing businesses to track and analyse their financial performance. It serves as the foundation for generating important financial reports such as the trial balance, income statement, and balance sheet.

    In osFinancials, the general ledger module allows users to create and manage their chart of accounts, record transactions, manage budgets, generate financial reports, and perform other essential accounting tasks. It provides a comprehensive view of a company's financial position and financial performance. This helps to facilitate accurate and reliable financial management and decision-making.

    Account groups and Financial Categories

    In osFinancials, you can add reporting groups for Account Group 1 and/or Account Group 2 to categorise your accounts further. These groups allow you to classify and organise accounts based on their nature or function. Each account can be mapped or linked to the appropriate reporting group (Account group 1 and/or Account group 2).  The category or classification for Balance sheet accounts and Income statement accounts could be as follows:

    1. Balance sheet accounts (ledger accounts, bank accounts, and tax accounts for VAT/GST/sales tax) could be linked to the appropriate reporting group (Account Group 1 and/or Account Group 2) such as Cash and cash equivalents, Fixed assets or Non-current assets, PP&E - Property, Plant, and Equipment, Current assets or Non-current assets, Intangible assets, Current liabilities, Non-current liabilities, Long-term liabilities, Equity.
    2. Income statement accounts (income accounts and expense accounts) could be linked to the appropriate reporting group (Account Group 1 and/or Account Group 2) such as Operating revenue, Other revenue, Cost of Sales (COS) / Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) / Cost of Services Rendered (COSR), Operating expenses, Non-operating revenue, Non-operating expenses.

    Financial categories: Each reporting group can be assigned to one of the five financial categories: assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, and expenses. This ensures that the accounts are properly categorized and aligned with the standard financial reporting structure.

    Sequence/order: In the Setup → Groups (Setup ribbon), you have the flexibility to change the sequence order of the reporting groups. This allows you to structure the layout of the chart of accounts in the trial balance, balance sheet, and income statement according to your preferences.

    By utilising reporting groups, financial categories, and sequence order, osFinancials enables you to effectively organize and analyse your chart of accounts, providing a clear representation of your financial data in various financial reports.

Configuring these settings, which reduce errors when processing transactions in journals:
      • Contra Account: Assign each batch (journal) to a specific contra account for easy identification and assignment of transactions to the corresponding contra account. This account will be the balancing account. When you balance the batch, the contra transactions will be generated to this contra account in the journal.
      • Lookup Accounts Configuration: You can set a journal so that you can select all accounts for the general ledger, debtors, creditors or only accounts for the general ledger or only debtors or only creditors in the journal.     1. Open Item Transactions: This is particularly useful for matching receipts or payments transactions with outstanding amounts. When you allocate transactions against open item accounts, you can choose to link the transactions against outstanding transactions.     Open item processing 

    The Open item processing feature in osFinancials is a method used in accounting and financial management to track and manage outstanding or unresolved transactions, often referred to as open items. An open item represents a transaction or a payment that has not yet been fully cleared or reconciled.

    In osFinancials, the open item processing module allows businesses to efficiently handle and monitor these open items, ensuring accurate tracking, reconciliation, and resolution of outstanding transactions. The module typically includes the following functionalities:

    1. Recording Open Items: Users can enter details of transactions or payments that are not yet fully reconciled or resolved. This may include invoices issued to customers, bills received from suppliers, outstanding payments, or any other transaction that requires further processing.
    2. Matching and Reconciliation: The open item processing module enables users to match open items with corresponding transactions or payments. For example, matching a customer payment with an outstanding invoice or reconciling a bank statement with recorded transactions. This helps identify and resolve discrepancies or differences between recorded transactions and actual financial records.
    3. Ageing Analysis: The module provides ageing analysis functionality, allowing users to categorize and analyse open items based on their age or duration since the transaction occurred. This helps monitor the time it takes to clear or resolve outstanding items and prioritize actions accordingly.
    4. Follow-up and Communication: Users can add notes or comments to open items, facilitating communication and collaboration within the organization. This feature allows employees involved in the resolution process to leave messages, update statuses, or provide additional information related to specific open items.
    5. Reporting: The open item processing module offers reporting capabilities to generate summaries and detailed reports on open items. These reports provide insights into the status of outstanding transactions, ageing analysis, unresolved balances, and other relevant information.

    By effectively utilizing open item processing in osFinancials, businesses can streamline their reconciliation processes, ensure accuracy in financial records, track and resolve outstanding transactions efficiently, and maintain up-to-date and reliable financial information.

    Backup / Restore 

    The Backup / Restore process supports the options

    Small backup 

    Small backup (books) . If you wish to e-mail the backup, you may select the "Small backup (books)" option to create a backup of your Set of Books database in a single file only (e.g. books.fdb - for the firebird database). If this option is selected, no images or other files linked to your Set of Books will not be included in your backup. This will reduce the size of your backup file. 

    Uni dump 

    If your Set of Books in osFinancials is created as a Microsoft SQL Server (MSSQL) database type, the "uni dump" file created as an SQL file type can be used to restore the Set of Books.

    The "uni dump" file contains SQL statements that represent the structure and data of the database. By executing these SQL statements, you can recreate the database and restore it to its previous state.

    To restore a Set of Books from a "uni dump" file in osFinancials using MSSQL, you would typically follow these steps:

    1. Ensure that you have a compatible version of MSSQL installed and running.
    2. Create an empty database in MSSQL that will serve as the destination for the restored Set of Books.
    3. Open the "uni dump" file in a text editor or an SQL client.
    4. Execute the SQL statements from the "uni dump" file in the MSSQL database. This can be done using an SQL client or by running a script.
    5. Once the SQL statements have been executed successfully, the Set of Books should be restored in the MSSQL database.

    It's important to note that the specific steps and tools for restoring the database from a "uni dump" file may vary depending on your environment and the version of MSSQL you are using. It's recommended to consult the documentation or resources specific to your version of MSSQL for accurate instructions on restoring a database from an SQL file. Additionally, it's always a good practice to backup your database before performing any restoration to ensure data integrity.

    When sending documents as email attachments, you have the option to choose different file formats, including PDF and UBL 2.1. Here's an explanation of each format:

    1. PDF (Portable Document Format): PDF is a widely used file format that preserves the formatting, fonts, and layout of a document. It ensures that the recipient sees the document exactly as intended, regardless of their software or operating system. PDF files are typically read-only, allowing recipients to view and print the document but not easily edit its content. Sending documents as PDF attachments guarantees compatibility and easy viewing for recipients.
    2. UBL 2.1 (Universal Business Language): UBL is an XML-based standard for exchanging business documents electronically. UBL 2.1 is a specific version of the UBL standard. UBL files follow a predefined structure and contain structured information about various business documents such as invoices, purchase orders, and payment instructions. UBL files are commonly used in business-to-business (B2B) transactions to facilitate automated processing and interoperability between different software systems. By sending documents as UBL 2.1 attachments, you provide recipients with machine-readable data that can be easily processed by their business systems.

    When emailing documents, you can attach both a PDF file for human-readable viewing and a UBL 2.1 file for machine-readable data. This offers flexibility to the recipient in interacting with the information based on their specific needs and systems.
