    Sales tax / Use tax - Wisconsin (WI)

    Wisconsin (WI) - Tax system key points

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, here are some key points about the tax system in Wisconsin (WI):

    1. Sales Tax: Wisconsin imposes a state-level sales tax on the retail sale of tangible personal property and certain services. The general state sales tax rate is 5%. However, there may be additional local sales taxes imposed by counties and municipalities, resulting in varying total sales tax rates in different areas of the state.
    2. Use Tax: Wisconsin also has a use tax, which is applied when tangible personal property is purchased out-of-state and brought into Wisconsin for use, storage, or consumption. If sales tax was not paid at the time of purchase, the purchaser owes use tax on the value of the property.
    3. Individual Income Tax: Wisconsin levies an individual income tax on residents and non-residents who earn income in the state. The state has a progressive income tax system with tax rates ranging from 3.54% to 7.65% based on income levels.
    4. Corporate Income Tax: Wisconsin imposes a corporate income tax on corporations conducting business in the state. The corporate income tax rates range from 4.0% to 7.9% based on taxable income.
    5. Property Taxes: Wisconsin imposes property taxes on real property, such as land and buildings. Property tax rates are determined at the local level and can vary significantly by municipality.
    6. Vehicle Taxes and Fees: Wisconsin imposes various taxes and fees on motor vehicles, including registration fees, license plate fees, and a vehicle-specific tax known as the Title and Registration (T&R) fee.
    7. Inheritance Tax and Estate Tax: Wisconsin does not impose an inheritance tax or an estate tax. As of my last update, both the inheritance tax and the estate tax have been eliminated.
    8. No Local Income Tax: Wisconsin does not allow local jurisdictions to impose their own income taxes.
    9. Tax Credits and Incentives: Wisconsin offers various tax credits and incentives to individuals and businesses, including the Homestead Credit for eligible homeowners and credits related to child care, education, and more.

    It's important to note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's recommended to consult the Wisconsin Department of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to taxes in Wisconsin.

    Wisconsin (WI) - Sales Tax and Use Tax

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, Wisconsin imposes a sales tax and use tax on various goods and services. Here are some key points about the Wisconsin Sales Tax and Use Tax:

    Sales Tax:

    • Sales Tax Rate: The state sales tax rate in Wisconsin is 5%. Additionally, there may be county sales taxes and stadium district taxes imposed by certain counties and municipalities, which can result in varying total sales tax rates in different areas of the state.
    • Exemptions: Some items and services may be exempt from Wisconsin's sales tax. Common exemptions include prescription drugs, groceries, and sales to tax-exempt organizations.
    • Filing and Payment: Businesses that make sales subject to Wisconsin's sales tax are required to register with the Wisconsin Department of Revenue and collect the appropriate tax from their customers. The filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, or annually) depends on the business's sales volume. Businesses must file sales tax returns, reporting the total sales made and the corresponding tax collected.
    • Online Sales: Wisconsin has adopted economic nexus laws for online sales. This means that businesses with a certain level of sales or transactions in the state, even if they have no physical presence there, may be required to collect and remit sales tax.

    Use Tax:

    • Use Tax Rate: The use tax rate in Wisconsin is also 5%. The use tax is applicable when residents or businesses in Wisconsin purchase taxable items from out-of-state sellers who do not collect Wisconsin sales tax. The purchaser is then responsible for paying the corresponding use tax directly to the state.
    • Filing and Payment: Individuals and businesses in Wisconsin are required to report and remit use tax on their state income tax return if they made purchases without paying sales tax. This typically applies to out-of-state purchases, internet purchases, and other transactions where sales tax was not collected at the time of purchase.
    • Online Purchases: Use tax in Wisconsin is relevant to out-of-state purchases and online purchases where sales tax was not collected. Individuals and businesses are required to report and remit the use tax on these transactions when filing their state income tax return.

    Please note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's essential to consult the Wisconsin Department of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to sales tax and use tax in Wisconsin.
