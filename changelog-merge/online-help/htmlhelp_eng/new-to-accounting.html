    New to Accounting

    Welcome to the world of accounting with osFinancials accounting software! Whether you are starting a new business or taking over the financial management of an existing one, osFinancials provides a powerful and easy-to-use accounting solution for your needs. With osFinancials, you can easily manage your finances, keep track of transactions, generate reports, and more. This software is designed to simplify accounting tasks and provide accurate financial information to help you make informed decisions. As a beginner to accounting and osFinancials, we will guide you through the basics of the software and help you get started on your journey towards financial management success.

    osFinancials accounting software can help you save time and reduce errors, allowing you to focus on other important aspects of your business. In this introduction, we will discuss the benefits of using osFinancials accounting software and how it can help simplify your accounting tasks, even if you have little to no experience with accounting.

    Many accounting systems have been marketed as "easy-to-use," but their main function is to present financial reports or statements to investors, bank managers, creditors, and consultants in an understandable format. These reports provide crucial financial information that is essential for decision-making.

    However, systems that take shortcuts and fail to comply with accounting principles often fail to communicate important financial information to accountants. Accountants have their own language, and if you are not familiar with it, accounting systems like osFinancials may seem obscure at first. You don't need to be fluent in accounting language, but it's important to understand the basics.

    Fortunately, osFinancials is a Generally Accepted Accounting Practice (GAAP) compliant system. It follows accounting principles and standards that have been widely accepted and adopted by the accounting profession. Moreover, osFinancials provides steps and features that can guide users in the right direction and minimize the need for extensive accounting knowledge.

    Hands-on Tutorials 

    osFinancials5 installation provides tutorial Sets of Books in both English and Afrikaans. These tutorial books allow users to explore different settings, transactions, and reports within the software. Here are the available tutorial sets: 

    Accounting Made Easy

    English tutorials (using separate receipt and payments  journals for banking / cash transactions)

    Accounting Made Easy - Bank Import

    English tutorials (using the BankImport plugin for banking / cash transactions)

    Rekeningkunde Met Gemak (Afrikaans)

    Afrikaanse handleidings (gebruik aparte ontvangste- en betalingsjoernale vir bank- / kontanttransaksies)

    The following Set of Books based on this tutorial, is installed in the “…\bin\Tutorials\ENGLISH\” folder:

    • HANDYMAN (No transactions)
    • HANDYMAN-A (Unposted transactions)
    • HANDYMAN-B (Posted transactions)

    The following Set of Books based on this tutorial, is installed in the “…\bin\Tutorials\BANK-IMPORT\” folder:

    • HANDY-BANK (No transactions).
    • HANDY-BANK-A (Unposted transactions)
    • HANDY-BANK-B (Posted transactions).
    • HANDY-BANK-UNPOSTED-BANK-STATEMENT – Posted transactions, excluding bank / cash transactions. Bank statement needs to be imported from the “…\bin\Tutorials\BANK-IMPORT\BANK-STATEMENTS” folder.

    Die volgende Stel Boeke gebaseer op die Afrikaanse handleidings, is in die “…\bin\Tutorials\AFRIKAANS\”-lêergids geïnstalleer:

    • NUTSMAN (Geen transaksies).
    • NUTSMAN-A (Ongeposteerde transaksies)
    • NUTSMAN-B (Geposteerde transaksies).

    To open the tutorial Sets of Books, installed in the "bin / Tutorials" directory, you need to click on the Open icon on the Start ribbon.  On the “Set of Books” screen, click on the Browse icon and select the desired tutorial Set of Books that you wish to follow. 

    Additionally, there are other tutorial folders available with relevant files for import into batches. The following folders contains files which may be imported into batches:

    • EXPORTED-BATCH-Delimited-File – Exported batch transactions using the “Write delimited file” option and may be imported into a batch using the “Delimited file import” option (F9:Process icon) on batches.  
    • EXPORTED-BATCH-Spreadsheets – Exported batch transactions in the *.xlsx (Excel 2007-365) file format. These transaction files may be opened in the Spreadsheet import icon on batches, and imported into a selected batch. 

    These tutorials are designed to guide users, regardless of their accounting experience, through the basics of accounting and demonstrate how to effectively use osFinancials for managing business finances. The tutorials cover various aspects, including software features, chart of accounts setup, transaction creation, processing sales and purchase documents, and report generation. They also serve as a reference guide to osFinancials.

    By the end of these tutorials, users will have gained a solid understanding of the accounting process and will be able to confidently utilize osFinancials for managing their business finances.
