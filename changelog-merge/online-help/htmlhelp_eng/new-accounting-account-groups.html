    Account groups - Financial categories - Income statement

    Each of the Income statement Financial categories, which are INCOME and EXPENSES, are further classified into reporting groups for accounts, which are described as follows:

    REPORTING GROUPS FOR ACCOUNTS

    DESCRIPTION

    NET PROFIT

    RETAINED INCOME

    RETAINED INCOME, at the beginning of the financial year, the income/expense accounts are zeroed and the resulting balance transferred to the retained income account.

    Income / Expense accounts created (in osFinancials) should be assigned to an appropriate reporting group and linked to a financial category in osFinancials ( INCOME or  EXPENSES).

    The Financial results influence the net worth of a business. A profit will increase the capital value of a business; the opposite is true for a loss.

    *The DR/CR column in the table indicates whether the account has a debit or credit balance. The NET PROFIT or NET LOSS is calculated by subtracting the total expenses from the total income. At the beginning of the financial year, the income/expense accounts are zeroed, and the resulting balance is transferred to the RETAINED INCOME account.

    Accounts created in osFinancials should be assigned to an appropriate reporting group and linked to a financial category (INCOME or EXPENSES) in osFinancials. The financial results influence the net worth of a business, with a profit increasing the capital value of a business and a loss decreasing it.
