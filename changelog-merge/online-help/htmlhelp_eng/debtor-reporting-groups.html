  1. Debtor (customer / client) accounts

    Debtor groups

    This option allows you to set up to two Reporting groups for your Debtors (customers / clients). Debtor groups allows you to group or categorise the Debtors accounts on which you would like to report. Reporting groups for debtors can vary depending on the specific needs and requirements of your business. 

    These Debtor groups can be linked to each debtor (customer/ client) account. 
    • You may use the context menu option on the debtors grid to set or change the reporting groups for Debtor group 1 and/or Debtor group 2  for selected debtor account(s).
    • You may use the debtors grid to filter, search or group debtor accounts.
    • You may print Debtor listing reports and Print labels for postal addresses and delivery addresses for all or only for debtor accounts linked to a selected debtor group or groups.
    • You may print Sales analysis reports for Debtor group 1 or Debtor group 2.  

    Examples of reporting groups for debtors (customers / clients)

    Here are a few examples of reporting groups that you can use for debtor accounts:

    1. Customer type: You can create reporting groups based on the type of customers or clients you have. For example, you may have reporting groups such as "Retail customers," "Wholesale customers," or "Corporate clients."
    2. Geographic region: If you serve customers from different geographic regions, you can create reporting groups based on their locations. This can be helpful for analysing regional sales or managing relationships with customers in specific areas.
    3. Sales channel: If you sell your products or services through different channels, you can create reporting groups to track and analyse sales performance for each channel. This could include groups like "Online sales," "Retail store sales," or "Direct sales."
    4. Payment terms: You can create reporting groups based on the payment terms agreed upon with your debtors. This allows you to track and analyse the payment behaviour of different groups. For example, you might have reporting groups for "Cash sales," "Credit sales," or "Instalment payments."
    5. Sales representative: If you have a sales team responsible for different territories or customer segments, you can create reporting groups based on the sales representatives. This can help in assessing individual performance and analysing sales by representative.
    6. Customer rating: If you have a rating system to evaluate the creditworthiness or loyalty of your customers, you can create reporting groups based on their ratings. This allows you to identify high-value or high-risk customers. For example, you might have reporting groups for "Gold customers," "Silver customers," or "Preferred customers."

    These are just a few examples, and you can customize reporting groups based on the unique characteristics and needs of your business. The goal is to create groups that allow you to analyse and report on debtor data in a meaningful way, enabling you to make informed decisions and effectively manage your customer relationships.

    Define reporting groups for debtors (customers / clients)

    To set reporting groups for Debtor accounts: 

    1. On the Setup ribbon, select Setup â†’ Groups. The "Groups (Account group 1)" default screen will be displayed.
    2. Click on the Debtor group 1 button. 

    1. If you wish to rename the Reporting group name - Debtor group 1, over-type the description with your new description. This will change the description of the button for Debtor group 1.
    2. To add a new Group to the root directory or list, select the REPORTING GROUP ROOT on the list and click on the New button.
    3. Enter the name of your Debtor group 1 and click on "REPORTING GROUP ROOT" on the list. 
    4. Repeat the process until you have entered all the Debtor groups, etc. 

    Adding sub groups for debtor groups:

    Select a Debtor group, for example; "Debtor group 1A" and click on the New button. Enter the name of your sub group.  
    1. If you need to add Reporting group 2 for Debtors, click on the Debtor group 2 button and repeat the process as for Debtor group 1.
    2. Click on the OK button to save your changes and close (exit) this screen.

    Changing the sequence of groups for debtors:

    Select a Debtor group, for example; "Debtor group 1B" and click on the Move up or Move down button. This will list the debtor group according to your preferred sequence.  

    You may click on the Print button to print a list of your debtor groups.

    You may set colours to display on the Debtor accounts List screen. To do this, click on the Back colour button to set the rows background colour and the Font colour button to set the Font colour. 

    To retain and save the selected colors, you also need to set both the "Back color" and the "Font color".
