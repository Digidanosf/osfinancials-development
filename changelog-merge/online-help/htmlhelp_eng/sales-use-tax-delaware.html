    Delaware (DE) - Tax system key points

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, here are some key points about the tax system in Delaware:

    1. No Sales Tax: Delaware does not have a statewide sales tax. This means there is no sales tax imposed on goods or services sold within the state. As a result, retail purchases in Delaware are generally tax-free at the state level.
    2. No Use Tax: Similarly, Delaware does not have a statewide use tax. Use tax is typically applied when residents or businesses purchase taxable items from out-of-state sellers who do not collect the state's sales tax. In Delaware, there is no use tax applicable to such transactions.
    3. Corporate Income Tax: Delaware is known for its favorable business climate and attracts many corporations due to its corporate-friendly tax laws. The state imposes a corporate income tax on corporations doing business in Delaware.
    4. Franchise Tax: Delaware imposes an annual franchise tax on corporations incorporated in the state. The franchise tax amount is based on the corporation's authorized shares or the number of issued shares and their par value.
    5. Personal Income Tax: Delaware levies a personal income tax on individuals residing in the state. The income tax rates are graduated, meaning that higher income levels are subject to higher tax rates.
    6. Property Taxes: Delaware imposes property taxes on real property, such as land and buildings. Property tax rates vary by county and are levied by the local governments.
    7. Limited Business Taxation: Besides the corporate income tax, Delaware does not impose a state-level sales tax, gross receipts tax, or inventory tax, making it attractive to businesses.
    8. Estate Tax: Delaware does not have its own estate tax. However, it does have a "pick-up" tax, which means that it can collect a portion of the federal estate tax paid by the estate.
    9. Tax-Friendly Trust Laws: Delaware is known for its trust-friendly laws, making it a popular state for establishing trusts.
    10. No Vehicle Sales Tax: Delaware does not impose a sales tax on vehicle purchases, making it a tax-free state for buying cars.

    It's important to note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's recommended to consult the Delaware Division of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to taxes in Delaware.
