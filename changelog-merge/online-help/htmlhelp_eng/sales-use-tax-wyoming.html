    Wyoming (WY) - Tax system key points

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, here are some key points about the tax system in Wyoming (WY):

    1. Sales Tax: Wyoming does not have a state-level sales tax. As a result, there is no general sales tax imposed on the retail sale of tangible personal property or services at the state level.
    2. Use Tax: Since Wyoming does not have a state sales tax, there is no corresponding use tax imposed on the purchase of goods brought into the state.
    3. Individual Income Tax: Wyoming does not impose an individual income tax on residents' or non-residents' earned income. This absence of an income tax is one of the key features of Wyoming's tax system.
    4. Corporate Income Tax: Similar to individual income tax, Wyoming does not impose a corporate income tax on corporations conducting business in the state.
    5. Property Taxes: Wyoming imposes property taxes on real property, such as land and buildings. Property tax rates are determined at the local level, and the state has relatively low property tax rates compared to other states.
    6. Vehicle Taxes and Fees: Wyoming imposes various taxes and fees on motor vehicles, including registration fees, license plate fees, and certain excise taxes.
    7. Inheritance Tax and Estate Tax: Wyoming does not impose an inheritance tax or an estate tax. This means there are no state-level taxes on the transfer of property upon death.
    8. No Local Income Tax: Wyoming does not allow local jurisdictions to impose their own income taxes.
    9. Severance Taxes: Wyoming relies significantly on revenue from severance taxes, which are levied on the extraction of natural resources such as oil, gas, coal, and minerals.
    10. Tax Credits and Incentives: Wyoming offers various tax credits and incentives to individuals and businesses, including incentives related to agriculture, manufacturing, and energy production.

    It's important to note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's recommended to consult the Wyoming Department of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to taxes in Wyoming.

    Wyoming (WY) - Sales Tax and Use Tax

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, Wyoming imposes a sales tax and use tax on various goods and services. Here are some key points about the Wyoming Sales Tax and Use Tax:

    Sales Tax:

    • Sales Tax Rate: The state sales tax rate in Wyoming is 4%. However, Wyoming is one of the few states that does not impose a statewide sales tax on most goods and services. Instead, local jurisdictions have the option to impose a local sales and use tax, which can result in varying total sales tax rates in different areas of the state.
    • Exemptions: Wyoming does not impose a statewide sales tax on most goods and services, but local jurisdictions that have opted for a local sales tax may have exemptions for certain items.
    • Filing and Payment: Businesses in areas with a local sales tax in Wyoming are required to register with the Wyoming Department of Revenue and collect the appropriate tax from their customers. The filing frequency and process depend on the local jurisdiction's rules. Businesses must file sales tax returns, reporting the total sales made and the corresponding tax collected.
    • Online Sales: Wyoming does not have statewide economic nexus laws for online sales due to the absence of a statewide sales tax. However, local jurisdictions with local sales taxes may have their own rules for taxing online sales.

    Use Tax:

    • Wyoming does not have a statewide use tax. The concept of a use tax is closely tied to the presence of a sales tax, which Wyoming does not have at the state level. However, individuals and businesses may still be subject to local use taxes in areas with local sales taxes.
    • Online Purchases: The absence of a statewide sales tax means that Wyoming residents are not generally required to pay sales or use tax on purchases made within the state. However, they may still be subject to any local sales taxes imposed by their jurisdiction.

    Please note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's essential to consult the Wyoming Department of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to sales tax and use tax in Wyoming.
