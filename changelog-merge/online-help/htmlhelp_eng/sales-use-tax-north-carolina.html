    North Carolina (NC) - Tax system key points

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, here are some key points about the tax system in North Carolina (NC):

    1. Sales Tax: North Carolina imposes a state-level sales tax on the retail sale of tangible personal property and certain services. The general state sales tax rate is 4.75%. Additionally, local jurisdictions such as counties and municipalities can impose their own local sales taxes, resulting in varying total sales tax rates in different areas of the state.
    2. Use Tax: North Carolina also has a use tax, which is applied when tangible personal property is purchased out-of-state and brought into North Carolina for use, storage, or consumption. If sales tax was not paid at the time of purchase, the purchaser owes use tax on the value of the property.
    3. Individual Income Tax: North Carolina levies an individual income tax on residents and non-residents who earn income in the state. The state has a progressive income tax system with tax rates ranging from 5.25% to 5.499%.
    4. Corporate Income Tax: North Carolina imposes a corporate income tax on corporations conducting business in the state. The corporate income tax rate is 2.5% for the 2021 tax year and will decrease to 2.0% for the 2022 tax year.
    5. Franchise Tax: North Carolina imposes a franchise tax on corporations and LLCs registered in the state. The tax is based on the company's net worth or book value and is calculated using specified rates.
    6. Property Taxes: North Carolina imposes property taxes on real property, such as land and buildings. Property tax rates are determined at the local level and can vary significantly by county and municipality.
    7. Vehicle Taxes and Fees: North Carolina imposes various taxes and fees on motor vehicles, including sales tax on vehicle purchases, registration fees, and a highway use tax based on the vehicle's value.
    8. Excise Taxes: North Carolina imposes excise taxes on various items such as tobacco products, alcoholic beverages, gasoline, and motor fuels. The rates for these taxes can vary.
    9. Inheritance Tax and Estate Tax: North Carolina phased out its estate tax, and it does not have an inheritance tax.
    10. Local Income Tax: North Carolina does not impose a statewide local income tax.
    11. Local Taxes: In addition to state taxes, local governments in North Carolina can impose additional taxes or fees, such as local sales taxes, property taxes, and other local assessments.
    12. Tax Credits and Incentives: North Carolina offers various tax credits and incentives to individuals and businesses, including credits related to job creation, research and development, and more.

    It's important to note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's recommended to consult the North Carolina Department of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to taxes in North Carolina.

    North Carolina (NC) - Sales Tax and Use Tax

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, North Carolina imposes a sales tax and use tax on various goods and services. Here are some key points about the North Carolina Sales Tax and Use Tax:

    Sales Tax:

    • Sales Tax Rate: The state sales tax rate in North Carolina is 4.75%. Additionally, there are local sales taxes imposed by counties and municipalities, which can result in varying total sales tax rates across the state.
    • Exemptions: Some items and services may be exempt from North Carolina's sales tax. Common exemptions include prescription drugs, groceries, and sales to tax-exempt organizations.
    • Filing and Payment: Businesses that make sales subject to North Carolina's sales tax are required to register with the North Carolina Department of Revenue and collect the appropriate tax from their customers. The filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, or annually) depends on the business's sales volume. Businesses must file sales tax returns, reporting the total sales made and the corresponding tax collected.
    • Online Sales: North Carolina has adopted economic nexus laws for online sales. This means that businesses with a certain level of sales or transactions in the state, even if they have no physical presence there, may be required to collect and remit sales tax.

    Use Tax:

    • Use Tax Rate: The use tax rate in North Carolina is also 4.75%. The use tax is applicable when residents or businesses in North Carolina purchase taxable items from out-of-state sellers who do not collect North Carolina sales tax. The purchaser is then responsible for paying the corresponding use tax directly to the state.
    • Filing and Payment: Individuals and businesses in North Carolina are required to report and remit use tax on their state income tax return if they made purchases without paying sales tax. This typically applies to out-of-state purchases, internet purchases, and other transactions where sales tax was not collected at the time of purchase.
    • Online Purchases: Use tax in North Carolina is relevant to out-of-state purchases and online purchases where sales tax was not collected. Individuals and businesses are required to report and remit the use tax on these transactions when filing their state income tax return.

    Please note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's essential to consult the North Carolina Department of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to sales tax and use tax in North Carolina.

    North Carolina (NC) - Sales Tax and Use Tax - Notes

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, here's information about North Carolina's sales tax and use tax:

    Sales Tax: North Carolina imposes a general sales tax on most retail sales of tangible personal property and certain services. The state's base sales tax rate is 4.75%. However, the actual total sales tax rate can be higher due to additional local sales taxes imposed by counties and municipalities. Local sales tax rates can vary across different areas of the state.

    It's important to note that North Carolina has a few exemptions from the sales tax. Common exemptions include prescription medications, most groceries, and some types of medical equipment. However, the specifics of exemptions can vary, and it's advised to check with the North Carolina Department of Revenue or a tax professional for accurate and up-to-date information.

    Use Tax: Use tax is a companion to the sales tax and is designed to ensure that individuals and businesses don't avoid paying sales tax by making purchases from out-of-state vendors that do not collect North Carolina sales tax. If you buy taxable items for use in North Carolina from an out-of-state vendor and the vendor doesn't collect North Carolina sales tax, you are generally required to report and pay use tax on those purchases. Use tax is usually at the same rate as the state sales tax.

    In practical terms, use tax is often applicable to online purchases, catalog purchases, or purchases made out of state. Individuals and businesses are expected to self-report and remit the use tax when applicable.

    It's important to keep in mind that tax laws and rates can change, so for the most current and accurate information about North Carolina's sales tax and use tax, you should consult the North Carolina Department of Revenue or seek advice from a tax professional.
