    Sales tax / Use tax - Florida (FL)

    Florida (FL) - Tax system key points

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, here are some key points about the tax system in Florida:

    1. Sales Tax: Florida imposes a state-level sales tax on the retail sale of tangible personal property and certain services. The general state sales tax rate is 6%. However, there are some exceptions to the general rate, such as a reduced rate of 3% for sales of new mobile homes, 4% for amusement machine receipts, 5.5% for the rental, lease, or license of commercial real property, and 6.95% for electricity.
    2. Use Tax: Florida also has a use tax, which is applied when tangible personal property is purchased out-of-state and brought into Florida for use, storage, or consumption. If sales tax was not paid at the time of purchase, the purchaser owes use tax on the value of the property.
    3. No Individual Income Tax: Florida is one of the few states that do not impose an individual income tax on wages and salaries.
    4. No Corporate Income Tax on Regular C Corporations: Florida does not impose a corporate income tax on regular C corporations. However, there is a corporate income tax on certain financial institutions and utility companies.
    5. No Estate Tax: Florida does not have its own estate tax. Additionally, it does not have a "pick-up" tax, meaning it does not collect a portion of the federal estate tax paid by the estate.
    6. Property Taxes: Florida imposes property taxes on real property, such as land and buildings. Property tax rates are determined at the local level and can vary by county.
    7. Communication Services Tax (CST): Florida imposes a Communication Services Tax on services such as telecommunications, cable, satellite, and VoIP services.
    8. Documentary Stamp Tax: Florida imposes a documentary stamp tax on various documents, such as deeds, promissory notes, and mortgages.
    9. Gross Receipts Tax: Florida has a gross receipts tax on certain businesses, including rental car companies, commercial rentals, and certain tobacco products.
    10. No Local Income Tax: Florida prohibits local jurisdictions from imposing their own income taxes.

    It's important to note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's recommended to consult the Florida Department of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to taxes in Florida.

    Florida (FL) - Sales Tax and Use Tax

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, Florida imposes both a sales tax and a use tax on various goods and services. Here are some key points about the Florida Sales Tax and Use Tax:

    Sales Tax:

    1. Sales Tax Rate: The state sales tax rate in Florida is 6%. Additionally, some counties may impose an additional discretionary sales surtax, which means the total sales tax rate can be higher in certain areas. The combined state and local sales tax rates can range from 6% to 8.5% or higher, depending on the county.
    2. Exemptions: Some items and services may be exempt from Florida's sales tax. Common exemptions include prescription drugs, certain groceries, medical equipment, and sales to tax-exempt organizations.
    3. Filing and Payment: Businesses that make sales subject to Florida's sales tax are required to register with the Florida Department of Revenue and collect the appropriate tax from their customers. The filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, or annually) depends on the business's sales volume. Businesses must file sales tax returns, reporting the total sales made and the corresponding tax collected.
    4. Online Sales: Florida has adopted economic nexus laws for online sales. This means that businesses with a certain level of sales or transactions in the state, even if they have no physical presence there, may be required to collect and remit sales tax.

    Use Tax:

    1. Use Tax Rate: The use tax rate in Florida is the same as the sales tax rate, which is 6%. The use tax is applicable when residents or businesses in Florida purchase taxable items from out-of-state sellers who do not collect Florida sales tax. The purchaser is then responsible for paying the corresponding use tax directly to the state.
    2. Filing and Payment: Individuals and businesses in Florida are required to report and remit use tax on their state income tax return if they made purchases without paying sales tax. This typically applies to out-of-state purchases, internet purchases, and other transactions where sales tax was not collected at the time of purchase.
    3. Online Purchases: As mentioned above, use tax in Florida is relevant to out-of-state purchases and online purchases where sales tax was not collected. Individuals and businesses are required to report and remit the use tax on these transactions when filing their state income tax return.

    Please note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's essential to consult the Florida Department of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to sales tax and use tax in Florida.

    Florida - Special Sales Tax Rates

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, Florida does not have any special sales tax rates in addition to the standard state sales tax rate. The state sales tax rate in Florida is 6%, and this rate applies uniformly to most taxable transactions throughout the state.

    Florida does have a discretionary sales surtax, also known as the local option sales tax, which is imposed by many counties. This surtax is in addition to the state sales tax and varies by county. The discretionary sales surtax is used to fund various county and local government initiatives, such as infrastructure projects, public services, and education.

    The combined state sales tax and discretionary sales surtax rates can range from 6.5% to 8.5% or higher, depending on the county where the taxable transaction occurs. Each county has the authority to set its own discretionary sales surtax rate, and the rate may differ even within the same region.

    It's important to be aware of the specific tax rates applicable in your area and for the goods or services you are buying or selling. For the most current and accurate information regarding Florida's sales tax rates, exemptions, and any other relevant tax regulations, it's recommended to consult the Florida Department of Revenue or a tax professional. They can provide the latest information on sales tax rates and any specific local taxes or exemptions that may be applicable in Florida.

    Florida - Sales Tax - Notes

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, Florida imposes a sales and use tax on various goods and services. Here are some key points about the Florida Sales and Use Tax:

    1. Sales Tax Rate: The state sales tax rate in Florida is 6%. Additionally, local jurisdictions (counties) may impose their own discretionary sales surtax, which can add up to an additional 2.5%. The total sales tax rate, including state and local taxes, can range from 6% to 8.5% depending on the specific location.
    2. Use Tax: Florida also has a use tax, which is applicable when residents or businesses in the state purchase taxable items from out-of-state sellers who do not collect Florida sales tax. The purchaser is then responsible for paying the corresponding use tax directly to the state.
    3. Exemptions: Some items and services are exempt from Florida's sales and use tax. Common exemptions include prescription drugs, groceries, medical equipment, and agricultural products.
    4. Filing and Payment: Businesses that make sales subject to Florida's sales tax are required to register with the Florida Department of Revenue (DOR) and collect the appropriate tax from their customers. The filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, or annually) depends on the business's sales volume. Businesses must file sales tax returns, reporting the total sales made and the corresponding tax collected.
    5. Online Sales: Florida has not adopted an economic nexus law for online sales. However, it does require out-of-state sellers with a substantial number of sales in the state to collect and remit sales tax, provided they have sufficient nexus under other provisions of the law.
    6. Resale Certificates: Businesses that purchase goods for resale are eligible for a resale certificate. This certificate allows them to buy goods without paying sales tax on those items. Instead, they will collect sales tax when they sell the goods to the end consumer.

    Please note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's essential to consult the Florida Department of Revenue or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to sales and use tax in Florida.

    Florida Sales and Use Tax - Additional information 

    Here are some more key points:

    1. Sales Tax Exceptions: The general state sales tax rate in Florida is 6%, but there are exceptions with different rates for specific items or services. For example:
      • Retail sales of new mobile homes have a reduced tax rate of 3%.
      • Amusement machine receipts are taxed at a rate of 4%.
      • The rental, lease, or license of commercial real property is taxed at a rate of 5.5%.
      • Electricity is subject to a higher tax rate of 6.95%.
    1. Discretionary Sales Surtax: Many Florida counties impose a discretionary sales surtax, also known as a county tax, in addition to the state sales tax. The county surtax rate is added to the state sales tax rate and applies to most transactions subject to sales or use tax. The surtax rate varies by county and is applied to taxable items or services delivered into the county. Different rates may apply to motor vehicles and mobile homes based on the purchaser's home address.
    2. Use Tax: Florida's use tax is due when sales tax was not paid at the time of purchase. This tax applies to the use or consumption of taxable goods or services in situations such as buying a taxable item in Florida without paying sales tax, using a tax-exempt item for personal use or business purposes, or bringing a taxable item from outside Florida without paying sales tax on it.
    3. Transient Rental Taxes: In addition to the state sales and use tax and discretionary sales surtax, some Florida counties may impose local option transient rental taxes on short-term rentals or leases of accommodations. These taxes apply to hotels, motels, apartments, rooming houses, mobile home parks, RV parks, condominiums, and timeshare resorts for rental terms of six months or less. The local transient rental taxes are often reported and remitted directly to the local government, while sales tax and discretionary sales surtax on transient rentals are reported and remitted to the Florida Department of Revenue.

    It's important to keep in mind that tax laws can change, and it's advisable to verify the most current information with the Florida Department of Revenue or a tax professional for any updates or specific questions regarding sales and use tax in Florida.
