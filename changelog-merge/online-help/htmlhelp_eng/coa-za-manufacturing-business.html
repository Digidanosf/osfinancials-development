    South Africa - Chart of Accounts - Manufacturing

    Sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for a South African Manufacturing business?

    A Chart of Accounts (COA) is a structured list of all the accounts and categories used to organize a company's financial transactions. For a South African manufacturing business, the COA needs to reflect the specific needs and requirements of the industry and the country's accounting practices. Here's a sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for a South African manufacturing business:

    1. Assets 
      • 1.1 Current Assets 
        • 1.1.1 Cash and Cash Equivalents 
        • 1.1.2 Accounts Receivable 
        • 1.1.3 Inventory (Raw materials, work in progress, finished goods) 
        • 1.1.4 Prepaid Expenses 
        • 1.1.5 Other Current Assets
      • 1.2 Non-Current Assets 
        • 1.2.1 Property, Plant, and Equipment (Land, Buildings, Machinery, etc.) 
        • 1.2.2 Intangible Assets (Patents, Trademarks, Software, etc.) 
        • 1.2.3 Long-Term Investments 
        • 1.2.4 Other Non-Current Assets
    1. Liabilities 
      • 2.1 Current Liabilities 
        • 2.1.1 Accounts Payable 
        • 2.1.2 Short-Term Loans and Borrowings 
        • 2.1.3 Accrued Liabilities 
        • 2.1.4 Taxes Payable 
        • 2.1.5 Other Current Liabilities
      • 2.2 Non-Current Liabilities 
        • 2.2.1 Long-Term Loans and Borrowings 
        • 2.2.2 Deferred Tax Liabilities 
        • 2.2.3 Other Non-Current Liabilities
    1. Equity 
      • 3.1 Share Capital 
        • 3.1.1 Ordinary Shares 
        • 3.1.2 Preference Shares 
      • 3.2 Retained Earnings 
      • 3.3 Other Equity Items
    1. Income 
      • 4.1 Sales Revenue 
      • 4.2 Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) 
      • 4.3 Gross Profit 
      • 4.4 Operating Revenue (if applicable) 
      • 4.5 Operating Expenses 
        • 4.5.1 Selling and Marketing Expenses 
        • 4.5.2 General and Administrative Expenses 
        • 4.5.3 Research and Development Expenses 
      • 4.6 Operating Profit 
      • 4.7 Other Income (e.g., interest income, rental income)
    1. Expenses 
      • 5.1 Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) 
        • 5.1.1 Direct Materials 
        • 5.1.2 Direct Labor 
        • 5.1.3 Factory Overhead 
      • 5.2 Selling and Marketing Expenses 
        • 5.2.1 Advertising and Promotion 
        • 5.2.2 Sales Commissions 
      • 5.3 General and Administrative Expenses 
        • 5.3.1 Salaries and Wages 
        • 5.3.2 Rent and Utilities 
        • 5.3.3 Office Supplies 
      • 5.4 Research and Development Expenses 
      • 5.5 Other Operating Expenses
    1. Cost of Sales
    2. Taxation 
      • 7.1 Current Tax 
      • 7.2 Deferred Tax
    1. Other Comprehensive Income 
      • 8.1 Foreign Currency Translation Gains/Losses 
      • 8.2 Unrealized Gains/Losses on Investments 
      • 8.3 Other Items
    1. Gains and Losses 
      • 9.1 Gain on Sale of Assets 
      • 9.2 Loss on Impairment of Assets
    1. Intercompany Transactions 
      • 10.1 Intercompany Revenue 
      • 10.2 Intercompany Expenses
    1. Dividends
    2. Subsidiary and Associate Companies

    Please note that the above COA is a general outline and can be tailored to meet the specific needs and reporting requirements of the manufacturing business. Additionally, it's essential to consult with a qualified accountant or financial expert to ensure compliance with South African accounting standards and regulations.
