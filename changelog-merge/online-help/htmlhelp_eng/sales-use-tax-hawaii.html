    General Excise Tax / Use tax - Hawaii (HI)

    Hawaii (HI) - Tax system key points

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, here are some key points about the tax system in Hawaii:

    1. General Excise Tax (GET): Hawaii has a unique tax called the General Excise Tax (GET). It is a broad-based tax imposed on business activities rather than on sales to consumers. The GET is applied to almost all business transactions, including sales of goods and services, and is levied at different rates depending on the type of business activity. The statewide general excise tax rate is 4%.
    2. Transient Accommodations Tax (TAT): Hawaii imposes a Transient Accommodations Tax (TAT) on hotel and other transient accommodations, such as vacation rentals. The TAT rate is 10.25% as of my last update.
    3. Sales Tax: Hawaii does not have a traditional state-level sales tax as most other states do. Instead, it relies on the General Excise Tax, which is applied to business activities at different rates.
    4. Individual Income Tax: Hawaii levies an individual income tax on residents and non-residents who earn income in the state. The state has multiple tax brackets with rates ranging from 1.4% to 11% based on income levels.
    5. Corporate Income Tax: Hawaii imposes a corporate income tax on corporations conducting business in the state. The corporate tax rates vary depending on the company's taxable income.
    6. Property Taxes: Hawaii imposes property taxes on real property, such as land and buildings. Property tax rates are determined at the county level and can vary by location.
    7. Vehicle Taxes and Fees: Hawaii imposes various taxes and fees on motor vehicles, including a registration fee based on the vehicle's weight, an annual vehicle weight tax, and a county surcharge.
    8. Estate Tax: Hawaii has its own estate tax, which is separate from the federal estate tax. The Hawaii estate tax applies to estates with a value above a certain threshold, and the tax rates range from 10% to 20%.
    9. No Local Income Tax: Hawaii does not allow local jurisdictions to impose their own income taxes.
    10. No Use Tax: Hawaii does not have a separate use tax. The General Excise Tax applies to both sales and use of goods and services.

    It's important to note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's recommended to consult the Hawaii Department of Taxation or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to taxes in Hawaii.

    Hawaii (HI) - General Excise Tax and Use Tax

    As of ChatGPT's last update in September 2021, Hawaii imposes a general excise tax (GET) instead of a traditional sales tax. The Hawaii General Excise Tax is a broad-based tax that applies to various business activities, including the sale of goods and services. Here are some key points about the Hawaii General Excise Tax:

    1. General Excise Tax Rate: The Hawaii General Excise Tax rate is not a fixed percentage like a traditional sales tax. Instead, there are different rates depending on the type of business activity. As of my last update, the standard tax rates are as follows:
      • 4% on the retail sale of tangible personal property and certain services.
      • 0.5% on wholesale transactions.
      • 0.15% on insurance commissions.
      • 4.5% on businesses engaged in certain activities, such as the sale of alcohol for consumption on the premises or selling rental motor vehicles.
    1. Island Districts: Hawaii's excise tax rates may vary within different island districts. For example, the county of Honolulu has an additional surcharge on the general excise tax, making the total rate higher in that district.
    2. Exemptions: Some items and transactions may be exempt from the Hawaii General Excise Tax. However, the GET is generally broader in scope than traditional sales taxes, so many business activities are subject to taxation.
    3. Filing and Payment: Businesses are required to register for a GET license with the Hawaii Department of Taxation and report their gross receipts regularly. The filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, or semi-annually) is based on the amount of gross receipts generated by the business.
    4. Use Tax: Unlike many other states, Hawaii does not have a separate use tax. Instead, the GET applies to both sales and the use of tangible personal property and services.
    5. Online Sales: Hawaii has adopted economic nexus laws for remote sellers. This means that businesses with a certain level of sales or transactions in the state, even if they have no physical presence there, may be required to register for a GET license and collect and remit the tax.

    Please note that tax laws are subject to change, and it's essential to consult the Hawaii Department of Taxation or a tax professional for the most up-to-date and specific information related to the General Excise Tax in Hawaii.
