    Selling prices

     Selling price descriptions in osFinancials

    Price agreement plugin - Manual - Shop - Licence : Once-off - 

    In addition to the standard 3 selling prices in osFinancials, Price agreement plugin, allows you to set up to five (5) levels of quantity discounts that can be applied to specific debtors or groups of debtors. Additionally, you can set a special price for individual debtors per product. These prices will overrule the default Selling price 1 / 2 / 3 and can be set per debtor (customer / client). The price agreement may also be activated for creditors (suppliers / vendors).

    Global selling prices

    osFinancials supports up to 3 selling prices (i.e. Selling price 1, Selling price 2 and Selling price 3). You may change the descriptions of these selling prices on the "Selling price descriptions" section of the Default tab of Setup â†’ Stock information to suit your specific requirements and set one of these Selling prices as the default Selling price (global setting) for your Set of Books. 

    These selling price descriptions will be displayed in the osFinancials interface, stock items and reports in the following options:

    • Stock item grid and stock item form 
    • Documents - when processing sales documents (i.e. invoices, credit notes and quotes), these selling price descriptions may be used to select a different selling price than the default selling price. 

    Default selling price for debtors

    In addition to this, you may set one of these Selling prices as the default Selling price on the Accounting information tab for specific debtor (customer / client) accounts. 


    Examples of Selling price descriptions

    Selling price descriptions in osFinancials can vary based on the specific needs and industry of the user. These descriptions are typically used to differentiate between different pricing tiers, discounts, or special offers. Here are some examples of selling price descriptions:

    1. Standard Price: Regular selling price without any discounts or promotions.
    2. Wholesale Price: The price offered to wholesale buyers or bulk purchasers.
    3. Retail Price: The price at which the product is sold to end consumers through retail channels.

    Here are some more examples of selling price descriptions:

    1. Promotional Price: A temporarily reduced price for a specific period or promotion.
    2. Member Price: Exclusive pricing for members or subscribers to a loyalty program.
    3. Contract Price: Agreed-upon price for products or services under a contractual agreement.
    4. Volume Discount Price: Price reduction based on the quantity purchased, encouraging bulk buying.
    5. Seasonal Price: Pricing that varies according to seasons or specific times of the year.
    6. Bundle Price: Combined pricing for a package or bundle of related products.
    7. Clearance Price: Heavily discounted price to clear out excess or outdated inventory.
    8. Special Event Price: Pricing for special events or occasions, often with limited-time offers.
    9. Preferred Customer Price: Discounted price for preferred or loyal customers.
    10. Tiered Pricing: Differentiated prices based on customer tiers, such as bronze, silver, and gold levels.

    These descriptions are just examples, and users can customize them according to their business requirements. It's important to choose descriptions that make it easy for users to understand and select the appropriate selling price for each inventory item.
