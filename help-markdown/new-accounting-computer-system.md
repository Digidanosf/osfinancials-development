# Computerised system

***

In a computerized accounting system, financial transactions are entered and processed using accounting software such as osFinancials5/TurboCASH5. The system allows for the pre-configuration of the accounting database and includes processes for journal or batch processing, sales documents (quotes, invoices, and credit notes), and purchase documents (orders, purchases, and supplier returns). Transactions are captured and processed in the software, which automatically updates the relevant records when transactions are posted to the ledger.

The basic processes in a computerised system involves the following steps:

| ***Step*** | ***Process*** | ***Description*** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| *&#49;.*&nbsp; | ***Input*** | *Identify the transaction from source documents, such as a sale, purchase, payment, or receipt of funds, and enter it into the accounting software using a keyboard and mouse. osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 provides specific forms and templates to guide the user in entering the required information for transactions.*&nbsp; *To capture transactions in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, there are two options available: journals or batches, and documents.* [***Journals or Batches***](<batches-journals.md>)*: osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 allows users to capture transactions using journals or batches. Batch types can be preconfigured to provide specific options that minimize errors during transaction entry. This means that different types of transactions can be grouped together in a batch for easier processing and tracking.* [***Documents***](<documents.md>)*: osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 also supports the creation and processing of sales documents (such as [quotes*](<quotes.md>)*, [invoices*](<invoices.md>)*, and [credit notes*](<credit-notes.md>)*) and purchase documents (such as [orders*](<orders.md>)*, [purchases*](<purchases.md>)*, and [supplier returns*](<supplier-returns.md>)*). Users can enter the required information for these documents using specific forms and templates provided by osFinancials5/TurboCASH5. Additionally, users have the option to print a source document using document layout files, which allows for easy documentation and record-keeping.* *Both options aim to simplify the process of capturing transactions by providing structured forms, templates, and preconfigured batch types. This helps ensure that the required information is entered accurately and efficiently into the accounting software.* |
| *&#50;.* | ***Process*** | *Once the transactions are entered or captured in the respective journals or documents using osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, the transaction data is automatically stored in the system. Users have the option to review and check these journals and documents and make any necessary edits or modifications.* *After the transactions are captured and reviewed, the next step is to balance the journals. Balancing the journals ensures that the debits and credits of each transaction match and that the accounting equation remains in balance.* *Once the journals are balanced, the processed sales documents (quotes, invoices, credit notes) and purchase documents (orders, purchases, supplier returns) are processed. During this processing, the relevant accounts in the general ledger and subsidiary ledgers, such as the debtor's ledger and creditor's ledger, are automatically updated.* *In addition to updating the financial accounts, the computerized accounting system, like osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, also automatically updates the stock (inventory) records when processing documents. This means that the stock levels and values are adjusted based on the transactions related to purchases, sales, returns, and any other inventory-related activities.* *By automatically updating these records, the computerized accounting system ensures that the financial and inventory information is accurate and reflects the current state of the business. It eliminates the need for manual calculations and reduces the risk of errors or discrepancies in the records.* |
| *&#51;.* | ***Output*** | *After the transactions have been processed and posted to the ledger, osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 automatically updates all applicable records. This ensures that the financial information is up to date and accurately reflects the transactions that have taken place.* *Once the updates are made, osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 provides various outputs and reports that can be easily accessed by the user. These reports can be generated with just a few keystrokes or mouse clicks, making it convenient and efficient to obtain the necessary information. Some of the available reports include:* [***Trial balance***](<ledger-analyser-trial-balance.md>)*: This report summarizes the balances of all accounts in the general ledger, showing the total debits and credits for each account. It helps in verifying that the accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) is in balance.* [***Income statement***](<ledger-analyser-income-statement.md>)*: Also known as the profit and loss statement, this report shows the revenues, expenses, and net income or net loss for a specific period. It provides an overview of the company's financial performance.* [***Balance sheet***](<ledger-analyser-balance-sheet.md>)*: This report presents the financial position of the business at a specific point in time. It includes the assets, liabilities, and equity of the company, giving a snapshot of its financial health.* [***Debtor statements***](<debtor-statements.md>)*: These statements provide details of outstanding customer balances, including invoices, payments, and any outstanding amounts. They help in monitoring and managing accounts receivable.* [***Creditor remittance advises***](<creditor-remittance-advices.md>)*: These reports provide information about amounts owed to suppliers, including invoices, payments, and any outstanding balances. They assist in managing accounts payable.* *These are just a few examples of the reports that osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 offers. There may be additional options and customization features available within the software to cater to specific reporting requirements or preferences.* ***Spreadsheets** - In addition to generating reports within the osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 software, you have the option to export certain reports and data grids to spreadsheets for further analysis or sharing. Here are some key points regarding exporting reports and data from osFinancials:* ***Reports**: Reports such as the [Trial balance*](<ledger-analyser-trial-balance.md>)*, [Income statement*](<ledger-analyser-income-statement.md>) *and [Balance sheet*](<ledger-analyser-balance-sheet.md>) *can be exported to spreadsheet formats like Excel or CSV (Comma-Separated Values) files. This allows you to manipulate the data, create custom charts or graphs, and perform additional calculations as needed.* ***Data grids**: Within the software, there are grids that display information on various modules, including [Debtors*](<debtor-filter-search-options.md>)*, [Creditors*](<creditor-filter-search-options.md>)*, [Documents*](<documents-grid.md>) *and [Stock (Inventory*](<stock-items-grid.md>)*). These grids provide a tabular view of the data, and you have the ability to apply specific filters to refine the displayed information based on your requirements.* ***Filtering and exporting**: Within the data grids, you can apply filters to display specific subsets of data that match your criteria. Once you have applied the desired filters and have the relevant data displayed, you can export the filtered data to a spreadsheet file. This export functionality allows you to work with the data in spreadsheet software for further analysis, reporting, or integration with other systems.* ***Spreadsheet compatibility**: The exported data is typically compatible with popular spreadsheet software like Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets, or other similar applications. This ensures that you can seamlessly import the exported data into these tools without any compatibility issues.* *By exporting reports and data grids to spreadsheet files, you have the flexibility to analyse and manipulate the information further based on your specific needs. This enables you to perform customized calculations, create visualizations, or combine the data with other sources for comprehensive reporting and decision-making purposes.* [***Pivot tables***](<ledger-analyser-pivot.md>)&nbsp; osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 provides its own built-in pivot table functionality, allowing users to view and analyse reports such as the [Trial Balance, Income statement, Balance sheet](<ledger-analyser-pivot.md>), and [Budgets](<budgets-pivot-tab.md>) directly within the software. This means that you don't necessarily need to export the reports to a spreadsheet to utilize pivot tables. *By using the pivot table feature within osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, you can perform dynamic analysis and customization on the data directly in the software. You can define rows, columns, values, and filters within the pivot table to organize and summarize the data according to your specific needs. This enables you to gain insights, identify patterns, and make informed decisions based on the financial information presented in these reports.* *Furthermore, if you choose to export the reports to a spreadsheet, you can still leverage the pivot table functionality within the spreadsheet software to further analyse and manipulate the data. This provides you with the flexibility to use either osFinancials5/TurboCASH5' built-in pivot tables or the pivot table features of your preferred spreadsheet software, depending on your preferences and requirements.* *In summary, osFinancials offers its own pivot table functionality for viewing and analyzing reports, and if desired, you can also export the reports to a spreadsheet to utilize the pivot table features of the spreadsheet software. Both options allow you to perform dynamic and customizable analysis on your financial data.* |
| *&#52;.* | ***Backups*** | *It is important to safeguard the books of account and supporting documents from unauthorized access, fire, theft, and other risks. Computerized accounting systems often offer features like automatic backups, data encryption, and audit trails to enhance data security and reliability.* *Safeguarding the books of account and supporting documents is crucial to protect sensitive financial information and ensure business continuity. Here are some key considerations:* ***Unauthorized access**: Implement proper access controls to restrict access to the accounting system and sensitive data. Use strong passwords, user permissions, and authentication mechanisms to prevent unauthorized individuals from accessing the system.* ***Fire and theft**: Store physical documents and backup files in a secure location that is protected from fire and theft risks. This could include using fireproof safes or off-site storage facilities with appropriate security measures.* ***Backup files**: Regularly create backups of your accounting database and store them in a safe external location. This could involve using external hard drives, cloud storage, or backup services. Ensure that backups are performed consistently and verify their integrity periodically.* ***Disaster recovery plan**: Develop a comprehensive disaster recovery plan that outlines procedures for restoring the accounting system in the event of unforeseen circumstances. This plan should include steps for recovering data from backup files and restoring the system to a functional state.* ***Test backups and restoration**: Periodically test the backup files and restoration process to ensure they are working correctly. This helps identify any issues or discrepancies in the backup files and allows for adjustments or improvements to be made.* ***Data encryption**: Consider encrypting sensitive financial data to add an extra layer of protection. Encryption ensures that even if unauthorized individuals gain access to the data, they won't be able to decipher or use it without the encryption key.* *By implementing these measures, you can enhance the security and integrity of your accounting system and be prepared to recover quickly in the event of any unforeseen circumstances or data loss.* |


Overall, the computerised accounting system is faster, more efficient, and less prone to errors than a manual system. It can handle a large number of transactions and provide real-time updates on the financial status of the business.&nbsp;
