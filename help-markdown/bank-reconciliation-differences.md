# Steps to resolve differences in the Bank reconciliation

If there are differences between the bank statement and the company's records during the bank reconciliation process, the following steps can be taken to resolve them:

* **Verify the accuracy of both sets of records**: Check the company's records and the bank statement to make sure that they are accurate and up-to-date.
* **Check for errors**: Look for any errors or mistakes that may have been made in recording transactions. For example, a transaction may have been recorded twice, or an incorrect amount may have been entered.
* **Investigate outstanding transactions**: Outstanding transactions, such as checks that have not yet cleared the bank, may need to be investigated to determine the cause of the difference.
* **Contact the bank**: If the cause of the difference cannot be determined, contact the bank to request more information about the transactions in question.
* **Make adjustments**: Once the cause of the difference has been identified, adjustments can be made to correct the records. For example, if an outstanding check is causing the difference, it can be subtracted from the company's records.
* **Reconcile again**: After making any necessary adjustments, reconcile the bank statement and the company's records again to ensure that the difference has been resolved.

It is important to resolve any differences in the bank reconciliation process as quickly as possible to ensure that the company's financial records are accurate and up-to-date.

# Examples causing differences

A few examples that can possibly cause a difference:

* Your closing bank balance is incorrectly entered on the *"Reconcile Bank account"* screen (**Reconciliation** on the **Default** ribbon).
* Incorrect date entered ("*Include Items until*" field) on "*Bank account recon options"* screen (**Reconciliation** on the **Default** ribbon).
* Errors in posting - they should have been detected when your bank reconciliation was done.
* If everything else fails you may sometimes have to look at your Cash-book and link up the source documents to check if you have entered all your transactions correctly. You could also have posted an amount incorrectly e.g. as 64.00 and entered it as 46.00. &nbsp;

You may need to print the posted batch transactions for the period on the following options to trace the errors:&nbsp;

* Batch transactions per period report for the Payments and Receipt Batch and the (**Reports → Batch** **entry**) (select the period of your Bank reconciliation).&nbsp;
* Batch entry for the Payments batch and/or Receipts batch (**Batch** **entry - Posted batches** tab). Select the correct batch and double-click on it. Click on the **Print** button. &nbsp;
