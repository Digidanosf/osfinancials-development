# Subsistence and travel allowances and advances

***

# What methods are commonly used for subsistence and travel allowances and advances and recording of the transactions?

Subsistence and travel allowances are common forms of employee reimbursements for expenses incurred during business travel or other work-related activities. Advances may also be provided to employees to cover anticipated expenses prior to travel. Here are some methods that are commonly used for subsistence and travel allowances and advances, as well as the recording of these transactions:

1. **Per diem rates**: One common method for calculating subsistence allowances is to use per diem rates. These rates are set by the company or government agency and represent a fixed amount that employees can claim for meals, lodging, and incidental expenses while on business travel. Per diem rates are often set based on the location of the travel and can vary depending on the time of year.
1. **Receipt-based reimbursement**: Another common method is to reimburse employees for actual expenses incurred during business travel. In this method, employees must provide receipts or other documentation for expenses such as meals, lodging, transportation, and other expenses incurred during travel. The company then reimburses the employee for the actual expenses, up to a pre-determined limit.
1. **Advances**: Companies may also provide advances to employees to cover anticipated expenses during business travel. These advances are typically reconciled against actual expenses incurred by the employee, with any excess returned to the company and any shortfall repaid by the employee.
1. **Recording transactions**: The recording of subsistence and travel allowances and advances typically involves creating a separate account for each type of expense in the company's chart of accounts. These accounts may include categories such as travel, lodging, meals, and incidentals. Transactions related to subsistence and travel allowances and advances are recorded in these accounts, with appropriate documentation attached to support each transaction.

In addition to these methods, many companies use expense management software to automate the process of tracking and reimbursing employee expenses. These software systems can help reduce errors and streamline the reimbursement process, saving time and improving accuracy.

# Is Travel \& Subsistence an expense account and which tariffs apply in different countries?

Yes, "*Travel \& Subsistence*" is typically recorded as an expense account in the chart of accounts. It represents the costs incurred by employees for business-related travel, including transportation, accommodation, meals, and other related expenses.

Regarding tariffs or rates applicable to travel and subsistence expenses, it varies depending on the country and its specific regulations. Each country may have its own tax laws, guidelines, and reimbursement rates for travel and subsistence expenses.

For example, in the United States, the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) provides guidelines and reimbursement rates known as the "*per diem rates*" for various locations. These rates specify the maximum amount that can be reimbursed for lodging, meals, and incidental expenses without the need for detailed receipts.

Similarly, other countries may have their own tax authorities or government agencies that set guidelines and rates for travel and subsistence expenses. These rates may vary based on factors such as location, duration of travel, and the purpose of the trip.

It is essential to consult the tax laws and regulations of the specific country in question or seek guidance from a qualified tax professional to determine the applicable tariffs or rates for travel and subsistence expenses.
