# What is provisional tax?

Provisional tax is a system used in many countries to collect income tax from individuals and businesses in a timely manner, rather than waiting until the end of the financial year. It is a method for taxpayers to make payments towards their income tax liability in advance, usually in multiple installments throughout the year.

The purpose of provisional tax is to ensure that taxpayers make timely payments towards their eventual income tax liability, thus preventing a large tax burden from accumulating at the end of the tax year. This is particularly important for individuals and businesses with fluctuating or irregular incomes, as well as for self-employed individuals and freelancers who do not have taxes withheld from their earnings like traditional employees do.

The system generally works as follows:

1. **Estimation**: Taxpayers are required to estimate their total income for the current tax year and calculate their expected tax liability. This can include income from various sources such as business profits, investments, rental income, etc.
1. **Installments**: Based on this estimated tax liability, taxpayers are required to make regular payments, often in the form of installments, throughout the tax year. These installments are usually due on specific dates during the year.
1. **Adjustments**: At the end of the tax year, when the actual income and deductions are known, taxpayers reconcile their estimated tax payments with their actual tax liability. If they have overpaid, they may receive a refund. If they have underpaid, they may need to pay the remaining tax balance.

It's important to note that the rules and procedures for provisional tax can vary significantly from one country to another. Some countries have complex systems with different payment schedules and methods for calculating the estimated tax, while others have simpler systems. Taxpayers are usually required to keep accurate records of their income, expenses, and estimated tax payments to ensure compliance with the provisional tax requirements.

If you need specific information about provisional tax in a particular country, it's advisable to consult the tax authority or a tax professional in that jurisdiction.

# In which countries is provisional tax applicable?

Provisional tax systems are used in various countries around the world to collect income tax payments in advance. However, the specific rules, regulations, and terminology may differ from one country to another. Here are some countries where provisional tax or similar concepts are applicable:

1. **New Zealand**: New Zealand has a well-known provisional tax system. Individuals and businesses with a certain level of income are required to make regular payments towards their income tax liability based on their estimated tax for the year.
1. **Australia**: Australia has a Pay As You Go (PAYG) system that functions similarly to provisional tax. Under PAYG, individuals and businesses make regular tax payments throughout the year to cover their expected tax liability.
1. **South Africa**: In South Africa, provisional tax is paid by individuals and businesses that earn income that is not subject to employees' tax (PAYE). Taxpayers make two or three installment payments based on their estimated tax liability.
1. **Canada**: Canada has a system called "*installment payments*", where individuals with substantial tax liabilities are required to make regular payments throughout the year to cover their income tax liability.
1. *United States*: While the United States doesn't have a formal provisional tax system, the estimated tax payment requirement serves a similar purpose. Individuals who expect to owe a certain amount of tax that isn't covered by withholding need to make quarterly estimated tax payments.
1. **United Kingdom**: The United Kingdom has a system called "*Payments on Account*" for self-employed individuals and certain other taxpayers. This requires making advance payments toward the upcoming year's tax liability.
1. **India**: India has a system of Advance Tax, where taxpayers are required to pay their tax liability in installments if their tax liability exceeds a certain threshold. This applies to individuals, companies, and firms.
1. **Ireland**: Ireland's Pay As You Earn (PAYE) system for income tax includes a "*Preliminary End of Year Statement*" (PEOYS) process, which allows taxpayers to make payments towards their expected tax liability before the end of the tax year.
1. **Singapore**: In Singapore, individuals who have non-salary income are required to make regular payments called "*Estimated Chargeable Income*" (ECI) to cover their potential tax liability.

These are just a few examples, and the specific details of each country's provisional tax system can vary widely. It's important to refer to the tax authority or consult with a tax professional in the specific country to understand the exact rules and requirements related to provisional tax or similar concepts.
