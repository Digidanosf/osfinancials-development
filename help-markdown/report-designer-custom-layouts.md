# Designing custom layout files for documents

When designing custom layout files for documents such as invoices or any other types of documents in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 **Report designer** (**Setup** ribbon), it's crucial to consider the requirements of your tax jurisdiction. This includes not only the content of the document but also its appearance and formatting.

Here are a few points to keep in mind when designing custom layout files for documents, especially invoices, while considering tax jurisdiction requirements:

1. **Document Name**: The name of the document may need to include specific terms like "*Invoice*","*Tax Invoice*" or "*VAT Invoice*," based on the requirements of your tax jurisdiction. Ensure that the document name aligns with the regulations.&nbsp;
1. **Mandatory Information**: Different tax jurisdictions may require specific information to be present on invoices, such as your business details, customer details, itemised list of products or services, quantities, prices, applicable taxes, and total amount. Make sure all the necessary information is included and properly formatted.
1. **Tax Details**: Invoices should clearly display tax-related details, including the tax rate, tax amount, and any tax identification numbers required by your tax authority.
1. **Currency**: If you conduct transactions in multiple currencies, consider including the currency in which the transaction is conducted.
1. **Formatting**: Ensure that the layout is clear, well-organized, and easy to read. Follow any specific formatting guidelines prescribed by your tax jurisdiction.
1. **Localisation**: If your business operates in multiple countries or regions with different tax regulations, consider creating different layouts or templates tailored to each jurisdiction's requirements.
1. **Logo and Branding**: Your company logo and branding should still be prominently displayed on the layout, maintaining your professional identity.
1. **Language**: If required, provide translations or multilingual options to accommodate different languages spoken in your region.
1. **Accessibility**: Ensure that the layout is accessible to people with disabilities, complying with accessibility standards if applicable.
1. **Testing and Adjusting**: Ensure to thoroughly test the document layout file by printing it and generating it in PDF format. This helps to verify the alignment and presentation of the content. If needed, make adjustments to ensure the layout appears as intended and meets the necessary requirements.

By carefully considering the tax jurisdiction requirements and integrating them into your document layout design, you can help ensure that your invoices and other documents meet legal and regulatory standards while maintaining a professional and consistent appearance for your business communications.
