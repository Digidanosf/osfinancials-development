# Accounting equation - Balance sheet

***

In accounting, the balance sheet typically reflects three main types of accounts: proprietary accounts, asset accounts, and liability accounts. These accounts provide a snapshot of the business's worth on a specific date, by showing the value of assets held (such as motor vehicles, stock, equipment and machinery), monies owed, and the capital and loan accounts of the owners. The balance sheet is an important financial statement that provides a summary of a company's financial position, and helps stakeholders make informed decisions about investing, lending, and doing business with the company.

The balance sheet is a financial statement that presents a list of all the assets owned by the business and how these assets are financed. It provides a snapshot of the financial position of the business at a specific point in time.

The sources used to finance the assets of the business are listed on the balance sheet under the categories of owner's equity and liabilities. Owner's equity represents the capital invested by the owner in the business, while liabilities represent amounts owed by the business to third parties. Together, owner's equity and liabilities are referred to as the "*sources of financing*" or "*capital employed*".

The assets that the business owns are listed on the balance sheet as the "*employment of capital*". These assets are financed by the capital and liabilities of the business. The balance sheet provides a clear picture of the financial health of the business, by showing the relationship between its assets and the sources used to finance those assets.
