# Customising terminology for VAT/GST/Sales tax

***

The [language associated with each Set of Books](<set-default-language.md>) determines the specific tax labels used to cater to different countries or tax regulations. For instance, en-UK and en-South Africa use terminology related to VAT, while in South Africa, Afrikaans refers to BTW. Australia and New Zealand use GST-related terminology, whereas the United States and Canada employ Sales tax terms.

These tax-related labels are visible within the osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 interface when you access a Set of Books. They are also present in reports and document layout files generated by the system.

To adapt the tax terminology to suit your country, jurisdiction, and industry, you can customize and modify the tax-related labels via the *"Customize Languages*" screen. This screen is accessible through the **Tools → [Customise Language**](<customise-language.md>) menu on the **Setup** ribbon.

You may also find it helpful to utilize the search options available. This enables you to search for a specific label either in the currently active language file you're editing or in the default "*English*" language file. This assists you in identifying and modifying specific text to better suit your requirements.
