# What types of discounts are there?

There are several types of discounts that businesses may offer to customers, including:

1. **Cash discount**: A discount offered to customers who pay for their purchases in cash, rather than using credit or another form of payment.
1. **Trade discount**: A discount offered to customers who are part of a particular trade or industry. Trade discounts are often used to encourage bulk purchases or to incentivize repeat business.
1. **Quantity discount**: A discount offered to customers who purchase a large quantity of goods or services. Quantity discounts are often used to incentivize bulk purchases or to clear inventory.
1. **Seasonal discount**: A discount offered during a particular season or time of year, often to clear out inventory or to encourage sales during a slow period.
1. **Promotional discount**: A discount offered as part of a marketing or promotional campaign, often to introduce a new product or to encourage customers to try a product or service.
1. **Early payment discount**: A discount offered to customers who pay their bills early or before the due date. Early payment discounts are often used to incentivize prompt payment and improve cash flow.
1. **Loyalty discount**: A discount offered to customers who have been loyal to a business or who have made repeat purchases over a certain period of time. Loyalty discounts are often used to incentivize customer retention and build brand loyalty.

The specific types of discounts offered by a business may vary depending on the industry, the target customer base, and the overall business strategy.

# What is the difference between trade discounts and cash discounts?

Trade discounts and cash discounts are two different types of discounts that businesses may offer or receive.

**Trade discounts** are discounts that are offered by a supplier to a customer based on the quantity or volume of goods purchased. Trade discounts are not typically recorded as a separate transaction in accounting.
