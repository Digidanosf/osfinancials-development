# Year-end procedures

***

Depending on the accounting basis (cash basis or accrual basis) on which your accounting records or system is based, you may need to do specific adjustments before you do the year-end procedures in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5.

Examples of some of the adjustments based on the accrual accounting basis are; provision for bad debts, prepaid expenses, accrued expenses, provision for accrued expenses, income received in advance, and accrued income. Most of these adjustments are short-term adjustments, where these expenses or income relate to the next successive accounting period or financial year.

However, some adjustments may relate to the long term where the income or expenses are for more than one financial year. A good example is depreciation on fixed assets where the amounts are calculated over the life span of the assets and are apportioned over more than one year.

# Steps for the year-end process

Some general steps for the year-end process in accounting:

1. **Reconcile accounts**: Reconcile all accounts, including bank accounts, accounts receivable, accounts payable, and inventory. This involves reviewing all transactions and ensuring that the account balances match the corresponding statements.
1. **Adjust entries**: Make any necessary adjusting entries to ensure that the accounts reflect the correct balances. This includes accruals, deferrals, and any other necessary adjustments.
1. **Perform inventory valuation**: Perform a physical inventory count and reconcile the results with the accounting records. This is necessary to determine the actual value of inventory at the end of the year.
1. **Review fixed assets**: Review the fixed assets to ensure that all items are properly accounted for and that depreciation schedules are accurate.
1. **Review accounts receivable and payable**: Review the accounts receivable and payable to ensure that all invoices have been paid and all payments have been made.
1. **Prepare financial statements**: Prepare financial statements, including the balance sheet, income statement, and statement of cash flows, for the year. These statements should be reviewed for accuracy and completeness.
1. **Perform a year-end audit**: Perform an audit of the financial statements to ensure that they comply with accounting standards and regulations. This is typically performed by an external auditor.
1. **Prepare tax returns**: Prepare tax returns and ensure compliance with tax regulations. This may involve working with a tax professional to ensure that all deductions and credits are properly accounted for.
1. **Close the books**: Close the books for the year, which involves finalizing all transactions and updating account balances. This ensures that the accounting records are up-to-date and accurate.

These are just some general steps for the year-end process. The specific steps may vary depending on the organization and its accounting procedures.

# Year-End Procedures

After processing all your daily transactions for a specific year, you have to check the results and ensure that certain actions have been performed before the Year-end procedure can be performed.

It is important to develop a checklist to meet your specific requirements. This will ensure that you generate useful reports and that you evaluate your business performance and financial position for a specific period on correct information.

The following represent a basic list and may vary according to your circumstances:&nbsp;

1. All batches (journals) have been updated (posted) to the ledger.
1. All documents, invoices, credit notes and supplier returns) have been updated (posted) to the ledger.
1. Depreciation transactions have been entered and updated (posted) to the ledger.
1. Bad debts have been have written off and updated (posted) to the ledger.
1. Provision for bad debts or doubtful debts have been created or adjusted and updated (posted) to the ledger.
1. Payroll (Salaries and Wages) transactions have been entered and updated (posted) to the ledger.
1. Repeating transactions and Repeating invoices have been updated (posted) to the ledger.
1. Adjustments and Corrections have been entered and updated (posted) to the ledger.
1. Bank Reconciliation is finalised.
1. Interest on overdue debtors accounts have been entered and updated (posted) to the ledger.
1. Generate any Multiple Entries (Fixed amounts to debtor accounts, Debtors with charge amount, and Creditors with charge amount, etc.).
1. Print Reports as necessary, e.g. trial balance, income statements and balance sheets and any other reports. You need to analyse these reports and do the necessary adjustments or corrections, if necessary.
1. Print debtors statements and creditor remittance advises.
1. Create sufficient Backups of your Set of Books.

Adjustments are those transactions that occur, which usually do not involve a third party. These adjustments are usually made at the end of a financial year to adjust certain accounts to reflect the accounting policy of the business entity or to comply with the principles of the Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) or International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS).
