# Common Errors in Accounting

There are several common errors that can occur in accounting, including:

1. **Data entry errors**: These errors occur when transactions are entered incorrectly, such as transposing numbers or recording the wrong amount.
1. **Timing errors**: These errors occur when transactions are recorded in the wrong period, such as recording a transaction in the wrong month or year.
1. **Omission errors**: These errors occur when a transaction is not recorded at all, such as forgetting to record a sale or purchase.
1. **Reversal errors**: These errors occur when debits and credits are reversed, such as debiting an account that should be credited.
1. **Overstatement or understatement errors**: These errors occur when transactions are recorded with the wrong amount, such as overstating revenue or understating expenses.
1. **Duplication errors**: These errors occur when a transaction is recorded twice, resulting in an overstatement of assets or income.
1. **Incorrect account errors**: These errors occur when a transaction is recorded in the wrong account, resulting in misstatements in financial statements.

It is important for businesses to have internal controls in place to prevent these common errors from occurring. Regular review and reconciliation of accounts can help identify and correct any errors that do occur. It is also important to have well-trained accounting staff and to use reliable accounting software, such as osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 to minimize the risk of errors.

# What is the general journal used for?

The general journal is a book or computer program used in accounting to record all transactions in chronological order. It is also known as the "journal" or "journal entry book".

Each transaction recorded in the general journal includes a date, a brief description of the transaction, the accounts involved, and the amounts debited or credited. These transactions are then later transferred to the appropriate ledgers for posting and summarization.

The general journal is used to record a wide range of transactions, such as sales, purchases, expenses, and other financial transactions. It is also used to record adjusting entries and closing entries at the end of an accounting period.

By recording all transactions in the general journal, a business can keep a complete and accurate record of its financial activities. This information is then used to prepare financial statements and to analyze the financial performance of the business. The general journal is an essential tool for any business that wants to maintain accurate financial records and make informed decisions about its financial activities.

* **Sales**: For recording sales, specialised journals like the sales journal should be used. The sales journal allows you to enter and manage sales-related transactions effectively.

In addition to the sales journal, you can process [invoices](<documents-setup-invoices.md>) and [credit notes](<documents-setup-credit-notes.md>) using the **Documents** section on the **Default** ribbon. [Invoices](<documents-setup-invoices.md>) and [credit notes](<documents-setup-credit-notes.md>) can be linked to the sales journal in **Setup → [Documents setup**](<documents-setup.md>) (**Setup** ribbon). These documents can be printed and reprinted as needed, enabling you to send them to your debtors (customers/clients) as required.

Once the invoices and credit notes are updated and posted to the ledger, the corresponding transactions will be automatically processed in the sales journal. Each item included in the invoice or credit note will be recorded in the sales journal, ensuring accurate tracking of your sales transactions.
