# Sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for a South African mixed Retail and Service business?

For a South African mixed retail and service business, the Chart of Accounts (COA) needs to reflect the unique financial transactions and reporting requirements of both retail and service operations. Here's a sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for such a business:

1. Assets&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;.1 Current Assets

    - &#49;.1.1 Cash on Hand&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.2 Cash in Bank&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.3 Petty Cash&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.4 Accounts Receivable (Trade Debtors for retail sales and service invoices)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.5 Inventory (Retail merchandise for resale)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.6 Prepaid Expenses&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.7 Other Current Assets

  - &#49;.2 Non-Current Assets&nbsp;

    - &#49;.2.1 Property and Buildings (Store locations, office space, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.2 Land&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.3 Furniture and Fixtures&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.4 Equipment (Cash registers, service tools, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.5 Vehicles (if used for delivery or service)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.6 Intangible Assets (Software, licences, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.7 Other Non-Current Assets

2. Liabilities&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#50;.1 Current Liabilities&nbsp;

    - &#50;.1.1 Accounts Payable (Outstanding expenses, bills, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.2 Accrued Liabilities (e.g., Salaries payable, utilities)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.3 Short-Term Loans and Borrowings (if applicable)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.4 Credit Card Payables 2.1.5 Other Current Liabilities

  - &#50;.2 Non-Current Liabilities&nbsp;

    - &#50;.2.1 Long-Term Loans and Borrowings (if applicable)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.2.2 Lease Liabilities&nbsp;
    - &#50;.2.3 Other Non-Current Liabilities

3. Equity&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#51;.1 Share Capital&nbsp;

    - &#51;.1.1 Ordinary Shares&nbsp;
    - &#51;.1.2 Preference Shares&nbsp;

  - &#51;.2 Retained Earnings&nbsp;
  - &#51;.3 Other Equity Items

4. Income&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#52;.1 Retail Sales Revenue (Income from merchandise sales)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.2 Service Revenue (Income from providing services)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.3 Project Revenue (if applicable, for project-based services)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.4 Discounts and Allowances (Sales discounts, returns)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.5 Interest Income (e.g., from bank accounts) 4.6 Rental Income (if renting out store space)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.7 Other Income

5. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#53;.1 Beginning Inventory (for retail merchandise)&nbsp;
  - &#53;.2 Purchases (Cost of merchandise acquired for retail)&nbsp;
  - &#53;.3 Direct Labor Costs (for service operations)&nbsp;
  - &#53;.4 Freight and Shipping Costs (for retail merchandise)&nbsp;
  - &#53;.5 Ending Inventory (for retail merchandise)&nbsp;
  - &#53;.6 Cost of Services (Direct costs related to service delivery)&nbsp;
  - &#53;.7 Gross Profit

6. Expenses&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#54;.1 Salaries and Wages&nbsp;
  - &#54;.2 Rent and Lease Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#54;.3 Utilities (Electricity, Water, etc.)&nbsp;
  - &#54;.4 Insurance&nbsp;
  - &#54;.5 Advertising and Promotion&nbsp;
  - &#54;.6 Office Supplies&nbsp;
  - &#54;.7 Professional Fees (e.g., legal, consulting)&nbsp;
  - &#54;.8 Travel and Transportation Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#54;.9 Training and Professional Development&nbsp;
  - &#54;.10 Depreciation and Amortization&nbsp;
  - &#54;.11 Bank Fees and Interest Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#54;.12 Other Operating Expenses

7. Taxes&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#55;.1 Value Added Tax (VAT) Payable&nbsp;
  - &#55;.2 Pay-As-You-Earn (PAYE) Tax (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#55;.3 Other Taxes

8. Other Comprehensive Income&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#56;.1 Foreign Currency Translation Gains/Losses (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#56;.2 Other Items
