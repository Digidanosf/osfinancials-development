# What is Credit notes and Debit notes?

Credit notes and debit notes are both financial documents used in accounting to record adjustments in transactions and correct errors in invoices or billing. They are commonly used in business-to-business (B2B) transactions and can be issued by both sellers and buyers.

1. **Credit Note**: A credit note is a document issued by a seller to a buyer when there is an overcharge or an error in the original invoice. It serves to decrease the amount owed by the buyer to the seller. The reasons for issuing a credit note can vary, such as goods returned by the buyer, incorrect pricing, quantity discrepancies, or any other adjustments needed to the original invoice.

For example, if a company sells goods to a customer for $1,000 but later realizes that they overcharged by $100, they would issue a credit note to the customer for $100. The customer can then deduct this amount from their outstanding balance.

2. **Debit Note**: A debit note, on the other hand, is a document issued by a buyer to a seller when there is an undercharge or an error in the original invoice. It serves to increase the amount owed by the buyer to the seller. Like credit notes, the reasons for issuing debit notes can vary, such as the seller undercharging for goods or services, billing mistakes, or any other adjustments required.

For example, if a company receives goods from a supplier and the supplier undercharges them by $50, the buyer will issue a debit note to the supplier for $50. This additional amount will be added to the outstanding balance that the buyer owes to the supplier.

In both cases, credit notes and debit notes help in maintaining accurate and transparent accounting records, ensuring that financial transactions are correctly documented and any discrepancies are appropriately addressed. They serve as supporting documents and are often used during the process of reconciling accounts between buyers and sellers.
