# Sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for a South African NPO or NGO?

For a South African Non-Profit Organization (NPO) or Non-Governmental Organization (NGO), the Chart of Accounts (COA) will be designed to suit the specific financial transactions and reporting requirements of the organization. Here's a sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for a South African NPO or NGO:

1. Assets&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;.1 Current Assets&nbsp;

    - &#49;.1.1 Cash on Hand&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.2 Cash in Bank&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.3 Petty Cash&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.4 Accounts Receivable (Donations, Grants, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.5 Prepaid Expenses&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.6 Inventory (if applicable)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.7 Other Current Assets

  - &#49;.2 Non-Current Assets&nbsp;

    - &#49;.2.1 Property and Buildings&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.2 Land&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.3 Furniture and Fixtures&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.4 Equipment (computers, office equipment, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.5 Vehicles (if owned by the organization)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.6 Investments (if any)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.7 Other Non-Current Assets

2. Liabilities&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#50;.1 Current Liabilities&nbsp;

    - &#50;.1.1 Accounts Payable (Outstanding expenses, bills, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.2 Accrued Liabilities (e.g., Salaries payable, utility bills)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.3 Short-Term Loans and Borrowings (if applicable)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.4 Grants Payable&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.5 Other Current Liabilities

  - &#50;.2 Non-Current Liabilities&nbsp;

    - &#50;.2.1 Long-Term Loans and Borrowings (if applicable)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.2.2 Other Non-Current Liabilities

3. Net Assets&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#51;.1 Unrestricted Net Assets&nbsp;
  - &#51;.2 Temporarily Restricted Net Assets&nbsp;
  - &#51;.3 Permanently Restricted Net Assets

4. Income&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#52;.1 Donations and Contributions&nbsp;
  - &#52;.2 Grants and Funding (Government grants, foundation grants, etc.)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.3 Membership Fees&nbsp;
  - &#52;.4 Fundraising Income&nbsp;
  - &#52;.5 Interest Income&nbsp;
  - &#52;.6 Program Service Revenue (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.7 Other Income

5. Expenses&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#53;.1 Salaries and Benefits&nbsp;
  - &#53;.2 Rent and Utilities&nbsp;
  - &#53;.3 Office Supplies&nbsp;
  - &#53;.4 Program Expenses (Direct costs related to the organization's mission)&nbsp;
  - &#53;.5 Administrative Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.6 Fundraising Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.7 Outreach and Community Development Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.8 Advocacy and Awareness Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.9 Training and Capacity Building Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.10 Research and Evaluation Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.11 Other Operating Expenses

6. Taxes and Levies&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#54;.1 Value Added Tax (VAT)&nbsp;
  - &#54;.2 Pay-As-You-Earn (PAYE) Tax (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#54;.3 Other Taxes and Levies

7. Other Comprehensive Income&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#55;.1 Foreign Currency Translation Gains/Losses (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#55;.2 Other Items

8. Gains and Losses&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#56;.1 Gain on Sale of Assets (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#56;.2 Loss on Impairment of Assets (if applicable)

9. Inter-Project Transactions&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#57;.1 Transfers between Projects or Programs

10. Projects and Programs&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;0.1 Project A&nbsp;

    - &#49;0.1.1 Income&nbsp;
    - &#49;0.1.2 Expenses

  - &#49;0.2 Project B&nbsp;

    - &#49;0.2.1 Income&nbsp;
    - &#49;0.2.2 Expenses&nbsp;

  - &#49;0.3 Program&nbsp;

    - C 10.3.1 Income&nbsp;
    - &#49;0.3.2 Expenses&nbsp;

  - &#49;0.4 Other Projects and Programs

11. Memberships and Subscriptions&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;1.1 Membership Fees (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#49;1.2 Subscriptions to Publications (if applicable)

12. Donor-Restricted Funds&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;2.1 Donations with Specific Restrictions

Please note that the above COA is a general outline and can be customized to match the specific activities, projects, and financial reporting needs of the South African NPO or NGO. It is essential to consult with a qualified accountant or financial expert to ensure compliance with South African accounting standards and regulations for non-profit organizations.
