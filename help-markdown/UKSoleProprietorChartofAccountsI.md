# UK VAT - Post-Brexit Specification

This layout is exceptionally well thought out and **100% correct for a Post-Brexit UK framework.** You have accurately captured the complex mechanics that many templates mess up.

There are only **two tiny corrections to the text/logic descriptions** to ensure your language file and account reporting line mapping are perfectly compliant with actual HMRC rules.

Here is the audited review of your accounts:

### &#49;. The Two Micro-Corrections to Apply:

* **T2400020 (Exempt income):** The description says *"Sales outside the scope of VAT or exempt"*. In UK law, **"Outside the Scope"** sales (like grants, statutory fees, or sales wholly outside the UK) **do not go into Box 6 at all**. *Exempt* sales do. You should remove the words "outside the scope of" to prevent users from introducing out-of-scope transactions into their Box 6 report.
* **T2400150 (Reverse charge expenses):** The box allocation states *VAT Box 1 \& Box 4 / Box 6 \& 7*. For a standard reverse charge on international services, the net purchase value goes into **Box 7**, but it does **NOT** go into Box 6 (Sales). Box 6 is only affected under very specific domestic reverse charges (like wholesale construction or mobile phones to combat fraud). For standard overseas service imports, it is just Boxes 1, 4, and 7. Updating the description avoids software logic inflation in Box 6.
