# Purchase Returns and Purchase Discounts&nbsp;

Purchase Returns and Purchase Discounts list of account codes or account categories used in accounting or financial management systems. These codes are typically part of a chart of accounts, which is a standardized list of accounts that a business uses to categorize its financial transactions. Each account code corresponds to a specific type of financial transaction or activity. Here's what each of these accounts represents:

1. **G5900-000 - Purchase Returns**: This account is used to track returns of goods or products that a business has purchased from its suppliers. When a business returns a purchase, it records the transaction in this account to reflect a reduction in the cost of goods purchased. Depending on your company's accounting policies and jurisdiction, you may need to [configure the default contra account if you wish to allocate supplier returns to an account different from the default purchase account](<accounts-returns-purchase-return.md>) in most Sets of Books templates available in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5.
1. **G5950-000 - Purchase Discounts**: This account is used to track discounts received by a business for early payment of its bills or invoices from suppliers. When a business takes advantage of purchase discounts by paying its bills early, it records the discount amount in this account to reflect the savings achieved.

These account codes are part of a structured accounting system and help businesses accurately record and categorize their financial transactions for reporting and analysis purposes. The specific codes and account names may vary between businesses and accounting systems, but they generally serve similar purposes across different organizations.

# Sales Allowances and Sales Discounts

Sales Allowances and Sales Discounts are part of a chart of accounts used in accounting and financial management systems. Here's what each of these accounts represents:

1. **G4800-000 - Sales Allowances**: This account is used to track allowances granted to customers for issues such as damaged goods, defects, or other reasons that lead to a reduction in the sale price. Sales allowances are essentially deductions from the total sales revenue due to concessions made to customers after a sale has been completed. Depending on your company's accounting policies and jurisdiction, you may need to [configure the default contra account if you wish to allocate credit notes to an account different from the default sales account](<accounts-returns-credit-notes.md>) in most Sets of Books templates available in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5.
1. **G4900-000 - Sales Discounts**: This account is used to record discounts offered to customers as an incentive for early payment. When a customer takes advantage of a sales discount by paying their invoice early, the discount amount is recorded in this account. Sales discounts are often expressed as a percentage of the invoice total and are intended to encourage prompt payment.

These account codes help businesses keep track of various adjustments to their sales revenue, whether it's due to allowances made to customers or discounts offered to encourage timely payment. Properly categorizing these transactions is essential for accurate financial reporting and analysis.
