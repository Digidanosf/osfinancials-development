# Budgets (Reports ribbon)

***

| ![Image](<lib/admon-new.png>) | *The following new features have been added:* *Budget figures may be entered, adjusted or processed in the [**Budgets***](<budgets-reports-ribbon.md>) *option on the [**Reports***](<budgets-reports-ribbon.md>) *ribbon. This is in addition to the standard option to process budgets in **Setup → [Accounts***](<budget-setup-accounts.md>) *(**Setup** ribbon).* *The [**Pivot***](<budgets-pivot-tab.md>) *feature is introduced to analyse your Budget figures.* *Budgets may be entered for any of the periods or financial years.*&nbsp; *Budgets may be entered for a specific date - e.g. Expected once off purchases or a specific big sale, etc.* *Budgets may be copied from Budget figures from the previous year.* *Budgets may be copied from Actual figures from the previous year.* *Budgets may be entered for [Cost centres*](<cost-centres.md>) *(Cost centre 1 and/or Cost centre 2), if activated.* *Budgets may be entered for [Projects*](<projects.md>)*, if activated.* |
| --- | --- |


**To process your Budget figures:**

1. On the **Reports** ribbon, select **Budgets**. The **Budget** tab will be displayed.
1. **Period** - Select a period on the **Per period** tab. You may click on the **Free selection** tab to select a specific date or a range of dates to include budget figures.&nbsp; Select the period “Year” (i.e. “*2024/03/01*” to “*2025,/02/28*") to enter your budget figures for the selected period.

![Image](<lib/budget-01.png>)

3. Click on the **Refresh** button to populate the accounts and periods. This will list all the existing ledger accounts in your “*Chart of accounts*” (existing as added, edited in the **Setup → Accounts** (**Setup** ribbon)), in the Account code sequence.&nbsp;

| ![Image](<lib/admon-tip.png>) | *If you already have existing budget figures in the previous year, you may use the “Copy – From Budget Previous year” option.*&nbsp; &nbsp; *If you have already posted transactions in batches and documents in the previous year, you may use the “Copy – From Actual Previous year” option to use the transaction totals as basis for your budget.* &nbsp; |
| --- | --- |


4. **Reference** – The default is “*BUDG*”. You may enter your own reference, up to 15-characters, in this field.&nbsp; For the purposes, of this example, leave as is.

| ![Image](<lib/admon-tip.png>) | ***Options*** ***Cost centre 1** - Budgets may be entered for [Cost centres*](<cost-centres.md>) *(Cost centre 1), if activated.* ***Cost centre 2** - Budgets may be entered for [Cost centres*](<cost-centres.md>) *(Cost centre 2), if activated.* ***Projects** - Budgets may be entered for [Projects*](<projects.md>)*, if activated.* ***Percentage** - Tick this option and specify a percentage to adjust the budget figures.*&nbsp; ***Delete all** - If any budget figures exists, a confirmation message will be displayed.*&nbsp; *"Delete data for this period?"* *If you click **Yes**, the budget figures for the selected period or dates will be deleted.* ***Copy** -* ***From Budget Previous year** -&nbsp; If you already have budget figures for the previous financial year, select this option to populate last year's budget figures.*&nbsp; ***From Actual Previous year** -&nbsp; If you already have posted batch and document transactions for the previous financial year, select this option to populate last year's the actual totals of posted transactions.*&nbsp; ***Apply to selection** - Add amount to selection?If you key in a value (amount or percentage), and you click the Apply to selection button, a confirmation message will be displayed:*&nbsp; *" Add amount to selection?"*&nbsp; *Click **Yes** to change the amount or percentage. The value (amount or percentage) will increase or decrease any existing values.* |
| --- | --- |


| ![Image](<lib/admon-warning.png>) | *You may enter or overtype an amount. Editing an amount, e.g. editing one or more digits will produce an error message, for example;* &nbsp; *'-13,000.00' is not a valid floating point value for field '2024/06/01 - 2024/06/30'*&nbsp; |
| --- | --- |


5. **Description** – The default is “*Auto budget*”. You may enter your own description (up to 35 characters), in this field. Enter “*Budget 2024/2025*”. &nbsp;
5. Select the first account, i.e. “*Sales*” and enter the monthly budget amount, i.e. –10,000 in die amount field (before the **Apply** to selection button) in.

| ![Image](<lib/admon-tip.png>) | *The “ - “ (minus sign), added before the amount, indicates a credit budget amount. For a debit budget amount, add the amount without the “ - “ (minus sign).* |
| --- | --- |


7. Select the months for which you need to add the budget figures.

| ![Image](<lib/admon-tip.png>) | *To select multiple periods, press the “Shift” key on your keyboard and click on the periods to select the months.*&nbsp; |
| --- | --- |


8. Click on the **Apply** to selection button. A confirmation message is displayed:&nbsp;

*“Add amount to selection?”*&nbsp;

9. Click **Yes** to add the monthly budget figures to the selected months. &nbsp;
9. Repeat this process to add your budget figures.
9. After adding your budget figures for the accounts in this documentation, the “*Budgets*” screen should display as follows:&nbsp;

![Image](<lib/budget-02.png>)

| ![Image](<lib/admon-important.png>) | *Do not enter any budget figures for the main accounts. In this example, Budget figures were not entered for the Main account “Motor vehicle expenses”, but for each sub-account.* |
| --- | --- |


12. After finishing to process your budget figures, you may click on the [**Transaction details**](<budgets-transactions-tab.md>) tab or [**Pivot**](<budgets-pivot-tab.md>) tab view and explore the generated budget figures. &nbsp;
