# Quick Ratio (Acid-Test Ratio)

***

The Quick Ratio, also known as the Acid-Test Ratio, is another financial metric used to assess a company's short-term liquidity and its ability to meet its immediate obligations without relying on the sale of inventory. It is a more stringent measure of liquidity than the Working Capital Ratio because it excludes inventory from current assets. The Quick Ratio provides insight into a company's ability to cover its current liabilities using its most liquid assets.

The formula for calculating the Quick Ratio is as follows:

***Quick Ratio** = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities*

**Where**:

* &nbsp;
  * **Current Assets**: These are the same as in the Working Capital Ratio and include cash, accounts receivable, short-term investments, and other assets expected to be converted into cash within one year from the reporting date.
  * **Inventory**: This refers to the value of the company's unsold products or goods that are still in the process of being manufactured.
  * **Current Liabilities**: These are the same as in the Working Capital Ratio and represent the obligations due within one year from the reporting date.

The Quick Ratio provides a more conservative measure of a company's short-term liquidity because it excludes inventory, which may not be readily convertible into cash in case of financial difficulties or an economic downturn. Generally, a Quick Ratio of 1 or higher is considered financially healthy, as it indicates that the company can pay off its current liabilities using its most liquid assets without relying on selling inventory.

Similar to the Working Capital Ratio, the ideal Quick Ratio may vary across industries, and it's essential to consider other financial ratios and performance indicators to get a comprehensive view of a company's financial situation. Companies in industries with more stable and predictable cash flows may have lower Quick Ratios, while those in industries with uncertain or cyclical cash flows may aim for higher Quick Ratios to ensure they can meet their obligations in challenging times.
