# Sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for a South African Church?

For a South African church, the Chart of Accounts (COA) will differ from that of a manufacturing business due to the unique financial transactions and reporting needs in a religious organization. Here's a sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for a South African church:

1. Assets&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;.1 Current Assets&nbsp;

    - &#49;.1.1 Cash on Hand&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.2 Cash in Bank&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.3 Petty Cash&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.4 Accounts Receivable (Offerings and Donations)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.5 Prepaid Expenses&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.6 Other Current Assets

  - &#49;.2 Non-Current Assets&nbsp;

    - &#49;.2.1 Property (Church Building)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.2 Land&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.3 Furniture and Fixtures&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.4 Equipment (Sound system, musical instruments, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.5 Vehicles (if owned by the church)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.6 Investments (if any)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.7 Other Non-Current Assets

2. Liabilities&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#50;.1 Current Liabilities&nbsp;

    - &#50;.1.1 Accounts Payable (Outstanding expenses, bills, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.2 Accrued Liabilities (e.g., Salaries payable, utility bills)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.3 Short-Term Loans and Borrowings (if applicable)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.4 Other Current Liabilities

  - &#50;.2 Non-Current Liabilities&nbsp;

    - &#50;.2.1 Long-Term Loans and Borrowings (if applicable)&nbsp;
    - &#50;.2.2 Other Non-Current Liabilities

3. Fund Balances&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#51;.1 General Fund&nbsp;
  - &#51;.2 Building Fund&nbsp;
  - &#51;.3 Mission Fund&nbsp;
  - &#51;.4 Other Designated Funds

4. Income&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#52;.1 Offerings&nbsp;
  - &#52;.2 Tithes&nbsp;
  - &#52;.3 Donations&nbsp;
  - &#52;.4 Fundraising Income&nbsp;
  - &#52;.5 Rental Income (if the church rents out its facilities)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.6 Interest Income&nbsp;
  - &#52;.7 Other Income

5. Expenses&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#53;.1 Salaries and Benefits (for church staff)&nbsp;
  - &#53;.2 Rent and Utilities&nbsp;
  - &#53;.3 Office Supplies&nbsp;
  - &#53;.4 Maintenance and Repairs&nbsp;
  - &#53;.5 Outreach and Mission Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.6 Worship and Music Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.7 Educational and Training Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.8 Charitable Contributions&nbsp;
  - &#53;.9 Other Operating Expenses

6. Taxes and Levies&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#54;.1 Value Added Tax (VAT)&nbsp;
  - &#54;.2 Pay-As-You-Earn (PAYE) Tax (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#54;.3 Other Taxes and Levies

7. Other Comprehensive Income&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#55;.1 Foreign Currency Translation Gains/Losses (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#55;.2 Other Items

8. Gains and Losses&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#56;.1 Gain on Sale of Assets (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#56;.2 Loss on Impairment of Assets (if applicable)

9. Interchurch Transactions&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#57;.1 Interchurch Transfers

10. Ministries and Projects&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;0.1 Youth Ministry&nbsp;
  - &#49;0.2 Women's Ministry&nbsp;
  - &#49;0.3 Outreach Projects&nbsp;
  - &#49;0.4 Other Ministries and Projects

11. Memberships and Subscriptions&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;1.1 Church Memberships&nbsp;
  - &#49;1.2 Subscriptions to Religious Publications

12. Donor-Restricted Funds&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;2.1 Donations with Specific Restrictions

Please note that the above COA is a general outline and can be adapted based on the specific activities, ministries, and financial reporting needs of the South African church. Additionally, it's crucial to seek guidance from a qualified accountant or financial expert to ensure compliance with South African accounting standards and regulations for non-profit and religious organizations.
