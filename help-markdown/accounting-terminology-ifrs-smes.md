# What is the difference of Accounting terminology used in IFRS (full) and IFRS for SMEs?

The accounting terminology used in IFRS (full) and IFRS for SMEs (International Financial Reporting Standards for Small and Medium-sized Entities) is generally similar, but there are some differences in terminology and terminology usage. The main objective of IFRS for SMEs is to simplify the accounting requirements for SMEs and make them more accessible and cost-effective compared to the full IFRS framework.

Here are some key differences in accounting terminology between IFRS (full) and IFRS for SMEs:

1. **Terminology Adaptation**: IFRS for SMEs may use simplified or adapted terminology to make it more understandable and applicable to the needs of SMEs. This can include the use of simpler terms or specific terms commonly used in SME contexts.
1. **Presentation and Disclosure**: IFRS for SMEs may have reduced disclosure requirements compared to full IFRS. This means that certain terminology related to disclosure items in full IFRS may not be present or may be simplified in IFRS for SMEs.
1. **Measurement and Recognition**: IFRS for SMEs provides simplified measurement and recognition principles compared to full IFRS. This may result in the use of different terms or terminology specific to the simplified requirements of SMEs.
1. **Specific Industry Considerations**: IFRS for SMEs may provide industry-specific guidance or exemptions for certain industries or activities. This can result in the use of industry-specific terminology or terminology tailored to the needs of specific sectors.

It's important to note that while there may be differences in terminology, the underlying accounting principles and concepts remain consistent across both IFRS (full) and IFRS for SMEs. The aim is to provide a clear and transparent framework for financial reporting, irrespective of the size or complexity of the reporting entity.
