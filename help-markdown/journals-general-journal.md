# General journal - Key points

The General journal is a fundamental component of the accounting system used for recording miscellaneous transactions that do not fit into the other specialized subsidiary journals. It provides flexibility for recording various types of transactions that are not covered by the specific journals, such as the Sales journal, Purchase journal, or Payments and Receipts journals (linked to specific bank accounts).

Here are some key points about the General journal:

1. **Miscellaneous Transactions**: The General Journal is used to record transactions that are unique or do not have a designated journal for their specific type. It includes transactions such as corrections of errors, adjusting entries, accruals, deferrals, depreciation entries, and other non-routine transactions.
1. **Flexibility**: The General Journal allows for the recording of diverse transactions, making it a versatile tool in the accounting process. It accommodates transactions that do not fit into predefined categories or require special treatment.
1. **Manual Entries**: Unlike other subsidiary journals that may have automated or system-generated entries, the General Journal typically involves manual entries made by accountants or bookkeepers. These entries require careful consideration and proper documentation.
1. **Debits and Credits**: The General journal follows the fundamental principles of double-entry bookkeeping, where each transaction is recorded with equal debits and credits to maintain the balance of the accounting equation.
1. **Ledger posting**: After entries are recorded in the General Journal, they are posted to the respective accounts in the General ledger. This ensures that the impact of the transactions is reflected accurately in the overall accounting records.
1. **Financial reporting**: The transactions recorded in the General Journal are ultimately reflected in the financial statements. They contribute to the preparation of the trial balance, income statement, balance sheet, and other financial reports.
1. **Audit trail**: The General Journal provides an audit trail for miscellaneous transactions, allowing for proper documentation and tracking of entries. This helps in ensuring transparency, accountability, and accuracy in financial reporting.
1. **Internal controls**: The General Journal, like other accounting journals, plays a role in maintaining internal controls within an organization. It allows for proper authorization, review, and approval processes for miscellaneous transactions, minimizing the risk of errors or fraudulent activities.

Overall, the General journal serves as a crucial tool in accounting, providing flexibility for recording various types of transactions that do not fit into other specialized journals. It ensures that all transactions are properly documented, recorded, and reported, maintaining the integrity of the financial records and supporting accurate financial analysis and decision-making.

# When should a general journal be used?

The general journal, on the other hand, is typically used for recording non-routine or unusual transactions that do not fit into other specialized journals. It is a versatile journal that can be used for various purposes, including corrections of errors. If there is no specific journal available for a particular type of transaction, using the general journal is a suitable option.

However, whenever there is a specialized journal available for a specific type of transaction, it is recommended to use that journal to ensure accuracy and efficiency in record-keeping. This helps maintain consistency and allows for easier analysis and reporting of transactions within their relevant categories.

In general, a general journal is used to record non-routine or unusual transactions that do not fit into the other specialized journals. However, if there is a specific journal available for a particular type of transaction, it is best to use that journal to ensure accuracy and efficiency in record-keeping

# When should a general journal NOT be used?

A general journal should not be used in certain situations where there are specific journals designed for recording transactions. For example:

* **Sales**: For recording sales, specialised journals like the sales journal should be used. To record sales transactions in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, you can use the sales journal available in the **Batch entry** section of the **Default** ribbon. The sales journal allows you to enter and manage sales-related transactions effectively.

In addition to the sales journal, you can process [invoices](<documents-setup-invoices.md>) and [credit notes](<documents-setup-credit-notes.md>) using the **Documents** section on the **Default** ribbon. [Invoices](<documents-setup-invoices.md>) and [credit notes](<documents-setup-credit-notes.md>) can be linked to the sales journal in **Setup → [Documents setup**](<documents-setup.md>) (**Setup** ribbon). When you process invoices and credit notes, the source documents will be automatically generated, allowing you to have physical or digital copies of these documents for record-keeping purposes. These documents can be printed and reprinted as needed, enabling you to send them to your debtors (customers/clients) as required.

Once the invoices and credit notes are updated and posted to the ledger, the corresponding transactions will be automatically processed in the sales journal. Each item included in the invoice or credit note will be recorded in the sales journal, ensuring accurate tracking of your sales transactions.

By utilising the sales journal and linking invoices and credit notes, you can efficiently manage and maintain a comprehensive record of your sales activities in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5.

* **Purchases**: For recording purchases, specialised journals like the purchase journal should be used. To record purchase transactions in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, you can use the purchase journal available in the **Batch entry** section of the **Default** ribbon. The purchase journal allows you to enter and manage purchase-related transactions effectively.

In addition to the purchase journal, you can process purchase and supplier return documents using the **Documents** section on the **Default** ribbon. [Purchases](<documents-setup-purchases.md>) and supplier return documents can be linked to the purchase journal in **Setup → [Documents setup**](<documents-setup.md>) (**Setup** ribbon). When you process [purchase](<documents-setup-purchases.md>) and [supplier return](<documents-setup-supplier-returns.md>) documents, the source documents will be automatically generated, allowing you to have physical or digital copies of these documents for record-keeping purposes. These documents can be printed and reprinted as needed, enabling you to send them to your creditors (suppliers/vendors) as required.

Once the [purchase](<documents-setup-purchases.md>) and [supplier return](<documents-setup-supplier-returns.md>) documents are updated and posted to the ledger, the corresponding transactions will be automatically processed in the purchase journal. Each item included in the purchase and [supplier return](<documents-setup-supplier-returns.md>) documents will be recorded in the purchase journal, ensuring accurate tracking of your purchase transactions.

By utilising the purchase journal and linking purchase and [supplier return](<documents-setup-supplier-returns.md>) documents, you can efficiently manage and maintain a comprehensive record of your purchase activities in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5.

* **Cash transactions**: To record cash transactions in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, specialised journals such as the cash receipts journal and cash disbursements journal are used. In osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, when setting up bank accounts and petty cash accounts in the **Setup → [Accounts**](<bank-accounts-new.md>) menu of the **Setup** ribbon, each account will be associated with its own unique payments journal and receipts journal.

If the [**Bank import plugin**](<plugin-bank-import.md>) is not activated in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, you can manually process cash transactions in the respective payments journal and receipts journal associated with the bank account or petty cash account. To access these journals, you can go to the **Batch entry** option on the **Default** ribbon.

If the [**Bank import plugin**](<plugin-bank-import.md>) is activated, you have the option to process and import transactions directly into the payments journal and receipts journal associated with the bank account or petty cash account. This can be done through the **Cash/Bank entry** option on the **Default** ribbon.

By using the appropriate journals for cash transactions, you can effectively record and manage your cash receipts and disbursements. The journals allow for accurate tracking of cash flows, providing a clear overview of your financial activities.
