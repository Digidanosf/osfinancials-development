# Credit control and bad debts

Credit control and managing bad debts are essential aspects of financial management for businesses. Here are some key points to consider regarding credit control and handling bad debts:

1. **Credit control policies**: Establishing robust credit control policies and procedures is crucial. This involves assessing the creditworthiness of customers before extending credit, setting appropriate credit limits, and defining payment terms and conditions.
1. **Credit checks**: Conducting credit checks on potential customers can help assess their financial stability and credit history. This information assists in determining whether to grant credit and the appropriate credit limit.
1. **Clear terms and conditions**: Clearly communicate your payment terms and conditions to customers upfront. This includes specifying credit periods, penalties for late payments, and any applicable interest charges.
1. **Invoicing and Statements**: Timely and accurate invoicing is essential to ensure prompt payment. Regularly send account statements to customers to keep them informed of their outstanding balances.
1. **Payment reminders**: Implement a systematic process for sending payment reminders to customers as payment due dates approach. This can be done through automated reminders or personalized communications.
1. **Debt collection**: If customers fail to make payments within the agreed terms, initiate a structured debt collection process. This may involve sending reminder letters, making phone calls, or engaging a debt collection agency if necessary.
1. **Provision for Bad Debts**: Estimate and allocate provisions for bad debts based on the likelihood of non-payment. This helps account for potential losses and maintains accurate financial reporting.
1. **Write-offs**: When it becomes clear that a debt is uncollectible, it may need to be written off as a bad debt. Proper documentation and accounting entries should be made to reflect this.
1. **Legal options**: In extreme cases where all collection efforts fail, legal action may be pursued to recover outstanding debts. Consult legal professionals to explore available options.
1. **Regular review**: Continuously monitor and review your credit control processes and performance. Identify areas for improvement and implement necessary adjustments to minimize bad debts.

Effective credit control practices can help mitigate the risk of bad debts and maintain healthy cash flow. Regular monitoring, proactive communication, and diligent follow-up are crucial in managing credit effectively and minimizing the impact of bad debts on your business.

# When granting credit to customers, the credit screening, credit bureaus and legislation to consider?

When granting credit to customers, it is important to consider several factors related to credit screening, credit bureaus, and relevant legislation. Here's an overview of each aspect:

1. **Credit screening**:
- &nbsp;

  - **Financial information**: Assess the financial stability and creditworthiness of the customer by reviewing their financial statements, bank references, and credit references.
  - **Credit history**: Obtain credit references from other suppliers or vendors to gauge the customer's payment history and credit behavior.
  - **Trade references**: Contact the customer's previous or existing suppliers to gather information about their payment habits and creditworthiness.
  - **Credit application**: Require customers to complete a credit application form that includes essential details such as business information, financial statements, and references.

2. **Credit bureaus**:
- &nbsp;

  - **Credit reports**: Utilize credit reports obtained from credit bureaus that provide information on an individual or company's credit history, payment patterns, outstanding debts, and credit scores.
  - **Credit scores**: Evaluate credit scores provided by credit bureaus to assess the risk associated with extending credit to a particular customer. Credit scores are typically based on various factors such as payment history, credit utilization, length of credit history, and public records.

3. **Legislation and regulations**:
- &nbsp;

  - **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)**: Adhere to the provisions of the FCRA, which governs the collection, dissemination, and use of consumer credit information, ensuring compliance with consumer rights and privacy.
  - **Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)**: Comply with the ECOA, which prohibits discrimination based on race, colour, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, or receipt of public assistance when evaluating credit applications.
  - **Data Protection Laws**: Abide by applicable data protection and privacy laws to safeguard customer data and ensure proper handling and storage of credit-related information.
  - **Industry-Specific Regulations**: Consider any industry-specific regulations or guidelines that may impact the extension of credit, such as those related to financial services, healthcare, or consumer lending.

By considering these factors, businesses can make informed decisions when granting credit to customers, mitigating the risk of defaults and ensuring compliance with relevant laws and regulations. It is advisable to establish clear credit policies and procedures, conduct thorough credit assessments, and regularly monitor the creditworthiness of customers to manage credit risks effectively.

# Open item feature versus the balance brought forward - Advantages to manage debtor accounts to reduce bad debts

Managing debtor accounts using the Open Item feature can offer advantages in reducing bad debts. Here's how it can help:

1. **Improved accuracy**: With the Open Item feature, each transaction is treated as a separate item or entry. This allows for precise tracking of individual invoices and payments. By accurately matching payments to specific invoices, you can identify any outstanding balances more easily, ensuring that debts are promptly addressed.
1. **Enhanced visibility**: The Open Item feature provides a clear overview of each debtor's account, showing all outstanding invoices and payments. This visibility enables you to identify overdue invoices and take appropriate actions, such as sending reminders or initiating collection efforts promptly.
1. **Proactive credit control**: By regularly reviewing open items and following up with debtors, you can maintain better control over credit terms and ensure timely payments. Promptly addressing any discrepancies or disputes can help prevent bad debts from accumulating.
1. **Accurate ageing analysis**: The Open Item feature enables accurate ageing analysis of outstanding invoices. By categorizing debts based on their ageing periods (e.g., current, 30 days, 60 days, etc.), you can identify problematic accounts or overdue payments more effectively. This analysis helps in prioritising collections and taking necessary actions to recover outstanding amounts.
1. **Prompt dispute resolution**: The detailed tracking provided by the Open Item feature facilitates faster resolution of invoice disputes. By promptly addressing and resolving any discrepancies or disputes, you can minimise the risk of bad debts arising from unresolved issues.

Overall, the Open Item feature offers greater control and visibility over debtor accounts, helping you proactively manage credit control and reduce the occurrence of bad debts. It promotes accuracy, enables effective ageing analysis, and facilitates prompt actions to address outstanding balances, enhancing your overall debt management practices.

# Open item and managing of debtor accounts&nbsp;

The Open Item feature in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 allows for efficient management of debtor accounts by matching transactions and tracking outstanding amounts. Here are the key aspects of the Open item feature:

1. **Enabling open item**: The Open item feature is selected in the "*Open item account*" of the [**Accounting**](<open-item-link-settings.md>) tab in a Debtor account. It allows transactions, such as invoices, credit notes, and payments, to be matched to credit or debit transactions.
1. **Processing transactions**: When processing transactions in batches, journals, or documents, an Open Item selection screen displays all outstanding transactions. Here, specific transactions can be selected and linked to the current transaction.
1. **Open item options on the Default ribbon:**
- &nbsp;

  - [**Open item**](<open-item-link.md>): This feature allows you to manage links, clear links, generate balance forward, unlink transactions, view transactions in a statement layout, and export transactions to a spreadsheet. You can select all debtor accounts or a specific debtor account.
  - **Debtors - Transactions tab**: The [**Transactions**](<debtor-tab-transactions.md>) tab in debtor accounts lists all transactions or only unmatched outstanding transactions. Filtering options are available, and you can print posted batch reports or view transactions in the T-Account viewer, which also allows printing and exporting to a spreadsheet.
  - [**Documents**](<documents-filter-search-options.md>): The powerful document grid for Invoices and Credit notes allows filtering and searching for specific documents with outstanding balances or documents without any outstanding or due amounts for a particular debtor account. You may [export](<export-documents-grid.md>) these filter results to a spreadsheet.

4. **Open item options on the Reports ribbon**:
- &nbsp;

  - [**Transaction list reports**](<debtors-transaction-lists.md>): Transaction reports under the **Reports → Debtors → Transactions** menu offer options such as the **Transaction list** (lists a complete list of transactions) or a **Transaction list - Open item** (lists only the outstanding transactions) for all debtors or a selected debtor account.&nbsp;
  - [**Age analysis**](<open-item-due-tab.md>): Age analysis (accessed on the **Reports** ribbon or on the **Due** tab of **Age analysis** on the **Default** ribbon) generate on-screen or printed reports based on the system date or a selected date of ageing.
  - [**Age analysis**](<reports-debtors-age-analysis.md>) **- Reports**: The **Reports → Debtors → Age analysis** menu provides the ability to print age analysis reports for all debtors or a selected debtor account.
  - [**Debtor statements**](<debtor-statements.md>): Debtor statements can be printed for all transactions or only outstanding transactions.

By utilising the Open Item feature and accessing its various options, users can effectively manage debtor accounts, match transactions, generate reports, and monitor outstanding balances for efficient credit control and bad debt reduction.

# Managing credit limits

## Available balances - Credit control listing

In osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, besides the age analysis reports, you have the option to print a list of debtor's available balances as of the system date. This report is available on the **Reports → Debtors listing** - [Credit control listing (credit limit vs actual balance) as at](<debtors-list-credit-control.md>) report. This report provides information on the available balances for all debtors or for a selected debtor account or for all debtors associated with a particular debtor reporting group. Moreover, you have the option to include the contact details of the debtors in the report. Please note that this report reflects the available balances as of the current date recorded in your system.&nbsp;

| ![Image](<lib/admon-important.png>) | *The current balance displayed in the "[Credit control listing (credit limit versus actual balance) as at*](<debtors-list-credit-control.md>)*" report does not include unposted transactions in batches or journals, as well as unposted documents such as invoices and credit notes. It represents the balance based on posted transactions recorded in the ledger. Please keep this in mind when reviewing the report, as any unposted transactions will not be reflected in the current balance.* |
| --- | --- |


The report will include the following details for each debtor:

1. **Credit limit**: This is the maximum amount of credit extended to the debtor.
1. **Posted transactions in batches and documents**: It will display the totals of the transactions that have been recorded in batches (journals) and documents.&nbsp;
1. **Available balances**: The report will show the available balance for each debtor. The available balance is calculated by subtracting the outstanding balance from the credit limit. It represents the remaining credit that the debtor has available.

By printing this report, you can have an overview of the credit limits, current balances, and available balances for your debtors. This information can help you monitor credit utilisation, identify potential risks, and make informed decisions regarding credit extensions and debt management.

Remember to review the report regularly and take appropriate actions based on the information provided to maintain healthy debtor accounts and minimize credit-related issues.

## Available balances in documents - Messages

In osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, when processing sales documents such as quotes, invoices, and credit notes, the available balances (credit limit minus current balance) are shown in the document entry footer section. When you complete the document and click **OK**, a warning message may appear if the debtor's balance exceeds the credit limit. The warning message will indicate the amount by which the balance exceeds the credit limit, such as&nbsp;

*"Debtor's balance is over credit limit with: -1100."*

This warning message serves as an alert to inform you that the debtor's outstanding balance has exceeded the predefined credit limit. It indicates that the debtor may have exceeded their credit terms or credit limit, and you may need to review their account and take appropriate actions, such as contacting the debtor to arrange payment or adjusting their credit limit.

It's important to address these warnings and take necessary steps to manage credit risks and ensure timely payment from debtors. This helps maintain healthy cash flow and minimizes the potential for bad debts or financial difficulties for your business.

# Calendar - Appointments and notes

Calendar - If you need to review a credit limit or have some concerns, you may schedule up to six categories of appointments with debtors in the [**Calendar**](<calendar-planner.md>). On these appointments, you may add notes about the intended reason for the appointment and discussions. This feature allows you to effectively manage and review credit limits, address concerns, and track important discussions with debtors.&nbsp;

By utilising the calendar feature, you can effectively manage your debtor-related appointments and stay on top of credit limit reviews and other important discussions. The ability to add notes and set reminders enhances your ability to track and follow up on these appointments, ensuring efficient debtor management.&nbsp;

A list and detailed information of all appointments, including any notes, can be accessed on the [**Appointment**](<debtor-tab-appointments.md>) tab of a selected debtor account in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5. This tab provides a comprehensive list of all scheduled appointments related to that particular debtor, allowing you to review and manage the details and notes associated with each appointment.

Remember to regularly check your calendar to stay updated on upcoming appointments and make necessary preparations. This feature provides a convenient way to schedule, manage, and keep track of debtor-related activities, promoting effective communication and timely follow-ups.

# Disable debtor accounts

Disable debtor accounts - In osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, if you have written off bad debts for a debtor and consider them to be a credit risk, you have the option to disable their debtor account. By selecting the "*Account disabled*" option on the [Accounting information](<debtor-tab-accounting-info.md>) tab, you can prevent any further processing and posting of transactions for that particular debtor.&nbsp;

By setting a debtor account as inactive or disabling a debtor account, you ensure that no further transactions can be processed or posted for that specific debtor. This helps in managing credit risks and preventing additional financial exposure. However, it's important to note that all existing transactions and historical data related to the debtor will still be available in reports and other relevant areas of the osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 system.
