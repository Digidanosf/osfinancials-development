# What is the chart of accounts?

The chart of accounts is a systematic arrangement of all the accounts that a business uses to record its financial transactions. It encompasses a comprehensive list of accounts tailored to fit the specific needs of each business. These accounts are linked to account groups, which classify them according to their nature, such as fixed assets, non-current assets, current assets, current liabilities, long-term liabilities, revenue, equity or capital, operating revenue, non-operating revenue, operating expenses, non-operating expenses, cost of sales, and more.

The chart of accounts is structured to align accounts with five financial categories: assets, liabilities, equity (capital), revenue (income), and expenses. By linking or mapping accounts to these financial categories, businesses ensure consistency and accuracy in financial records. The chart of accounts serves as a standardized structure for organizing financial information, facilitating the management and analysis of a business's financial data. It is a systematic arrangement of all the accounts that a business needs to prepare its financial statements, including the such as the [trial balance](<ledger-analyser-trial-balance.md>), [income statement](<ledger-analyser-income-statement.md>) and [balance sheet](<ledger-analyser-balance-sheet.md>).&nbsp;

The specific account groups and their links to financial categories may vary depending on the applicable accounting standards, such as Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS), IFRS for SMEs (International Financial Reporting Standards for Small and Medium-sized Entities). or other jurisdiction-specific frameworks. Adhering to these accounting standards ensures compliance and facilitates consistent financial reporting across entities.

In summary, the chart of accounts is an essential tool for financial reporting and analysis. It provides a standardized structure for organizing financial information, ensures consistency and accuracy in financial records, and can be customized to meet the specific needs of a business based on its size, industry, and accounting requirements.

# The accounting framework aligned with the chart of accounts, when processing transactions the accounting standards prescribes the treatment of transactions?

The accounting framework aligned with the chart of accounts provides the guidelines and principles for how transactions should be recorded and treated in financial statements. Accounting standards, such as GAAP or IFRS, prescribe the specific rules and principles that determine how transactions should be recognized, measured, and presented in financial statements.

When processing transactions, businesses follow the accounting standards to ensure that the financial information accurately reflects the financial position, performance, and cash flows of the company. The accounting standards dictate the appropriate accounting treatment for various types of transactions, such as revenue recognition, expense recognition, asset valuation, and liability measurement.

By adhering to the accounting standards, businesses maintain consistency and comparability in their financial reporting. This allows stakeholders, including investors, creditors, and regulatory bodies, to understand and analyse the financial information in a standardized and meaningful way.

The alignment of the chart of accounts with the accounting framework ensures that transactions are recorded and classified correctly, following the prescribed rules and principles of the applicable accounting standards. This helps ensure the accuracy and reliability of the financial statements and promotes transparency and trust in the financial reporting process.

# Steps to Customise a Set of Books (template)

Once you have downloaded a Set of Books (template), the first steps is to view and analyse the existing chart of accounts, groups and financial categories and change it to suit your specific requirements.&nbsp;

## View analyse and print the chart of accounts

After opening a Set of Books, you may print a list of the chart of accounts from the following options:

* **Reports →&nbsp; Ledger → [Listing**](<report-ledger-chart-of-accounts.md>) menu. You may select to print the chart of accounts in various sequences: account code, account description, account type, reporting group 1 or reporting group 2. , &nbsp;
* **Ledger analyser 1 / Ledger analyser 2** - [Trial balance](<ledger-analyser-trial-balance.md>) - "Ignore zero balances" option not selected. The default "Groups" is none. This will list all the Ledger accounts (Chart of accounts) according to the account code. To preview how the Chart of accounts is structured and linked to [Financial categories](<financial-categories.md>), [Account group 1 and Account group 2](<groups-accounts.md>),&nbsp; select Reporting groups 1 or Reporting group 2. &nbsp;
