## South African Household Chart of Accounts

The Personal Household Chart of Accounts is a streamlined, intuitive framework designed specifically to track your household’s unique financial footprint. By establishing a clear baseline of your **Assets, Liabilities, Income, and Expenses**, this system empowers you to take complete control of your net worth, monitor dynamic family budgets, and manage day-to-day liquidity with total confidence.

Beyond personal financial management, this architecture is built with professional compliance at its core. It natively aligns with the **South African Revenue Service (SARS)** reporting structures, transforming your annual eFiling process from a stressful guessing game into a simple, data-driven exercise. Every transaction flows into clean, audit-ready categories, giving you full peace of mind if SARS ever requests a verification of your returns.

It segregates your active trading turnover and operational costs from everyday domestic consumption, allowing you to scale a secondary business from home without complicating your personal books or losing your vital tax audit trail.

## Clear rules regarding side-hustles, spaza shops, and hobby businesses

In South Africa, there are very clear rules regarding side-hustles, spaza shops, and hobby businesses.

Understanding these rules comes down to a crucial distinction: **SARS has no "free" threshold for deciding whether or not to *declare* your business, but there are strict financial thresholds that change *how* you file and pay.**

### &#49;. The Core Declaration Rule: There is a "Zero Threshold"

The single biggest mistake people make is thinking, *"My hobby only makes a few thousand Rands, so I don’t need to tell SARS."*

By law, **all side-hustle income must be declared, regardless of how small or intermittent the amount is**.

* **How it is declared:** You do not file a separate company return if you are operating as an individual/sole proprietor. You declare your business turnover and its 5000-series Cost of Sales/Expenses inside the **Local Business Section** of your normal individual tax return (**ITR12**) on eFiling.
* **How it is taxed:** SARS pools your side-hustle net profit together with your regular salary (if you have a day job). You are taxed on the combined total using the progressive individual tax brackets (starting at 18%).

> **Example:** If your day job pays R200,000 a year, and your side-hustle nets R40,000, SARS assesses you on **R240,000**. Failing to declare that extra R40,000 is considered non-compliance.

### &#50;. The Provisional Tax Threshold: R30,000

Because a side-hustle does not have monthly PAYE automatically deducted like a salary, you enter the **Provisional Tax** system to pay your tax in advance instalments.

* **The Threshold:** If your side-hustle earns more than **R30,000 of taxable income (net profit)** in a tax year from sources outside of a standard salary, you are legally required to register as a provisional taxpayer.
* **The Filing Routine (IRP6):** You must submit two additional provisional tax returns every year via eFiling:

  * **&#49;st Period:** Due by **31 August** (paying 50% of your estimated tax liability for the year).
  * **&#50;nd Period:** Due by **28/29 February** (paying the remaining balance of the estimated year's tax).

### &#51;. The New VAT Registration Thresholds (Updated 2026)

If your side-hustle or spaza shop starts moving serious volume, you have to monitor your gross turnover for Value-Added Tax. Note that the Minister of Finance **officially increased these thresholds effective 1 April 2026**:&nbsp;

See - [Your complete guide to SARS tax filing for freelancers](<https://thesait.org.za/your-complete-guide-to-sars-tax-filing-for-freelancers/> "target=\"\_blank\"")&nbsp;

* **Compulsory VAT Registration: R2.3 Million** *(Increased from R1 million)*. If your total side-hustle sales exceed R2.3 million in any consecutive 12-month period, you must register for VAT within 21 days and begin charging 15% on your invoices.
* **Voluntary VAT Registration: R120,000** *(Increased from R50,000)*. If your turnover is under R2.3 million but over R120,000, you are allowed to *optionally* opt-in to the VAT system. This is exactly why we include those specialized **G2700000 VAT Control** accounts in the template—they remain safely disabled until you cross this threshold and choose to switch them on\!

### &#52;. The Simplified Alternative: Turnover Tax Threshold

If your side-hustle has a gross turnover of **under R2.3 million per year** *(also increased from R1 million effective April 2026)*, you can apply for the **Turnover Tax Regime**.

This is an incredibly generous system built for micro-businesses. Instead of calculating complex profit margins and paying normal income tax scales, you pay a tiny, single percentage tax based purely on your gross turnover, and your first **R600,000 of sales is 100% tax-free**. It completely replaces Income Tax, Provisional Tax, and CGT obligations.

### Summary Checklist for Your eFiling Workflow:

1. **Net Profit is R0 – R30,000:** Just declare it once a year in the Local Business section of your standard **ITR12** annual filing.
1. **Net Profit Exceeds R30,000:** Activate your **Provisional Tax** profile on eFiling and submit your two **IRP6** payment returns in August and February.
1. **Turnover Exceeds R120,000:** Keep track via your spreadsheet or ledger; you can now optionally choose to activate your **VAT201** profiles.
1. **Turnover Exceeds R2.3 Million:** Compulsory registration for **VAT**.

# Taking on opening balances

Taking on opening balances is the most critical step when setting up the **4-EN-SOUTH-AFRICA-PERSONAL-ACCOUNTS** template. Without accurate opening balances, your balance sheet won't balance, your net worth tracking will be distorted, and your tax audit trail will be flawed from day one.

In TurboCASH and osFinancials, opening balances are entered using an **Opening Balances Journal**. To keep the double-entry accounting system happy, every debit must have a credit. We achieve this by using a temporary system account called the **Opening Balances Contra Account** (usually a specialized equity account in the 3000 range) to balance the journal. Once all balances are entered, this contra account should net out to zero.

Here is the step-by-step framework to guide users through a clean take-on process:

## Step 1: Gather Your Take-On Documents

Before typing anything into the journal, users need to gather their financial values as of **28 February 2026** (the final day of the previous tax year), so they can cleanly start fresh on **1 March 2026**.

* **Bank \& Cash:** Final bank statements, credit card statements, and a physical count of petty cash.
* **Accounts Receivable: Customers -** A list of exactly who owes the side-hustle money.
* **Accounts Payable:** **Suppliers** - A list of outstanding bills the household owes to suppliers.
* **Assets:** Original purchase costs and accumulated depreciation values for vehicles and property.
* **Liabilities:** The outstanding capital balances on home loans, car finance, and personal loans.

### Step 2: The Take-On Cheat Sheet (Debits vs. Credits)

When entering values into the Opening Balances Journal, users must follow basic accounting rules. Here is a quick reference table of how to enter household balances:

| **Account Type** | **Examples** | **Journal Action** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Assets (What you own)** | Bank accounts, Investments, Property, Vehicles, Cash | **DEBIT** (Positive number) |
| **Asset Deductions** | Accumulated Depreciation accounts | **CREDIT** (Negative number) |
| **Liabilities (What you owe)** | Home Loans, Vehicle Finance, Credit Card debt, Tax debt | **CREDIT** (Negative number) |
| **Equity (Your Net Worth)** | Accumulated Funds / Retained Income from prior years | **CREDIT** (Negative number) |


### Step 3: Processing the Take-On in TurboCASH

1. **Open the Journal:** Go to **Batches / Journals** and open the designated *Opening Balances / Initial Take-On Journal*.
1. **Set the Date:** Ensure the transaction date is set to **28 February 2026** (or 1 March 2026 as the opening block).
1. **Enter the Lines:**
- &nbsp;

  - Select the target household account (e.g., B1000000 Transactional Bank Account).
  - Enter the balance as a **Debit**.
  - Select the *Opening Balances Contra Account* as the balancing offset.

4. **Repeat for all accounts:** Continue down the list entering your liabilities as credits, property as debits, etc.

### Step 4: The Ultimate Test (Balancing to Zero)

Once a user has entered every single asset, liability, and historical equity balance, they must look at the **Journal Total**:

> **The Golden Rule:** The total balance of the Opening Balances Journal must equal **0.00** before posting.

If the journal balances perfectly to zero, it means the user's personal financial equation is perfectly aligned:

***Assets=Liabilities+Equity***

Once posted, the household's financial dashboard is officially active, and they can immediately begin processing new 2026/2027 transactions, municipal bills, and side-hustle invoices\!
