# Translate the "*CUSTOMISE-EMPTY-BOOKS*" template&nbsp;

To work in the language of your choice, after you have use the **Browse**&nbsp; button on the Open Set of Books.&nbsp; Navigate to the bin/CUSTOMISE-EMPTY-BOOKS folder. Select from 3-digits to 8-digits main accounts templates of your choice and click **Open**. &nbsp;

**Basic Steps to customise or translate your Set of Books, based on one of the "*CUSTOMISE-EMPTY-BOOKS*" templates into the language of your choice:**&nbsp;

1. Open the Set Books ("*CUSTOMISE-EMPTY-BOOKS*" template).
1. Save the Set of Books with a name of your choice. To do this go to the [Save as](<books-save-as.md>) option on the **Start** ribbon and enter a unique name for your Set Books.&nbsp;
1. Select language of your choice in the [Switch language](<switch-language.md>) option (**Start** ribbon). If you have selected language of your choice, your Set of Books will automatically open in your selected language.&nbsp;
1. The customisation or translation is basic simple actions:&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - Translate the descriptions of records in the *'Types'* tab of **Tools → Customize language** (**Setup** ribbon). This will include, document types available in "*English*" language, (i.e. "*Invoice, Credit note, Quote, Purchase, Supplier return*" and "*Order*"). You can customise or translate it into your selected language or customise the document types for your specific language, industry or according to your unique needs.&nbsp;
  - This will also include the Reporting groups' group descriptions, e.g. "Account Group1" to "Account Group 1", etc. in **Setup → [Groups**](<setup-groups.md>), (**Setup** ribbon) update.&nbsp;
  - Translate the three control accounts i.e. "*Debtors control*, *Creditors control*" and "*Retained earnings*" In the **Setup → [Accounts**](<ledger-accounts.md>) (**Setup** ribbon).&nbsp;

Your basic Set of Books has been customised and translated, and you can take the rest of the actions such as creating or importing your [General ledgers accounts](<ledger-accounts.md>), [Debtor accounts](<debtor-accounts.md>), [Creditor accounts](<creditor-accounts.md>) and [Stock items](<stock-inventory.md>) (Inventory items or Products).

## Language settings

The language specific settings is as follows:

**Language** - "*English*" is the default language. You may select your language and country in [**Switch language**](<switch-language.md>) (**Start** ribbon).

## Stock items / Inventory items

Before you add or import your stock items / inventory items / products you may need to configure some important basic settings such as Cost of sales,&nbsp; Create tax classes / account classes&nbsp; and Selling prices.&nbsp;

At this point, your chart of accounts (general ledger accounts) should have already been finalised.&nbsp;

### Activate Cost of Sales&nbsp;

By default cost of sales is not activated. You need to configure cost of sales as follows:&nbsp;

1. Cost of Sales journal - Create a cost of sales batch (journal) type (**Setup → [Batch types**](<batch-types-setup.md>)) to update the transactions for cost of sales to the ledger when you post documents. &nbsp;
1. Cost of sales settings (**Setup → [Stock information**](<stock-create-cost-of-sales.md>)) - Select the following:
- &nbsp;

  - Create cost of sales - To activate cost of sales, select (tick) the "*Create cost of sales*" tick box.
  - Cost of sales batch - Select the Cost of sales batch (journal) in which the cost of sales transactions will be posted.&nbsp;
  - Use “*Average cost*” or “*Latest cost*” option.&nbsp;

| ![Image](<lib/admon-important.png>) | *The method you use to calculate the value of your stock is crucial for determining its impact on your profit and loss figures. It is important to consider whether to use the “average cost” or the “latest cost” method.*&nbsp; *If you are unsure which method to use, it is recommended that you seek guidance from your accountant.* |
| --- | --- |


### Create tax classes / account classes &nbsp;

At this point, your chart of accounts (general ledger accounts) should have already been finalised.&nbsp;

You need to link each stock item / inventory item / product to the applicable ledger accounts (i.e. Input tax, Output tax, Sales account, Cost of sales account and Stock control account).&nbsp;

Tax classes may be set and linked to each category of tax and various tax rates such as Output tax, Input tax - Standard rate,&nbsp; Input tax - Capital goods, Zero rated goods, Exempt supplies applicable to the stock items / inventory items / products in your country and tax jurisdiction, etc.&nbsp;

Tax classes is defined and managed in the Tax classes tab in **Setup → [Stock information**](<stock-information-tax-classes.md>). When you create add or import your stock items / inventory items / products you may select a pre-configured Tax class to automatically link the 5 accounts (i.e. Input tax, Output tax, Sales account, Cost of sales account and Stock control account) applicable to the stock item / inventory item / product.&nbsp;

### Selling prices&nbsp;

osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 supports up to 3 selling prices (i.e. Selling price 1, Selling price 2 and Selling price 3). You may change the descriptions of these selling prices on the "Selling price descriptions" section of the Default tab of Setup → [Stock information](<stock-selling-price-descriptions.md>) to suit your specific requirements and set one of these Selling prices as the default Selling price (global setting) for your Set of Books.&nbsp;

In addition to this, you may set one of these Selling prices as the default Selling price on the [**Accounting information**](<debtor-tab-accounting-info.md>) tab for specific debtor (customer / client) accounts.&nbsp;
