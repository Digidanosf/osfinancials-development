# Activa Transactions and Reports

## Activa transactions&nbsp;

The transactions generated by the Activa plugin are as follows:

* **Purchase** - The purchase transactions can be processed in journals and documents. You must update (post) these transactions in documents to the general ledger. After that, you need to set up each asset's details correctly.
* **Depreciation** - The transactions for writing off transactions for depreciation (value reduction) are entered in the Activa journal or other unique journal that you have selected for the Activa plugin in the setup. These transactions are automatically calculated according to the details and percentages or amounts you specified for each asset. When these transactions are processed in the **Activa** plugin, this journal will automatically update to the ledger.

All updated transactions will be included in all reports for the specific fixed asset accounts, depreciation (reduction in value) accounts, creditors' (supplier's) accounts as well as Tax (VAT/GST/Sales tax)&nbsp; accounts.

## Other Report options

The **Activa** - **Report** - **Pivot** tab allows you to group, sort, filter the totals and fixed asset transactions generated with the **Activa** plugin according to your specific requirements.. You can also analyse your transactions in the [Pivot](<ledger-analyser-pivot.md>) functionality on the [Ledger analyser 1](<ledger-analyser.md>) or [Ledger analyser 2](<ledger-analyser.md>) to analyse your data and build advanced filters.

In addition to the reports on the [**Reports**](<reports-menu.md>) menu and [**User reports**](<user-reports-menu.md>) menu (**Reports** ribbon), documents and reports can also be found and printed on other functionalities in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5. Some of these options include:

* Ledger analyser 1 or Ledger analyser 2 - Trial Balance, Balance Sheet and Income Statement, etc. You can also use the Pivot functionality on the[Ledger analyser 1](<ledger-analyser.md>) or [Ledger analyser 2](<ledger-analyser.md>)&nbsp; to analyse your transactions and data You may build advanced&nbsp; custom filters.
* [**Search (Investigator)**](<search-investigator.md>) -

  * **Document type** - Print a document for purchase transactions.
  * **Batch type** Initiate the [T-Accounts](<t-account-viewer.md>). You can print your transactions for depreciation in the [T-Accounts](<t-account-viewer.md>). You can even print the transactions for a specific asset's accumulated depreciation (depreciation) or the creditors' (suppliers') accounts and even VAT accounts. In addition, you can filter the transactions per account over the period of the life of the assets and export them to a spreadsheet.

* Creditor (Supplier) accounts -

  * [Documents tab](<creditor-tab-documents.md>) - You can print specific documents for the purchase transactions.
  * [Transactions tab](<creditor-tab-transactions.md>) - For specific transactions, you can right-click to select the Show transactions context-menu option. This will display the selected transactions on the Transactions screen. You can then print the Posted journal transactions.
  * [Stock item tab](<creditor-tab-stock-items.md>) - This will list all the assets that have been purchased.

* **Stock items** - You can select a document on the [Document groups](<stock-item-tab-document-groups.md>) tab and print the document.
* [**Spreadsheet reports**](<spreadsheet-reports.md>) - You can display the [Transactions per period](<spreadsheet-reports-transactions.md>) or even for a period spanning the life of the assets and use the **Save as** options to open the transactions in your favourite spreadsheet program.
* [**T-Accounts**](<t-account-viewer.md>) - You can print your transactions for depreciation in the [T-Accounts](<t-account-viewer.md>). You can even print the transactions for a specific asset's accumulated depreciation (depreciation) or the creditors' (suppliers') accounts and even Tax (VAT/GST/Sales tax) accounts. In addition, you can filter the transactions per account over the period of the life of the assets and export them to a spreadsheet.

## Activa - Reports - Data tab - Processed transactions

The **Activa** - **Reports** - **Data** tab lists all purchase transactions of the Fixed assets added and processed as well as the write off transactions over the life span of Fixed assets.

![Image](<lib/activa-reports-data-tab.png>)

To view the details of an asset, expand a transaction, click on the ![Image](<lib/pivot-01.png>) icon and to collapse (hide) click on the ![Image](<lib/pivot-02.png>) icon.

## ![Image](<lib/activa-reports-data-tab-detail.png>)

The columns is as follows:&nbsp; &nbsp;

1. **Transaction type** - "*Purchase, Write off*" or "*End of life*". Purchase transactions is the assets at cost price. Write off transactions will be posted to the Accumulated depreciation (Balance sheet) account as a credit amount and to the Depreciation (Income statement) account type as a debit amount.
1. **Date** - The transaction date for the Purchase transaction. When write off transactions is processed it will be the date when the transactions on scheduled write off date is processed.
1. **Amount** - Purchases of Fixed assets&nbsp; Purchase transactions is the assets at cost price is a debit amount. Write off transactions will be posted to the Accumulated depreciation (Balance sheet) account as a credit amount and to the Depreciation (Income statement) account type as a debit amount. Credit amounts (prefixed by a - (minus sign)).&nbsp;
1. **Reference** - **Documents** - The document number on which the asset was purchased. When write off transactions is processed it will be the "*Reference*" automatically generated for the write off transactions. If more than one date is applicable to the transactions on scheduled write off date, it will generate the same reference number for all transactions with the same processing date and another reference number for another scheduled write off date.
1. **Description** - Transaction description **Documents** - The document number for the document type (i.e. Purchase and Supplier returns) and the description of the Asset item or description of the asset as entered in the "*Description*" column of the document lines.&nbsp;
1. **Account code** - The account code of the Asset account at cost for purchases of fixed assets and Asset accumulated depreciation account (Balance sheet) for write off transactions or Depreciation (Income Statement) account.
1. **Account description** - The account name or account description of the Asset account at cost for purchases of fixed assets and Asset accumulated depreciation account (Balance sheet) for write off transactions or Depreciation (Income Statement) account.
1. **Account type** - Balance sheet or Income statement.
1. **Rest amount** - The amounts entered for the scrap value (trade-in value) of the asset at the end of the asset's life cycle (in the "*Rest value*" field of the Asset's detail form.
