# Stock groups

***

In osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, you have the option to set up to two (2) reporting groups for your stock items or inventory items, allowing you to categorise and report on your products more effectively. These reporting groups provide a way to organize and analyse your inventory based on specific criteria.&nbsp;

These Stock groups can be linked to each stock item (inventory item / product).&nbsp;

# Examples of reporting groups for stock items / inventory items / products

Here are some examples of reporting groups that you can use for stock items:

1. **Product category**: You can create reporting groups based on the category or type of products you offer. For instance, if you sell electronics, you might have reporting groups like *"Computers," "Mobile Devices," "Audio Equipment,"* etc.
1. **Brand or Manufacturer**: Another option is to create reporting groups based on the brand or manufacturer of your products. This can be useful for tracking sales and performance by brand. For example, you could have reporting groups like *"Samsung," "Apple," "Sony,"* etc.
1. **Product line**: If you offer multiple product lines, you can create reporting groups to differentiate them. For instance, if you sell clothing, you might have reporting groups like "*Men's Apparel," "Women's Apparel," "Children's Apparel*," etc.
1. **Location**: Reporting groups based on the location of your products can be helpful for inventory management and sales analysis. This is particularly useful if you have multiple warehouses or stores. Examples of reporting groups could be *"Warehouse A," "Store Branch 1," "Online Store,"* etc.
1. **Sales channel**: If you sell your products through various channels, you can set up reporting groups to track sales performance across those channels. This could include groups like *"Retail Stores," "E-commerce," "Wholesale,"* etc.

These are just a few examples, and you can customise the reporting groups in osFinancials to fit your specific business needs. By assigning stock items to the appropriate reporting groups, you can generate reports that provide insights into your inventory, sales, and performance based on the defined categorisation.

# Define groups for stock items / inventory items / products

**To set reporting groups for stock items:**&nbsp;

1. On the **Setup** ribbon, select **Setup → Groups**. The "*Groups* (*Account group 1)"* default screen will be displayed.&nbsp;
1. Click on the **Stock group 1** button.\
![Image](<lib/setup-groups-stock.png>)
1. If you wish to rename the Reporting group name - Stock group 1, over-type the description with your new description. This will change the description of the button for Stock group 1.
1. To add a new Group to the root directory or list, select the "*REPORTING GROUP ROOT*" on the list and click on the **New** button.
1. Enter the name of your Stock group 1 and click on "*REPORTING GROUP ROOT*" on the list.&nbsp;
1. Repeat the process until you have entered all the Stock groups, etc.&nbsp;
