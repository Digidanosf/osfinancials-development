# Salespersons

***

When processing sales documents, such as invoices, credit notes, and quotes, as well as purchase documents, such as purchases, supplier returns, and orders, you can select the relevant salesperson. When printing various reports, you can view salespersons.&nbsp;

# Examples of reporting groups for salespersons

Some examples of groups that can be used for salespersons:

1. **Sales territories**: You can create reporting groups based on sales territories or geographical regions. For example, you can have reporting groups for North America, Europe, Asia-Pacific, etc. This allows you to track and compare the sales performance of different territories.
1. **Product lines**: Another option is to group salespersons based on the product lines they handle. You can create reporting groups for specific product categories or types, such as electronics, clothing, or furniture. This helps in analysing the sales performance of each product line and identifying top-performing salespersons for each category.
1. **Customer segments**: You can assign salespersons to reporting groups based on the customer segments they focus on. For instance, you can have reporting groups for retail customers, corporate clients, or specific industries like healthcare or technology. This allows you to evaluate the sales performance and effectiveness of salespersons in different customer segments.
1. **Sales teams**: If you have multiple sales teams within your organization, you can create reporting groups to represent each team. This helps in tracking the performance of individual sales teams and comparing their results. It also allows for effective team management and resource allocation.
1. **Sales channels**: If your salespersons operate through different channels, such as direct sales, online sales, or distribution partners, you can assign them to reporting groups based on these channels. This enables you to analyse the sales performance across different channels and assess the effectiveness of each salesperson in their respective channel.

These examples illustrate how reporting groups for salespersons in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 can be used to organize and analyse sales data. By assigning salespersons to appropriate groups, you can evaluate their performance, identify sales trends, and make data-driven decisions to drive business growth.

# Define reporting groups for salespersons

**To add / edit /delete salespersons:**&nbsp;

1. On the **Setup** ribbon, select **Setup → Groups**. The "*Groups* (*Account group 1)"* default screen will be displayed.
1. Select "*Salespersons*".&nbsp;

![Image](<lib/setup-groups-salespersons.png>)

| ![Image](<lib/admon-tip.png>) | *If you want to organize your salespersons based on different criteria such as sales territories, product lines, customer segments, sales teams, sales channels, or any other relevant factors, you can create groups to categorise them accordingly. By assigning salespersons to specific groups, you can achieve better organisation, management, and analysis of sales activities and performance. This segmentation enables you to easily track and evaluate the performance of salespersons within each group, helping you optimise sales strategies and make informed business decisions.* |
| --- | --- |


| ![Image](<lib/admon-tip.png>) | *You may set different sequences for each reporting group in which the groups and salespersons are listed on reports, etc. This is done by selecting the groups on the list and use the **Move up** or **Move down** buttons.* |
| --- | --- |


3. If you wish to rename the Reporting group name - Salespersons, over-type the description with your new description. This will change the description of the button for **Salespersons**.
3. To add a new Group to the root directory or list, select the "*REPORTING GROUP ROOT*" on the list and click on the **New** button.
3. Enter the name of your next salesperson and click on "*REPORTING GROUP ROOT*" on the list.&nbsp;
3. Repeat the process until you have entered all the Salespersons, etc.&nbsp;
3. Click on the **OK** button to save your changes and close (exit) this screen.
