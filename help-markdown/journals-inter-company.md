# Inter-company transactions journal - Is there examples of transactions to be recorded in this journal?

Inter-company transactions involve transactions between different entities or subsidiaries within the same organization. Here are some examples of transactions that could be recorded in an Inter-company transactions journal:

1. **Inter-company sales**: When one entity within the organization sells goods or services to another entity, the transaction should be recorded in the Inter-company transactions journal. This ensures that the sales revenue is properly recognized in the selling entity's financial records and the purchasing entity's accounts payable is appropriately recorded.
1. **Inter-company purchases**: Similarly, when one entity purchases goods or services from another entity within the organization, the transaction should be recorded in the Inter-Company Transactions Journal. This ensures that the purchasing entity's accounts payable is accurately reflected, and the selling entity's sales revenue is appropriately recorded.
1. **Inter-company loans**: If one entity lends funds to another entity within the organization, the loan transaction should be recorded in the Inter-company transactions journal. This helps track the borrowing entity's liability for the loan and the lending entity's receivable for the loan.
1. **Inter-company service charges**: If one entity provides services to another entity within the organization and charges a fee for those services, the service charge transaction should be recorded in the Inter-company transactions journal. This ensures that the service revenue is appropriately recognized and the purchasing entity's accounts payable is properly recorded.
1. **Inter-company asset transfers**: When assets are transferred between entities within the organization, such as equipment, machinery, or inventory, the transfer should be recorded in the Inter-company transactions journal. This helps track the movement of assets between entities and ensures proper accounting for the value of the transferred assets.

These are just a few examples of transactions that could be recorded in an Inter-Company Transactions Journal. The specific transactions will depend on the nature of the inter-company activities and the policies of your organization. By recording these transactions in a dedicated journal, you can accurately track inter-company balances, eliminate inter-company transactions during consolidation, and ensure proper financial reporting within your organization.
