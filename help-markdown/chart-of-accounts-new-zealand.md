# What is a suggested Chart of Accounts according to the New Zealand Chart of Accounts, based on the New Zealand International Financial Reporting Standards (NZ IFRS)?

The suggested Chart of Accounts for a business in New Zealand based on the New Zealand International Financial Reporting Standards (NZ IFRS) typically includes the following main categories:

1. **Assets**: This category includes accounts related to the resources that a business owns or controls, such as cash, accounts receivable, inventory, and property, plant, and equipment.
1. **Liabilities**: This category includes accounts related to the obligations that a business owes to others, such as accounts payable, loans, and other forms of debt.
1. **Equity**: This category includes accounts related to the ownership interest in a business, such as share capital and retained earnings.
1. **Income**: This category includes accounts related to the revenue earned by a business, such as sales revenue, interest income, and rental income.
1. **Expenses**: This category includes accounts related to the costs incurred by a business in generating revenue, such as cost of goods sold, operating expenses, and interest expenses.

Within each of these main categories, the accounts are usually identified by a three-digit code, with the first digit indicating the category and the last two digits indicating the specific account within the category.

The specific accounts included in the Chart of Accounts can vary depending on the nature of the business and its financial reporting requirements. It is important to consult with an accountant or bookkeeper to determine the most appropriate Chart of Accounts for your business.

# What is the recommended financial categories and account groups for these accounts in the New Zealand Chart of Accounts?

The New Zealand Chart of Accounts is a standardized list of financial accounts that businesses in New Zealand can use to organize their financial transactions. Here are some recommended financial categories and account groups that can be used in the New Zealand Chart of Accounts:

1. **Assets**: This category includes all the resources that a business owns and controls. It includes subcategories such as:
- &nbsp;

  - Current assets (e.g. cash, accounts receivable, inventory)
  - Fixed assets (e.g. property, plant, and equipment)
  - Intangible assets (e.g. patents, trademarks, goodwill)

2. **Liabilities**: This category includes all the financial obligations that a business owes to others. It includes subcategories such as:
- &nbsp;

  - Current liabilities (e.g. accounts payable, taxes payable, short-term loans)
  - Long-term liabilities (e.g. long-term loans, bonds, mortgages)

3. **Equity**: This category includes the owners' investment in the business. It includes subcategories such as:
- &nbsp;

  - Share capital (e.g. common shares, preferred shares)
  - Retained earnings (e.g. accumulated profits or losses)

4. **Income**: This category includes all the revenue earned by the business. It includes subcategories such as:
- &nbsp;

  - Sales revenue (e.g. revenue from the sale of goods or services)
  - Other income (e.g. interest income, rental income)

5. **Expenses**: This category includes all the costs incurred by the business. It includes subcategories such as:
- &nbsp;

  - Cost of goods sold (e.g. the cost of producing or acquiring the goods sold)
  - Operating expenses (e.g. rent, salaries, utilities)
  - Depreciation and amortization (e.g. the cost of using up fixed assets and intangible assets over time)

These financial categories and account groups can be further customized and refined to suit the specific needs of each business. # What accounts is suggested for payroll in New Zealand?

In New Zealand, the following accounts are commonly used to record payroll transactions in the chart of accounts:

1. **Salaries and wages**: This account is used to record the gross wages and salaries paid to employees.
1. **PAYE payable**: This account is used to record the amount of tax withheld from employee wages, which must be paid to the Inland Revenue Department (IRD) on behalf of the employee.
1. **KiwiSaver payable**: This account is used to record the employer's contributions to the employee's KiwiSaver account, which is a government-approved retirement savings plan.
1. **Employee loan payable**: This account is used to record any loans or advances made to employees.
1. **Employee deduction payable**: This account is used to record deductions from employee wages such as union dues, charitable donations, or voluntary insurance contributions.
1. **Holiday pay payable**: This account is used to record the amount of holiday pay owed to employees, which must be paid out when the employee takes a holiday or at the end of their employment.
1. **Accrued payroll**: This account is used to record any payroll expenses that have been incurred but not yet paid, such as salaries and wages for the current pay period that will be paid in the following pay period.
1. **Bank account**: This account is used to record the transactions related to the company's payroll bank account, including deposits and withdrawals for payroll-related expenses.

It is important to tailor the chart of accounts to the specific needs of the business and to ensure compliance with local regulations.
