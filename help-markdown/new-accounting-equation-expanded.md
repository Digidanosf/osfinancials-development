# Expanded accounting equation - Balance sheet and Income statement

***

The expanded accounting equation takes into account the impact of all transactions on assets, liabilities, capital, income, and expenses. When sales are made, assets such as cash in the bank or accounts receivable increase, along with income. Conversely, expenses like advertising decrease assets, such as cash in the bank, or increase liabilities, like accounts payable. Income increases profit while expenses decrease profit.

Given that most transactions affect both the income statement and balance sheet, it's necessary to look at the full accounting equation.&nbsp;

The expanded accounting equation is as follows:

| --- | :---: |


This equation must always be in balance for the financial statements to be accurate.

**Balance sheet**

The balance sheet is one of the main financial statements that presents the company's financial position at a specific point in time. It shows the assets, liabilities, and equity of the company, and the balance sheet is based on the accounting equation.

| ![Image](<lib/admon-scale.png>) | ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-capital.svg>) ***CAPITAL =*** ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-assets.svg>) ***ASSETS –*** ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-liabilities.svg>) ***LIABILITIES*** |
| --- | :---: |


**Income statement**

The income statement is another important financial statement that reports a company's financial performance over a period of time, typically one year. It summarizes the revenues, expenses, and net income of the company for that period.

The income statement is a crucial financial statement that reports a company's financial performance during a specified period, usually one year. It provides a summary of the company's revenues, expenses, and net income for that period.

The accounting equation plays a vital role in the income statement. The net income reported on the income statement is calculated by subtracting total expenses from total revenues. This net income is then added to the equity section of the balance sheet, affecting the retained earnings balance. Thus, the accounting equation, which states that assets = liabilities + equity, is also impacted by the income statement.

| ![Image](<lib/admon-scale.png>) | ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-retained-income.svg>) ***PROFIT =*** ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-income.svg>) ***INCOME –*** ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-expenses.svg>) ***EXPENSES*** |
| --- | :---: |


**Balance sheet**

The balance sheet and income statement are connected through the accounting equation. The net income reported on the income statement affects the equity section of the balance sheet by flowing into the retained earnings balance. Additionally, changes in assets and liabilities on the balance sheet are reflected in the income statement through revenues and expenses.

All transactions impact either assets, liabilities, capital, income, or expense items. Sales increase assets, such as bank or debtors, and income. On the other hand, expenses like advertisements decrease assets, such as cash in the bank, or increase liabilities, like creditors.

Income increases profit, while expenses decrease profit. Since most transactions affect both the income statement and the balance sheet, it's essential to consider the full accounting equation or expand the accounting equation.

**Expanded accounting equation**

The accounting equation will then be as follows:
