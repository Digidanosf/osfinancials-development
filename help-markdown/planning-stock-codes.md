# Planning stock codes

***

Stock items are essential for managing goods, services, and products. They are used when processing sales documents such as invoices, credit notes and quotes as well as purchase documents such as purchases, supplier returns and orders.&nbsp;

Planning the structure of stock codes is important for efficient stock control. A well-organised structure based on the layout of stores and business needs will simplify the management of stock items.

Careful planning of stock codes can simplify the processing of documents, such as selecting stock items for stock lookups, and for point-of-sale invoices by selecting stock items on the POS lookup. Additionally, having a structured stock code system can aid in organizing and generating reports, such as sales analysis and purchase analysis reports, when the stock code sequence is selected.

In osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, there are several options available to optimize the control and management of stock items; i.e.:

* [**Stock item type**](<stock-item-types.md>) – You may create up to eleven (11) stock types, with "*Default*" being the standard type used for trading stock.&nbsp;
* **Stock code** –&nbsp; Use up to 15 alpha-numerical characters. You may also change the Stock code when you edit existing Stock items.
* **Reporting groups** - You may set up to two (2) [reporting groups](<stock-item-reporting-groups.md>) for your stock items (products). Stock groups allows you to group or categorise the stock items (products) on which you would like to report. These stock groups can be linked to each stock item (product) for better reporting, analysing and managing of your inventory (products).&nbsp;
* **Sub-accounts** -&nbsp; Sub-accounts can also be created for sales, cost of sales, and stock control accounts and linked to each stock item.&nbsp;
