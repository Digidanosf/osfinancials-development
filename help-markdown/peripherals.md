# What is peripherals?

Peripherals, in the context of computers and technology, refer to external devices or components that are connected to a computer or other main computing device to expand its capabilities or functionality. These devices serve specific purposes and often complement the central processing unit (CPU) or main system. Peripherals are typically connected to the computer through various ports such as USB, HDMI, or Bluetooth. They can be easily added or removed as needed.

Here are some common examples of peripherals:

1. **Keyboard**: A device used to input text and commands into a computer.
1. **Mouse**: A pointing device used to navigate and interact with graphical user interfaces.
1. **Monitor**: A display device that shows the output from the computer, allowing users to see information and perform tasks.
1. **Printer**: An output device that produces physical copies of documents or images from the computer.
1. **Scanner**: An input device used to convert physical documents or images into digital formats that can be stored and manipulated on a computer.
1. **External Hard Drive**: An additional storage device that is connected to a computer to expand its storage capacity.
1. **Speakers**: Output devices used to produce sound or audio from the computer.
1. **Headphones**: Audio output devices worn over the ears to listen to audio privately.
1. **Webcam**: A camera device used for video conferencing, live streaming, or recording video content on a computer.
1. **Microphone**: An input device used to capture audio and record voice or other sound inputs.
1. **External Optical Drive**: An external device used to read CDs, DVDs, or Blu-ray discs.
1. **USB Flash Drive**: A portable storage device that connects to the computer's USB port to transfer and store data.
1. **Graphics Tablet**: An input device used by artists and designers to draw directly on the surface of the tablet and have the drawing mirrored on the computer screen.

Peripherals are essential for enhancing the usability and functionality of computers and other devices, allowing users to interact with and utilize technology effectively.

# Peripherals for POS systems

Point of Sale (POS) systems are used in various businesses, such as retail stores, restaurants, and hospitality establishments, to process transactions and manage sales. Peripherals for POS systems are external devices that enhance the capabilities of the system and enable efficient and seamless operations. Some common peripherals used with POS systems include:

1. **Barcode Scanner**: A barcode scanner reads barcodes on products, allowing quick and accurate item identification during the checkout process. This speeds up transactions and reduces the risk of manual entry errors.
1. **Receipt Printer**: A receipt printer generates printed receipts for customers after a transaction is completed. It provides a tangible record of the purchase and can include details like item names, prices, taxes, and payment method.
1. **Cash Drawer**: A cash drawer is used to store cash and coins securely during transactions. It is typically connected to the POS system and can be automatically opened after a sale to provide change to customers.
1. **Card Reader or Payment Terminal**: This peripheral allows customers to make card payments, including credit and debit cards. Modern card readers may also support contactless and mobile payment methods like Apple Pay or Google Pay.
1. **Customer Display**: A customer display is a screen facing the customer that shows transaction details like the total amount, items purchased, and payment options. It helps customers verify the transaction before completing the payment.
1. **Pole Display**: A pole display is a similar display to the customer display but is mounted on a pole and often faces the cashier or salesperson. It shows transaction details for verification before finalizing the sale.
1. **Kitchen Display System (KDS)**: In restaurants or food service businesses, a KDS allows the kitchen staff to receive and manage orders electronically. It helps streamline order preparation and reduces the need for paper tickets.
1. **Kitchen Printer**: Alternatively to a KDS, a kitchen printer can be used to print order tickets in the kitchen. It provides a physical copy of the order to the kitchen staff for preparation.
1. **Scale**: In businesses that sell products by weight, such as grocery stores or delis, a scale integrated with the POS system ensures accurate pricing and automatic calculation of the total cost.
1. **Mobile Devices**: Some POS systems support mobile devices like tablets or smartphones, allowing employees to process sales and take orders anywhere on the premises.

These peripherals work in conjunction with the POS software to create a complete and efficient point of sale system. Businesses can choose the peripherals that best suit their specific needs and industry requirements to improve customer service and enhance operational efficiency.
