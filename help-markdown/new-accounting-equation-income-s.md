# Accounting equation - Income statement

***

The income statement is a financial statement that shows all the income and expense accounts of a business, which are also called nominal accounts. It is divided into two main financial categories: income accounts and expense accounts. It consists of two basic Financial categories:

| ***Financial category*** | ***What do they tell us?*** | ***Examples of accounts*** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-income.svg>) *Income accounts* | *Income accounts represent the sources of income for the business.* | ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-income-expense.svg>) *Sales* ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-income-expense.svg>) *Interest received* |
| ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-expenses.svg>) *Expense accounts*&nbsp; | *On the other hand, expense accounts reflect the expenses incurred by the business in order to generate income.* | ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-income-expense.svg>) *Motor vehicle expenses* ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-income-expense.svg>) *Rent* ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-income-expense.svg>) *Telephone* ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-income-expense.svg>) *Salaries and wages* ![Image](<lib/admon-material-accounts-income-expense.svg>) *Water and Lights* |


The difference between the total of the income and the total of the expense accounts will reflect how much profit/loss the business made.

The profit is the return or remuneration the owner or owners of the business receives on their investment in the business.&nbsp; This difference&nbsp; (Profit / Loss) is transferred to the Balance sheet (Retained income / earnings account) as it will increase the owner / owners equity (capital interest) in the business.
