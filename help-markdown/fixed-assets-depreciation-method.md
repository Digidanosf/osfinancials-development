# What is the common methods used to calculate depreciation?

There are several common methods used to calculate depreciation. The choice of method depends on factors such as the nature of the asset, its expected pattern of use, and the reporting entity's accounting policies. Here are some commonly used depreciation methods:

1. **Straight-line depreciation**: This is the most straightforward and commonly used method. It evenly distributes the cost of an asset over its estimated useful life. The formula for straight-line depreciation is:

*Depreciation Expense = (Cost of Asset - Residual Value) / Useful Life*

The depreciable amount is allocated equally to each period, resulting in a consistent depreciation expense over the asset's useful life.

2. **Declining balance depreciation**: Also known as accelerated depreciation, this method assigns higher depreciation expenses in the earlier years and gradually reduces them over time. Two common declining balance methods are:
1) &nbsp;

   1) **Double declining balance (DDB)**: This method applies a fixed rate of depreciation (typically twice the straight-line rate) to the asset's net book value each year. The formula for DDB depreciation is:

*Depreciation Expense = Net Book Value \* Depreciation Rate*

The net book value is reduced by the depreciation expense each year until it reaches the asset's residual value.

1. &nbsp;
   2. **Sum-of-years-digits (SYD)**: This method considers the asset's useful life and allocates higher depreciation in the earlier years. The formula for SYD depreciation is:

*Depreciation Expense = (Remaining Useful Life / Sum of Years) \* (Cost of Asset - Residual Value)*

The sum of years is calculated by adding the digits of the asset's useful life (e.g., for a 5-year useful life: 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 = 15).

3. **Units of production depreciation**: This method is based on the actual usage or production output of the asset. It calculates depreciation based on the number of units produced, hours worked, or any other suitable production measure. The formula for units of production depreciation is:

*Depreciation Expense per Unit = (Cost of Asset - Residual Value) / Total Units of Production Depreciation Expense = Depreciation Expense per Unit \* Units Produced or Utilized*

The depreciation expense varies based on the level of asset utilization or production activity.

These are just a few examples of commonly used depreciation methods. Other specialized methods, such as group depreciation or component depreciation, may also be used in specific situations. It's important to select a depreciation method that best aligns with the nature of the asset, applicable accounting standards, and relevant regulations.
