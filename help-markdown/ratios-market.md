# Market Ratios

***

Market ratios, also known as valuation ratios or market multiples, are financial metrics that help investors and analysts assess a company's valuation and its attractiveness as an investment. These ratios use the company's stock market price and other market data to evaluate its performance and potential. Some common market ratios include:

1. [**Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E Ratio)**](<ratios-price-earnings.md>): The Price-to-Earnings Ratio compares a company's stock price with its earnings per share (EPS). The formula is:

***P/E Ratio** = Stock Price / Earnings per Share*

A higher P/E ratio typically indicates that investors are willing to pay more for each dollar of earnings, suggesting a higher expectation for future growth or performance.

2. [**Price-to-Book Ratio (P/B Ratio)**](<ratios-price-book.md>): The Price-to-Book Ratio compares a company's stock price with its book value per share, which represents the net asset value of the company. The formula is:

***P/B Ratio** = Stock Price / Book Value per Share*

A higher P/B ratio suggests that the stock is trading at a premium relative to its book value, indicating positive investor sentiment.

3. [**Dividend Yield**](<ratios-dividend-yield.md>): The Dividend Yield measures the percentage of dividends paid per share relative to the stock's current market price. The formula is:

***Dividend Yield** = Annual Dividend per Share / Stock Price*

A higher dividend yield indicates a higher return on investment from dividends.

4. [**Earnings per Share (EPS)**](<ratios-earnings-per-share.md>): Earnings per Share represents the company's net earnings available to common shareholders divided by the average number of outstanding shares. It is often used as a component in other market ratios.

***EPS** = Net Earnings / Average Number of Outstanding Shares*

Higher EPS typically indicates better profitability on a per-share basis.

5. [**Price-to-Sales Ratio (P/S Ratio)**](<ratios-price-sales.md>): The Price-to-Sales Ratio compares a company's stock price with its revenue per share. The formula is:

***P/S Ratio** = Stock Price / Revenue per Share*

A higher P/S ratio suggests that investors are willing to pay more for each dollar of revenue, indicating positive market sentiment.

6. [**Market Capitalization**](<ratios-market-capitalization.md>): Market Capitalization represents the total value of a company's outstanding shares of stock. It is calculated by multiplying the stock price by the number of outstanding shares.

***Market Capitalization** = Stock Price × Number of Outstanding Shares*

Market capitalization provides an indication of the company's size in the market.

These market ratios provide valuable information to investors, helping them assess a company's relative value, growth potential, and risk. However, it's essential to use these ratios in conjunction with other financial metrics and industry analysis to make well-informed investment decisions.&nbsp;

Additionally, market ratios may vary significantly among companies and industries, so it's essential to compare them against industry peers and historical data for a more accurate assessment.
