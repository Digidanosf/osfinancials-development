# Sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for a South African Manufacturing business?

A Chart of Accounts (COA) is a structured list of all the accounts and categories used to organize a company's financial transactions. For a South African manufacturing business, the COA needs to reflect the specific needs and requirements of the industry and the country's accounting practices. Here's a sample outline of a Chart of Accounts for a South African manufacturing business:

1. Assets&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;.1 Current Assets&nbsp;

    - &#49;.1.1 Cash and Cash Equivalents&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.2 Accounts Receivable&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.3 Inventory (Raw materials, work in progress, finished goods)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.4 Prepaid Expenses&nbsp;
    - &#49;.1.5 Other Current Assets

  - &#49;.2 Non-Current Assets&nbsp;

    - &#49;.2.1 Property, Plant, and Equipment (Land, Buildings, Machinery, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.2 Intangible Assets (Patents, Trademarks, Software, etc.)&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.3 Long-Term Investments&nbsp;
    - &#49;.2.4 Other Non-Current Assets

2. Liabilities&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#50;.1 Current Liabilities&nbsp;

    - &#50;.1.1 Accounts Payable&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.2 Short-Term Loans and Borrowings&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.3 Accrued Liabilities&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.4 Taxes Payable&nbsp;
    - &#50;.1.5 Other Current Liabilities

  - &#50;.2 Non-Current Liabilities&nbsp;

    - &#50;.2.1 Long-Term Loans and Borrowings&nbsp;
    - &#50;.2.2 Deferred Tax Liabilities&nbsp;
    - &#50;.2.3 Other Non-Current Liabilities

3. Equity&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#51;.1 Share Capital&nbsp;

    - &#51;.1.1 Ordinary Shares&nbsp;
    - &#51;.1.2 Preference Shares&nbsp;

  - &#51;.2 Retained Earnings&nbsp;
  - &#51;.3 Other Equity Items

4. Income&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#52;.1 Sales Revenue&nbsp;
  - &#52;.2 Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.3 Gross Profit&nbsp;
  - &#52;.4 Operating Revenue (if applicable)&nbsp;
  - &#52;.5 Operating Expenses&nbsp;

    - &#52;.5.1 Selling and Marketing Expenses&nbsp;
    - &#52;.5.2 General and Administrative Expenses&nbsp;
    - &#52;.5.3 Research and Development Expenses&nbsp;

  - &#52;.6 Operating Profit&nbsp;
  - &#52;.7 Other Income (e.g., interest income, rental income)

5. Expenses&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#53;.1 Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)&nbsp;

    - &#53;.1.1 Direct Materials&nbsp;
    - &#53;.1.2 Direct Labor&nbsp;
    - &#53;.1.3 Factory Overhead&nbsp;

  - &#53;.2 Selling and Marketing Expenses&nbsp;

    - &#53;.2.1 Advertising and Promotion&nbsp;
    - &#53;.2.2 Sales Commissions&nbsp;

  - &#53;.3 General and Administrative Expenses&nbsp;

    - &#53;.3.1 Salaries and Wages&nbsp;
    - &#53;.3.2 Rent and Utilities&nbsp;
    - &#53;.3.3 Office Supplies&nbsp;

  - &#53;.4 Research and Development Expenses&nbsp;
  - &#53;.5 Other Operating Expenses

6. Cost of Sales
6. Taxation&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#55;.1 Current Tax&nbsp;
  - &#55;.2 Deferred Tax

8. Other Comprehensive Income&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#56;.1 Foreign Currency Translation Gains/Losses&nbsp;
  - &#56;.2 Unrealized Gains/Losses on Investments&nbsp;
  - &#56;.3 Other Items

9. Gains and Losses&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#57;.1 Gain on Sale of Assets&nbsp;
  - &#57;.2 Loss on Impairment of Assets

10. Intercompany Transactions&nbsp;
- &nbsp;

  - &#49;0.1 Intercompany Revenue&nbsp;
  - &#49;0.2 Intercompany Expenses

11. Dividends
11. Subsidiary and Associate Companies

Please note that the above COA is a general outline and can be tailored to meet the specific needs and reporting requirements of the manufacturing business. Additionally, it's essential to consult with a qualified accountant or financial expert to ensure compliance with South African accounting standards and regulations.
