# What is VAT MOSS?

VAT MOSS stands for "*VAT Mini One Stop Shop*." It is a scheme introduced by the European Union (EU) to simplify the process of VAT (Value Added Tax) compliance for businesses that supply certain digital services to customers in multiple EU member states.

Prior to the introduction of VAT MOSS, businesses that provided digital services (e.g., telecommunications, broadcasting, and electronically supplied services) to customers in different EU countries had to register for VAT in each member state where their customers were located. This resulted in significant administrative burdens and compliance complexities for such businesses.

With VAT MOSS, eligible businesses can register for the scheme in their home EU member state and submit a single quarterly VAT return that covers all their sales to customers in other EU countries. The tax authorities in the home member state will then distribute the VAT collected to the relevant member states where the customers are located.

Key points about VAT MOSS:

1. **Applicability**: VAT MOSS is applicable to businesses established within the EU that supply digital services to consumers (non-business customers) in other EU member states.
1. **Digital Services**: The scheme covers a range of digital services, such as streaming or downloading of videos, music, e-books, online courses, software, mobile applications, and other electronically supplied services.
1. **Single VAT Return**: Instead of registering for VAT in multiple member states, businesses registered for VAT MOSS can submit a single quarterly VAT return and payment to their home member state.
1. **VAT Rates**: The VAT rates used under VAT MOSS are those of the member states where the services are consumed by customers.
1. **Thresholds**: Businesses using VAT MOSS must comply with the distance selling thresholds of the EU member states they are supplying to. Once the threshold in a specific member state is exceeded, the business must charge the VAT rate of that member state on subsequent sales to customers in that country.

VAT MOSS is intended to simplify the VAT compliance process for digital service providers, reduce administrative burdens, and improve cross-border trade within the EU. It allows businesses to deal with their VAT obligations in a more efficient manner, without the need to register for VAT in multiple countries where they have customers.

# VAT MOSS - Structure of VAT Accounts in the Chart of Accounts

The structure of VAT accounts in the Chart of Accounts for businesses using the VAT MOSS scheme will typically include specific accounts to track the VAT on digital services provided to consumers in different EU member states. Here is a possible structure of VAT accounts in the Chart of Accounts for businesses using VAT MOSS:

1. **VAT MOSS Control Account**: This is the main VAT MOSS control account that tracks the net VAT payable or refundable under the scheme. It represents the total VAT collected on digital services provided to customers in other EU member states and the total VAT owed to the respective tax authorities of those member states.
1. **VAT MOSS Sales - Standard Rate**: This account records the VAT charged on digital services provided to consumers in other EU member states at the standard rate. It represents the VAT collected on such sales and is payable to the respective tax authorities.
1. **VAT MOSS Sales - Reduced Rate**: If applicable, this account records the VAT charged on digital services provided at a reduced rate to consumers in specific EU member states that have reduced VAT rates for certain services.
1. **VAT MOSS Sales - Zero Rate**: This account records the VAT charged on digital services that are subject to a zero rate in certain EU member states.
1. **VAT MOSS Input Tax**: This account tracks the VAT incurred on expenses directly related to the supply of digital services under the VAT MOSS scheme. It includes input tax on costs such as software licenses, digital content, and other services purchased for the provision of the digital services.
1. **VAT MOSS Adjustments**: This account is used to record any adjustments or corrections to previously reported VAT amounts under the VAT MOSS scheme. It may include adjustments for bad debts, errors, or changes in accounting methods.
1. **VAT MOSS Refunds**: If applicable, this account records any VAT refunds received from other EU member states under the VAT MOSS scheme.

It's important to note that the specific structure of VAT accounts may vary depending on the individual business's needs and the requirements of the tax authorities in the relevant EU member states. Businesses using VAT MOSS should maintain accurate and detailed records of their digital services sales and purchases to ensure compliance with the scheme's reporting and record-keeping requirements. Consulting with a tax professional or advisor familiar with VAT MOSS and EU VAT regulations can help businesses set up the appropriate Chart of Accounts and ensure proper VAT compliance.
