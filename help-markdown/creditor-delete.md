# Delete Creditor accounts

***

You may delete a creditor (supplier / vendor) account if the account has no transactions and/or balances.&nbsp;

You may disable a creditor (supplier / vendor) account to block (prevent) posting of any documents or batch transactions for the creditor (supplier / vendor). You may need to do this, if you are no longer doing business with the creditor (supplier / vendor), etc.

# Delete accounts

**To delete a Creditor (supplier / vendor) account:**

1. On the Creditor (supplier / vendor) accounts, select the account.

| ![Image](<lib/admon-tip.png>) | *If you wish to delete more than one (1) creditor (supplier / vendor) account at a time, press the **Ctrl** key and keep it down while selecting the other creditor (supplier / vendor) accounts to delete with a mouse click.* |
| --- | --- |


2. Click on the **Delete** button. A confirmation message is displayed:

*&nbsp;"Are you sure you want to delete this account?"*

| ![Image](<lib/admon-warning.png>) | *Make sure absolutely sure that the correct creditor (supplier / vendor) account is selected.* |
| --- | --- |


3. Click on the **Yes** button. A confirmation message is displayed:&nbsp;

*"Account deleted\!"*

4. Click on the **OK** button. This will permanently remove the creditor (supplier / vendor) account.&nbsp;

# Disable accounts

**To disable a Creditor (supplier / vendor) account:**

1. If the creditor (supplier / vendor) account has transactions and/or balances, you cannot delete it. A similar error message is displayed:

*"2AONES A-ONE Suppliers*&nbsp; \
*This account has balances, cannot be deleted\!"*

2. Click on the **OK** button. To block posting of any documents or batch transactions for the creditor (supplier / vendor), click on the **Edit** button and **Accounting information** tab.&nbsp;

![Image](<lib/accounting-information-disabled-setting.png>)

3. Select (tick) the "*Account disabled*" field.
3. Click on the **Save** button.&nbsp; You may delete a creditor (supplier / vendor) account if the account has no transactions and/or balances.&nbsp;
