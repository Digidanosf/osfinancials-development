# Is there specific accounting principles that apply to IFRS full and IFRS for SMEs?

There are specific accounting principles that apply to both IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards) and IFRS for SMEs (International Financial Reporting Standards for Small and Medium-sized Entities). While there is significant overlap between the two frameworks, IFRS for SMEs includes simplified accounting principles specifically designed for smaller entities.

Some of the specific accounting principles that apply to IFRS and IFRS for SMEs include:

1. **Accrual basis of accounting**: Both frameworks require the recognition of transactions and events when they occur, rather than when cash is received or paid.
1. **Substance over form**: Transactions are accounted for based on their underlying economic substance rather than merely their legal form.
1. **Fair presentation and Compliance with standards**: Financial statements should present a true and fair view of the entity's financial position, performance, and cash flows in accordance with the specific requirements of the applicable framework.
1. **Going concern**: Financial statements are prepared on the assumption that the entity will continue its operations for the foreseeable future, unless there is evidence to the contrary.
1. **Prudence**: This principle encourages the exercise of caution in uncertain circumstances and the exercise of professional judgment in making estimates and provisions.
1. **Materiality**: Financial information should be presented and disclosed based on its relevance and importance to the decision-making needs of the users of the financial statements.
1. **Cost principle**: Assets are initially recognized and measured at their historical cost, although certain exceptions exist for specific types of assets.
1. **Substance of Non-exchange transactions**: Non-exchange transactions, such as government grants or donations, are accounted for based on their substance and the related conditions.

These are just a few examples of the accounting principles that apply to IFRS and IFRS for SMEs. The specific set of principles and requirements within each framework are more comprehensive and detailed, providing guidance on various aspects of accounting and financial reporting.
