# What is the double-entry accounting system?

The double-entry accounting system is a method of recording financial transactions that has been used for centuries by businesses, accountants, and bookkeepers. It is based on the principle that every financial transaction has two equal and opposite effects on the accounting equation, which must be recorded in two accounts.

Under the double-entry accounting system, every transaction is recorded in at least two accounts: a debit account and a credit account. Debits and credits must always balance, meaning that the total amount debited must equal the total amount credited.

For example, let's say that a company receives $1,000 in cash from a customer for a product sale. The double entry accounting system would record this transaction as follows:

* The company's Cash account is debited for $1,000, which increases the Cash balance.&nbsp;
* The company's Sales Revenue account is credited for $1,000, which increases the Sales Revenue balance.

In this example, the debits and credits are equal and balance each other out, which ensures that the company's accounting equation remains in balance.

The double entry accounting system is important because it provides a systematic and accurate way to record financial transactions and generate financial statements. It allows businesses to track their financial performance, monitor their cash flow, and make informed financial decisions based on reliable data.
