# Document groups

***

You can set up to two (2) reporting groups for your documents, which can categorise them based on their types, such as invoices, credit notes, quotes, purchases, supplier returns, and orders. These document groups can be linked to each document for further reporting and analysis.&nbsp;

These Document groups can be linked to each document.&nbsp;

# Examples of reporting groups for sales documents

Some examples of reporting groups for sales documents (quotes, invoices and credit notes:

1. **Sales regions**: You can use reporting groups to categorize sales documents based on different sales regions. For example, you can create reporting groups for regions such as North America, Europe, Asia-Pacific, etc. This allows you to analyse and compare sales performance across different regions.
1. **Sales representatives**: Reporting groups can also be used to track sales documents by sales representatives or sales teams. Each sales representative can be assigned to a specific reporting group, enabling you to evaluate individual sales performance or team performance.
1. **Product categories**: Another way to utilize reporting groups is by grouping sales documents based on product categories. You can create reporting groups for categories like electronics, apparel, furniture, etc. This helps in analysing sales patterns and identifying the performance of different product categories.
1. **Customer types**: Reporting groups can be used to differentiate sales documents based on customer types. For instance, you can create reporting groups for retail customers, wholesale customers, corporate clients, etc. This allows you to assess sales trends and profitability based on customer segments.
1. **Sales channels**: If your business operates through multiple sales channels, reporting groups can help classify sales documents accordingly. You can create reporting groups for channels like online sales, retail stores, distribution partners, etc. This enables you to monitor and compare sales performance across different channels.

These are just a few examples of how you can utilize reporting groups for sales documents in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5. By assigning documents to appropriate reporting groups, you can gain insights into various aspects of your sales operations and make informed business decisions.

# Examples of reporting groups for purchase documents

Some examples of reporting groups for purchase documents (orders, purchases, and supplier returns):

1. **Supplier categories**: You can create reporting groups based on the categories or types of suppliers. For example, you can have reporting groups for raw material suppliers, equipment suppliers, or service providers. This helps in analysing and comparing the purchasing activities and performance for different supplier categories.
1. **Product categories**: Another option is to group purchase documents based on the product categories or types. You can create reporting groups for specific product lines or categories, such as electronics, office supplies, or machinery. This allows you to track the purchasing patterns and expenses associated with each product category.
1. **Approval levels**: If your organization follows an approval hierarchy for purchase documents, you can create reporting groups based on different approval levels. For example, you can have reporting groups for purchases approved by managers, directors, or executives. This helps in monitoring the approval process and identifying bottlenecks or delays in purchasing activities.
1. **Purchase regions**: If your organization operates in multiple regions or countries, you can assign purchase documents to reporting groups based on the regions or countries of the suppliers. This allows you to analyse and compare the purchasing activities and expenses across different regions.

These examples demonstrate how reporting groups for purchase documents in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5 can be used to categorize and analyse purchasing data. By assigning purchase documents to appropriate groups, you can track expenses, monitor supplier performance, and make informed decisions regarding purchasing strategies and cost management.

# Define reporting groups

**To set reporting groups for Documents:**&nbsp;

1. On the **Setup** ribbon, select **Setup → Groups**. The "*Groups* (*Account group 1)"* default screen will be displayed.
1. Click on the **Document group 1** button. \
![Image](<lib/setup-groups-documents.png>)
1. If you wish to rename the Reporting group name - Document group 1, over-type the description with your new description. This will change the description of the button for Document group 1.
1. To add a new Group to the root directory or list, select the "*REPORTING GROUP ROOT*" on the list and click on the **New** button.
1. Enter the name of your Document group 1 and click on "*REPORTING GROUP ROOT*" on the list.&nbsp;
