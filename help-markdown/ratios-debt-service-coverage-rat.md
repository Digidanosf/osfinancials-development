# Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)

***

The Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) is a financial metric used to assess a company's ability to meet its debt service obligations, including both principal and interest payments. It is a critical measure for creditors and lenders as it indicates the company's capacity to generate sufficient cash flow to service its debt.

The formula for calculating the Debt Service Coverage Ratio is as follows:

***DSCR** = (Net Operating Income + Depreciation \& Amortization) / Total Debt Service*

**Where**:

* &nbsp;
  * **Net Operating Income (NOI)** refers to the company's operating income after deducting operating expenses, but before deducting interest and taxes. It represents the cash generated from the company's core operations.
  * **Depreciation \& Amortization** are non-cash expenses that are added back to NOI since they do not involve cash outflows.
  * **Total Debt Service** includes all principal and interest payments that the company is required to make on its outstanding debt during a specific period.

The Debt Service Coverage Ratio measures the number of times a company's operating income (including non-cash expenses) can cover its debt service payments. A DSCR greater than 1 indicates that the company generates enough cash flow to comfortably cover its debt obligations, which suggests a lower risk of default.

For example:

* **A DSCR of 1.5** means that the company's cash flow is 1.5 times greater than its total debt service, implying a comfortable margin of safety.
* **A DSCR of less than 1** suggests that the company's cash flow is not sufficient to meet its debt obligations fully, indicating a higher risk of default.

Lenders and creditors typically require a minimum DSCR when evaluating a company's creditworthiness. The required DSCR may vary depending on the industry, the type of loan or credit facility, and the perceived risk associated with the borrower.

A high DSCR is generally favorable, indicating a strong ability to manage debt, while a low DSCR raises concerns about the company's ability to meet its debt obligations and may affect its borrowing capacity and interest rates.

The Debt Service Coverage Ratio is a critical financial metric used in loan underwriting, credit analysis, and investment decisions. However, it should be used alongside other financial ratios and performance indicators to gain a comprehensive understanding of a company's financial health and debt-servicing capability.
