# Provision for bad debts - Calculation methods

***

After calculating the amount using your preferred method, the next step is to create a provision for bad debts. In subsequent years or accounting periods, you will need to adjust the provision by either increasing or decreasing it based on the circumstances.

Adjusting the provision for bad debts allows for the recognition of changes in the expected collectability of outstanding debts. If the creditworthiness of debtors deteriorates or there are indications that certain debts may not be recoverable, it may be necessary to increase the provision. On the other hand, if there are improvements in the collectability of outstanding debts, a decrease in the provision may be warranted.

Regularly reviewing and adjusting the provision for bad debts ensures that it accurately reflects the potential losses from uncollectible debts. This adjustment is essential for reporting purposes and helps maintain the integrity of your financial statements.

# Method 1: Calculation based on Age analysis schedules

To calculate the provision for bad debts, you can begin by analysing the outstanding balances of your debtors based on the ageing periods for which these debts are outstanding. This analysis requires age analysis schedules or an age analysis report.

In osFinancials5/TurboCASH5, the ageing analysis report is particularly useful if you utilize the open item feature for debtor accounts and have resolved any disputes and correctly linked transactions.

Follow these steps to calculate the provision using the Debtors Age analysis reports in osFinancials5/TurboCASH5:

1. In osFinancials, navigate to **Age analysis** on the **Reports** ribbon or the **Open item link** option (**Due** tab) on the **Default** ribbon. You may also print age analysis reports from the **Reports → Debtors → Age analysis** menu.
1. Select all your debtors and set the Date of ageing as the last day of the period you want to use for the totals, typically the last day of your financial year.
1. Click the **Print** or **OK** buttons to generate the Debtors age analysis report.

The report will display the outstanding balances categorised by ageing periods, such as current, 30 days, 60 days, and 90 days. Each balance should be considered individually, with particular attention given to accounts with the longest outstanding balances. Calculating the percentages of the ageing periods in relation to the total transactions can provide guidelines for your calculations.

Based on this age analysis, you can identify doubtful debts. The list of doubtful debts will then be used to create or adjust the provision for bad debts.

If you have accounting records from a previous system that do not include debtor accounts for each outstanding period in osFinancials, you can refer to your old accounting records to find the Debtors Age analysis schedule (summary).

# Method 2: Calculation based on Credit sales

This method is useful when you have identified a trend, such as an average of 2% of the total credit sales over the past two years being bad debts. To calculate the provision for bad debts using this method, multiply the total credit sales for a specific financial year or accounting period by the estimated percentage. For example, if the total credit sales amount to $100,000, the provision for bad debts would be $2,000 (2% of $100,000).

Please note that this method provides a simple calculation or estimation for the provision for bad debts but does not consider the specific amounts owed by individual debtors.

This calculation for doubtful debts will then be used to create or adjust the provision for bad debts.

# Method 3: Calculation based on Total outstanding debt

You can also use the summarised information from the age analysis schedule or report to calculate or estimate the provision for bad debts.

Take the list of outstanding debt totals for each period and add an estimated percentage for the bad debts likely to be irrecoverable. These percentages are typically determined based on trends of past bad debts written off for debts in each of these ageing periods.

For example, if the trend shows 1% for the current period, 2% for debts older than 30 days, 5% for debts older than 60 days, and approximately 10% of outstanding debts are likely to become irrecoverable, the provision for bad debts would amount to $800.

Please keep in mind that these percentages may vary depending on the nature of your business, and implementing better credit control and debt collection practices can help reduce these percentages.

This calculation for doubtful debts will then be used to create or adjust the provision for bad debts.
