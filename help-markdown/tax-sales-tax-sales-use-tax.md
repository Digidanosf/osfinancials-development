# What is the difference between a sales tax and use tax in the USA?

In the USA, the main difference between a sales tax and a use tax lies in who is responsible for paying the tax and when it is collected. Although they both pertain to the taxation of goods and services, they are applied in different scenarios:

1. **Sales Tax**:
- &nbsp;

  - Sales tax is a tax imposed on the sale of tangible personal property and certain services.
  - The responsibility for collecting and remitting the sales tax lies with the seller.
  - The seller adds the sales tax to the total purchase price and collects it from the buyer at the time of the transaction.
  - The sales tax rate is based on the location of the seller and the buyer (destination-based or origin-based, depending on the state).
  - Sales tax is applicable to purchases made within the state where the seller has a physical presence or nexus.
  - The funds collected by the seller are remitted to the state's tax authority to fund public services and operations.

2. **Use Tax**:
- &nbsp;

  - Use tax is a tax imposed on the use, consumption, or storage of tangible personal property and certain services purchased from an out-of-state seller or from a seller who does not collect sales tax.
  - The responsibility for reporting and paying the use tax lies with the buyer or the consumer.
  - If the seller does not collect the sales tax at the time of the transaction, the buyer is required to self-assess and pay the use tax to their state's tax authority.
  - Use tax is applicable to purchases made from out-of-state sellers, online retailers without a physical presence in the buyer's state, or when purchases are made in a state with a lower sales tax rate and brought back to the buyer's home state.
  - The use tax rate is typically the same as the sales tax rate in the buyer's location.
  - The purpose of the use tax is to ensure that consumers and businesses pay the appropriate amount of tax on purchases, even if the seller is not required to collect the sales tax.

In summary, the key distinction between sales tax and use tax is based on who collects and remits the tax. Sales tax is collected and remitted by the seller at the time of the transaction, while use tax is reported and paid by the buyer or consumer when sales tax is not collected by the seller. Both taxes aim to generate revenue for states and local jurisdictions and ensure the fair taxation of goods and services. It's essential for businesses and consumers to understand and comply with the relevant sales and use tax regulations in their respective states to avoid potential tax liabilities.
