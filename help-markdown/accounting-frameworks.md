***

# What is an accounting framework?

An accounting framework refers to a set of rules, principles, and guidelines that govern the preparation and presentation of financial statements. Accounting frameworks provide a standardized framework for financial reporting, ensuring consistency and comparability in financial statements across different companies and jurisdictions. Examples of accounting frameworks include Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS).

# Is IFRS for SMEs also an accounting framework?

The IFRS for SMEs (International Financial Reporting Standards for Small and Medium-sized Entities) is considered an accounting framework. It is a simplified set of accounting standards specifically designed for small and medium-sized entities (SMEs) that do not have public accountability.

IFRS for SMEs provides a comprehensive set of accounting principles, rules, and guidelines that govern the preparation and presentation of financial statements for SMEs. It covers topics such as recognition, measurement, presentation, and disclosure of various financial elements including assets, liabilities, equity, income, expenses, and cash flows. The aim of IFRS for SMEs is to provide a less complex and more cost-effective financial reporting framework for SMEs compared to the full International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) framework.

The adoption of IFRS for SMEs enables SMEs to prepare their financial statements in a consistent and transparent manner, facilitating comparability and understanding of their financial performance and position. It helps SMEs meet the needs of various stakeholders, such as investors, lenders, and regulatory authorities, by providing relevant and reliable financial information.

It's important to note that the application of IFRS for SMEs is subject to local regulations and requirements in each jurisdiction. Some jurisdictions may have made modifications or amendments to the standard to suit their specific legal and regulatory frameworks.
